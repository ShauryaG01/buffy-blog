Searchanise vs Fast Simon: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Searchanise and Fast Simon head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

If you're comparing Searchanise vs Fast Simon in 2026, the short answer is: Searchanise is the cheaper, faster-to-deploy pick for small and growing catalogs, while Fast Simon is built for mid-market stores that want deeper AI merchandising and personalization and can absorb a session-based pricing model. This page is for Shopify founders and growth marketers - whether you're running a 200-SKU store out of a spare room or scaling a professional D2C operation in India - who need on-site search that doesn't tank conversion, and who want to know which tool actually fits their catalog size and budget before they commit to a monthly bill.

Searchanise starts at $19/mo (after a free tier) and scales by product count across seven tiers up to $349/mo, which makes cost predictable as your catalog grows but means a big SKU jump can bump you a tier overnight. Fast Simon starts at $40/mo and scales by search sessions, topping out at $500/mo before custom Enterprise pricing - it leans harder into AI-driven merchandising, upsell, and cross-sell, which shows up in average order value more than in raw search speed.

Both are legitimate, widely used Shopify search apps. Neither one, on its own, changes how your products show up in ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overviews - that's a separate, off-site problem. If on-site search is your only concern right now, this comparison will get you to a decision.

Best For

Pick Searchanise if you're under a few hundred SKUs and want instant search, filtering, and basic AI personalization without a big monthly commitment - the $19/mo tier covers most small catalogs comfortably.

Pick Fast Simon if you're mid-market, already driving solid traffic, and care more about AI-driven merchandising (dynamic collections, upsell, cross-sell) than about running the cheapest search box - the step up to Essential at $100/mo is where its merchandising tools really open up.

Pick Searchanise if your catalog is large but your traffic is lumpy - seasonal drops, flash sales, influencer spikes - since it prices by product count rather than sessions, so a traffic surge won't suddenly change your bill.

Pick Fast Simon if conversion lift from merchandising matters more to you than search-app cost on its own - brands that lean on upsell and cross-sell logic to grow average order value tend to get more out of Fast Simon's approach than Searchanise's.

Pros And Cons

Searchanise
Pros: Cheap entry point ($19/mo after a free tier), unlimited filters, genuinely instant search, AI personalization included even on lower tiers, and a 7-day trial so you can test it on a live catalog before paying.
Cons: Pricing is tied directly to SKU count, so catalog growth increases cost even if your traffic and revenue haven't grown yet; merchandising and upsell tooling is lighter than Fast Simon's.

Fast Simon
Pros: Deeper AI merchandising, personalization, and upsell/cross-sell tooling built to lift AOV, not just search speed; a free tier to start with no commitment.
Cons: Entry price is more than double Searchanise's ($40/mo vs. $19/mo), and the jump from Essential ($100/mo) to Growth ($500/mo) is steep for a mid-market budget; because pricing is session-based, an unplanned traffic spike can move you into a pricier bracket faster than a product-count model would.

Recommendation

If you're optimizing for cost and simplicity, Searchanise is the safer default - it's cheaper at every comparable tier, prices by something you control (catalog size) rather than something you don't (traffic volume), and its AI personalization is solid enough for most small-to-mid stores. Fast Simon earns its higher price when merchandising and AOV lift are the actual goal - its upsell and cross-sell tooling goes deeper, and for a mid-market store already spending on paid traffic, paying more for search that also sells can be worth it.

Don't choose on brand recognition alone: run the free tier or trial on both against your real catalog and real traffic pattern before committing, since the pricing models diverge enough (product count vs. sessions) that your actual bill will look different from the sticker price. And keep in mind that both tools solve on-site search and merchandising only - neither changes how your products are represented in AI assistants or AI-generated search results, so don't expect either purchase to move that needle.

Faq

How much does Searchanise cost compared to Fast Simon?

Searchanise starts at $19/mo (after a free tier) and scales through $39/mo, $89/mo, $139/mo, $209/mo, up to $349/mo at the top tier, priced by product count. Fast Simon starts at $40/mo (Starter), then $100/mo (Essential), $500/mo (Growth), with custom Enterprise pricing above that, priced by search sessions. Searchanise is cheaper at every comparable step, but check which pricing driver - SKUs or sessions - actually matches how your store grows.

Is Fast Simon worth the extra cost over Searchanise?

Depends what you're buying. For plain fast, accurate search and filtering, Fast Simon's premium over Searchanise ($40/mo vs. $19/mo at entry, and steeper from there) is hard to justify. If you specifically want AI merchandising, upsell, and cross-sell tooling to lift average order value, the extra spend can pay for itself - but measure that against AOV and conversion lift, not against search speed alone.

Does Searchanise have a free plan?

Yes - Searchanise offers a free tier plus a 7-day trial on paid tiers, so you can test instant search and filtering on a live store before paying anything. Fast Simon also offers a free plan, so both are low-risk to trial before committing to a paid tier.

Which is better for a large or fast-growing Shopify catalog?

Because Searchanise prices by product count, a fast-growing catalog will move you through its tiers ($19 → $39 → $89 → $139 → $209 → $349/mo) as SKUs increase. Fast Simon prices by session volume instead, so a large catalog with modest traffic may end up cheaper relative to size, while a smaller catalog with a traffic spike could cost more. Model your actual SKU count and traffic pattern before assuming either is cheaper at scale.

Do Searchanise or Fast Simon help with AI search visibility outside my own store, like ChatGPT or Google AI Overviews?

No - both are on-site search and merchandising tools; they improve how customers find products inside your Shopify store, not how your products are represented in AI assistants or AI-generated search results. That's a separate discipline entirely, which is where a tool like Buffy is built to complement - not replace - either of these, for DTC brands that want on-site AI search and off-site AI visibility handled together.
