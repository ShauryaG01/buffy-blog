Klevu: Review, Pricing, and Alternatives for Shopify

Klevu's enterprise AI search reviewed - pricing reality, best-fit store size, and alternatives.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

Klevu is enterprise-grade AI search and merchandising built for larger Shopify and Shopify Plus catalogs - not a self-serve, credit-card-checkout tool. Since merging with Athos Commerce, Klevu has gone quote-only: both its plans, Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery, require a sales call, and there's no published entry price. That's the right model for mid-market and enterprise merchants who need semantic search, self-learning ranking, and merchandising controls tuned to a large SKU count - but it's a mismatch for early-stage DTC brands or lean growth teams who want to see a price before booking a demo. This page is for professional growth teams - from the US to India - sizing up Klevu against Algolia, Searchspring, Boost AI Search & Discovery, Shopify's native search, and Buffy, so you can match the tool to your store's actual size and budget instead of your sales call.

Best For

Pick Klevu if you're running a Shopify Plus store with a large, complex catalog and need enterprise-grade semantic search, self-learning ranking, and merchandising - and you have the budget and patience for a sales-led, quote-based buying process.

Pick Algolia if you have in-house engineering resources and want maximum control over search relevance and UI - it's an API-first tool built for developers, not a plug-and-play app.

Pick Searchspring or Boost AI Search & Discovery if you're a mid-size Shopify merchant who wants strong AI search and merchandising without Klevu's enterprise sales process. Boost tends to be faster and cheaper to set up; Searchspring leans toward deeper merchandising controls.

Pick Buffy if your priority isn't just on-site search but also making sure your products surface when shoppers ask AI assistants and chat-based search for recommendations - Buffy pairs on-site AI search with off-site AI-visibility, which none of the pure search vendors above address.

Stick with Shopify's native Search & Discovery if you're pre-revenue or very early-stage and don't yet have the catalog size or budget to justify a paid search tool.

Pros And Cons

Klevu pros:
- AI semantic search understands shopper intent and synonyms, not just literal keyword matches
- Self-learning ranking improves relevance over time based on real shopper behavior
- Merchandising controls built for large, complex catalogs
- Backed by the broader Athos Commerce enterprise stack, giving it staying power and roadmap depth

Klevu cons:
- No public pricing anywhere - every deal requires a sales call and negotiation
- Enterprise-oriented positioning usually means longer implementation and onboarding than a self-serve app
- Likely overkill, and possibly overpriced, for stores below enterprise catalog size
- Harder to comparison-shop since you can't see cost upfront without engaging sales

Recommendation

If you're a mid-market or enterprise Shopify Plus merchant with a large catalog and a dedicated budget for search and merchandising, Klevu is a legitimate enterprise-grade pick - its AI semantic search and self-learning ranking are built for scale, and the Athos Commerce backing gives it staying power. But if you're a growing DTC brand without an enterprise budget, the quote-only pricing is itself a signal: you'll likely pay more, and wait longer, than you would with Boost, Searchspring, or even Algolia for comparable core search. Get a Klevu quote alongside at least one alternative before committing, and if AI visibility beyond your own storefront matters to your growth strategy, evaluate Buffy too - it's solving an adjacent problem that Klevu doesn't touch.

Faq

How much does Klevu cost?

Klevu doesn't publish pricing. It offers two plans, Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery, both quote-based since its merger with Athos Commerce. You'll need to book a sales call and share catalog size and traffic details to get a number - expect enterprise-level pricing rather than a flat self-serve monthly fee, so budget accordingly before you start the conversation.

Is Klevu worth it for a small Shopify store?

Probably not. Klevu is built and priced for mid-market and enterprise catalogs. If you're running a smaller catalog or don't have a dedicated search and merchandising budget, tools like Boost AI Search & Discovery or Shopify's native Search & Discovery will get you meaningfully better relevance without an enterprise sales process.

What's the difference between Klevu's Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery plans?

Both are custom-quoted, so exact feature scope isn't public. Based on Klevu's own positioning, Onsite Discovery covers core AI search and merchandising on your storefront, while Complete Discovery is the broader package pulling in additional discovery and merchandising capabilities from the Athos Commerce stack. Confirm the precise line items on your sales call.

What are the best Klevu alternatives for Shopify?

For self-serve or mid-size options, look at Algolia (developer-first, highly customizable), Searchspring (merchandising-heavy), and Boost AI Search & Discovery (Shopify App Store, faster setup, lighter cost). If your goal extends beyond on-site search to how your products surface in AI assistants and chat-based search, Buffy is worth evaluating alongside them.

Does Klevu work with standard Shopify, or only Shopify Plus?

Klevu is positioned for mid-market and enterprise merchants, which in the Shopify ecosystem usually skews toward Shopify Plus, but the underlying technology isn't exclusive to Plus. The real gating factor is catalog size and budget rather than your specific Shopify tier - confirm compatibility with your setup during the sales process.
