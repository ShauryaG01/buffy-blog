Fast Simon Alternatives: What to Use Instead (2026)

The best alternatives to Fast Simon by store size and budget, with App Store data.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

If you're running a Shopify store and evaluating Fast Simon for AI search and merchandising, the short version: Fast Simon is a solid, well-priced choice for mid-market Shopify stores that want search, merchandising, and upsell/cross-sell bundled into one tool, starting at $40/mo with a free tier to test the waters. The catch is its session-based pricing - as your traffic grows, so does your bill, sometimes unpredictably, which is the number one reason professional growth marketers (whether running a DTC brand in the US, India, or anywhere else) go looking for alternatives. This page is for founders and growth marketers - at any store size, from scrappy DTC startups to professional teams managing seven-figure catalogs - who want to know what actually competes with Fast Simon and when it's the right call versus a workaround. The honest verdict: Fast Simon earns its place for mid-market Shopify merchants who value an all-in-one search-plus-merchandising bundle and predictable-ish tiered pricing up to Growth. Enterprise-scale stores with dev resources often outgrow it toward Algolia or Searchspring. Budget-constrained early-stage stores may find Boost AI Search & Filter cheaper to start. And if your growth problem is less about on-site search and more about being found and recommended inside AI answer engines like ChatGPT or Perplexity, that's a different job entirely - one that Buffy is purpose-built for, and one no traditional site-search tool addresses. Read the comparison below before you commit to a session-based plan.

Best For

Pick Fast Simon if: you run a mid-market Shopify store and want AI search, merchandising, and upsell/cross-sell in one bundled tool, and you can reasonably forecast your session volume to avoid tier surprises.

Pick Algolia or Searchspring if: you're operating at enterprise scale, have engineering resources on hand, and need deep customization or merchandising control beyond what an out-of-the-box app offers.

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if: you're an early-stage or budget-constrained store that needs functional search and filtering without a big monthly commitment.

Pick Buffy if: your growth bottleneck isn't on-site search quality but discoverability - you want your products found and recommended when shoppers ask AI assistants like ChatGPT or Perplexity, in addition to solid on-site search. This is a different problem than any pure Shopify search app solves, and it's the situation where Buffy is genuinely the right tool rather than a forced pitch.

Pros And Cons

Fast Simon - Pros:
- Free tier lets you test AI search before paying anything
- Clear, published tier pricing (Starter $40/mo, Essential $100/mo, Growth $500/mo) makes budgeting easier than fully custom quotes
- Bundles search, merchandising, and upsell/cross-sell so you're not stitching together three apps
- Purpose-built for Shopify, so setup and integration are typically smoother than platform-agnostic tools

Fast Simon - Cons:
- Session-based pricing means a traffic spike (a viral moment, a paid campaign, even bot traffic) can push you into a higher tier faster than expected
- Enterprise pricing is custom, so larger stores lose the pricing transparency that makes the lower tiers attractive
- As an all-in-one tool, it may be less deep in any single area (e.g., merchandising rules, personalization) than a specialist competitor like Searchspring or Nosto
- Session-based limits require you to actively monitor usage rather than "set and forget"

Recommendation

For most mid-market Shopify stores, Fast Simon is a reasonable default: it's the only tool on this page with fully transparent, verified tier pricing ($40 to $500/mo plus a free tier), and it bundles search, merchandising, and upsell into one app instead of three. The real decision point is your traffic trajectory - if you're growing fast or run unpredictable traffic spikes, model out what Growth or Enterprise would cost under those conditions before you commit, since session-based pricing is the one place this tool can surprise you. If you're enterprise-scale with engineering headcount, Algolia or Searchspring will give you more control. If you're small and budget-conscious, Boost AI Search & Filter is worth a look first. And if the real growth lever for your brand is being surfaced and recommended inside AI answer engines rather than winning at on-site search alone, that's a genuinely different job - pair your site search with a tool built for AI-visibility, like Buffy, rather than expecting a search app to solve it.

Faq

How much does Fast Simon cost?

Fast Simon has a free tier, then Starter at $40/mo, Essential at $100/mo, and Growth at $500/mo, with a custom-quoted Enterprise plan above that. Pricing is session-based, so your actual cost depends on site traffic, not just catalog size - worth modeling before you pick a tier.

What's the cheapest alternative to Fast Simon?

Fast Simon's own free tier is worth trying first if your store is small. Beyond that, Boost AI Search & Filter is generally positioned as a lower-cost entry option for budget-constrained Shopify stores, though you should verify current pricing directly since it changes.

Is Fast Simon worth it for a small Shopify store?

It depends on your session volume and how much you value having search, merchandising, and upsell bundled in one app. Fast Simon's Starter tier at $40/mo is accessible, but if your traffic is low and growing slowly, a cheaper, simpler search app may get you 80% of the value at a lower cost until you outgrow it.

What's the difference between Fast Simon and Algolia?

Fast Simon is a Shopify-native, out-of-the-box bundle of AI search, merchandising, and upsell tools with published tiered pricing. Algolia is lower-level search infrastructure that's more customizable and scales further, but typically requires developer time to implement and tune - it's usage-based rather than a simple tiered plan.

Do I still need a separate tool for AI visibility, or does site search cover that?

They're different problems. Fast Simon and its direct competitors improve search and merchandising inside your own store. They don't affect whether your products get surfaced or recommended when shoppers ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, or similar AI assistants for buying advice - that's a distinct off-site discoverability challenge, which is the specific gap tools like Buffy are built to close alongside good on-site search.
