Buffy Intel vs Profound: Which Fits a DTC Brand?

An honest comparison - where Profound's enterprise depth wins and where Buffy Intel's daily DTC-focused monitoring fits better.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 5 min read

Overview

This page is for DTC founders and growth marketers trying to decide where to put budget as AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) start influencing purchase decisions. Buffy Intel vs Profound: which fits a DTC brand comes down to scope. Profound is an enterprise answer-engine optimization platform - deep multi-engine tracking, conversation analytics, Profound Agents, SOC2/SSO - built and priced for enterprise and larger mid-market marketing teams. Buffy Intel is the AI-visibility half of Buffy, a commerce engine built for DTC and Shopify brands, and it's bundled with on-site AI search, chat, and product recommendations so you can act on what the monitoring finds. Short version: if you're a lean DTC team that wants to know whether AI engines mention your products and then convert that traffic on-site, Buffy is the more complete, less expensive starting point ($49/mo, free tier available). If you're running AEO across an enterprise or multi-brand org and need compliance-grade reporting and true multi-engine depth, Profound's Growth or Enterprise tier is the better-built tool - you're paying for depth Buffy doesn't try to compete on.

Best For

Pick Buffy if you're a DTC or Shopify brand that wants AI-visibility monitoring and on-site AI search/chat/recommendations in one tool, and you want to start free before committing spend.

Pick Profound if you're an enterprise or large mid-market brand that needs multi-engine (not just ChatGPT) tracking, conversation-level analytics, and SOC2/SSO to clear a procurement review.

Pick Profound's Growth tier ($399/mo) or Enterprise plan if you have a dedicated AEO/SEO function and need Profound Agents plus full multi-engine coverage.

Pick Buffy's free tier if you simply want to test whether your brand shows up in ChatGPT or Gemini answers before deciding whether AI-visibility tracking is worth paying for at all.

Pros And Cons

Buffy (Intel + Search)
Pros: free tier with no credit card required, so you can validate AI-visibility monitoring before paying; bundles monitoring with on-site AI search, chat, and product recommendations, so being found and converting live in one tool; 5-minute Shopify install; entry price ($49/mo) undercuts Profound's $99/mo entry tier.
Cons: newer entrant with a shorter track record than Profound; Shopify-first, so weaker fit if you're on a different platform; not built for large multi-brand enterprise reporting.

Profound
Pros: multi-engine tracking beyond ChatGPT at higher tiers; Profound Agents and conversation-level analytics for deeper AEO insight; SOC2/SSO support for enterprise compliance needs; established enterprise AEO track record.
Cons: the $99/mo Starter plan tracks ChatGPT only - multi-engine coverage requires the $399/mo Growth tier or Enterprise; no on-site search, chat, or recommendation tooling, so visibility insight doesn't come with a way to act on it; enterprise custom pricing can be a stretch for small DTC budgets; no free tier to test before buying.

Recommendation

For most DTC brands running lean marketing teams, Buffy is the more defensible starting point: monitor AI visibility across ChatGPT and Gemini and act on it through on-site AI search and product recommendations, in one tool, starting free and scaling to $49–$199/mo. Once you're an enterprise or multi-brand organization with a dedicated AEO function, a compliance checklist (SOC2/SSO), and a need for multi-engine tracking plus conversation-level analytics, Profound's $399/mo Growth tier or custom Enterprise plan is the better-built tool for that job. The honest split: Buffy wins on cost, speed to value, and pairing visibility with conversion for DTC teams; Profound wins on analytical depth and enterprise readiness once you've outgrown a single-brand DTC use case.

Faq

Is Buffy Intel cheaper than Profound?

Yes. Buffy starts free with no credit card required, and its paid entry tier is $49/mo, versus Profound's $99/mo Starter plan. Profound's pricing also scales further - $399/mo for Growth and custom Enterprise pricing for full multi-engine coverage - so Buffy's entry price undercuts Profound at every published tier.

Does Profound's cheaper plan track every AI engine, or just ChatGPT?

Profound's $99/mo Starter plan tracks ChatGPT only. You need the $399/mo Growth plan or the custom Enterprise tier to get multi-engine tracking, conversation analytics, and Profound Agents. If multi-engine visibility matters to you from day one, budget for Growth rather than Starter.

Is Buffy Intel worth it for a small DTC brand?

If you're a DTC or Shopify brand that wants to know whether ChatGPT or Gemini are mentioning your products - and then actually capture that traffic - it's worth testing: the free tier costs nothing to try, and the $49/mo Starter tier pairs that monitoring with on-site AI search and product recommendations. If all you want is passive visibility tracking with no on-site component, that's a narrower need Buffy is designed to complement rather than replace.

Can a DTC brand use both Profound and Buffy?

A larger DTC brand could run Profound for deep, multi-engine AEO reporting while using Buffy for the on-site AI search, chat, and recommendation layer Profound doesn't offer - the tools cover different halves of the problem, being found versus converting once found. For most single-brand DTC teams starting out, though, one tool is enough.

What's the main difference between Buffy Intel and Profound?

Profound is a visibility-analytics platform built for enterprise AEO teams - multi-engine tracking, conversation analytics, SOC2/SSO - priced accordingly at $99–$399+/mo plus custom Enterprise. Buffy Intel is the AI-visibility half of a broader DTC commerce tool that also includes on-site AI search and product recommendations, priced for smaller teams at $49–$199/mo with a free tier. The real difference isn't just price - it's whether you want monitoring alone or monitoring plus the on-site tooling to act on it.
