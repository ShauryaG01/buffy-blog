Klevu vs Algolia: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Klevu and Algolia head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 5 min read

Overview

"Klevu vs Algolia" is the search comparison you run when Shopify's native search can't handle a catalog with real depth, synonyms, or merchandising rules. Both are AI-powered, both plug into Shopify and Shopify Plus, but they solve different problems for different teams. Klevu is a purpose-built ecommerce search and merchandising platform (now under Athos Commerce) that ships pre-tuned semantic search, self-learning ranking, and category merchandising with minimal engineering lift. Algolia is a general-purpose, developer-first search API that happens to power a lot of storefronts. It delivers a fast, flexible search experience, but you (or your dev team) build the ecommerce logic on top of it. Short version: if you want AI search a merchandiser can run without pulling in a developer, Klevu is the more direct fit. If you have engineers who want full control over ranking, facets, and a headless storefront, Algolia is the stronger foundation, at the cost of more setup and less predictable usage-based bills. Whether you're a solo Shopify Plus brand or a professional growth team in India managing a multi-region catalog, the decision usually comes down to how much engineering time you want to spend on search.

Best For

Pick Klevu if you run a mid-market or enterprise Shopify Plus catalog and want a merchandising team, not engineers, to own search tuning, synonyms, and category pages day to day.

Pick Algolia if you have in-house developers, a headless or custom storefront, and want to build bespoke ranking, filtering, or a unified search experience across web, app, and commerce.

Pick Algolia's free tier if you're pre-scale and want to test API-based search before committing budget, then watch usage-based pricing closely as traffic grows.

Pick Buffy if your priority is DTC-specific AI search paired with off-site AI-visibility (showing up correctly in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI shopping answers), not just on-site search, without needing a dedicated dev team to run it.

Pros And Cons

Klevu pros: purpose-built for ecommerce merchandising, self-learning AI ranking that improves with use, strong synonym and semantic handling out of the box, and less engineering lift to launch.

Klevu cons: no public pricing, so every deal goes through sales; quote-based buying can slow procurement for smaller teams; positioned toward mid-market and enterprise, which can feel like overkill for a small catalog.

Algolia pros: fast, flexible API; strong developer experience and documentation; a free tier to start; AI-powered ranking available on Grow Plus; useful well beyond ecommerce for any app search.

Algolia cons: not ecommerce-specific out of the box, so merchandising logic has to be built by your team; requires developer time to implement and maintain; usage-based pricing can become unpredictable as traffic or catalog size grows.

Recommendation

Neither tool is a wrong choice, the right pick depends on who owns search day to day. If a merchandiser or growth marketer will be tuning results, ranking rules, and category pages without pulling in engineering, Klevu's ecommerce-native tooling gets you there faster, even with quote-based pricing. If you have developers who want a headless or highly custom implementation and are comfortable owning ranking logic, Algolia gives you more flexibility and a free tier to start. For most single-storefront Shopify or Shopify Plus brands without a dedicated search engineer, Klevu is the more direct fit. For DTC brands that also care about being found and cited correctly in AI answer engines, not just on-site search, that's a separate problem Buffy is built for, and it's worth evaluating alongside whichever search tool you pick on-site.

Faq

Is Klevu or Algolia better for Shopify?

Klevu tends to be the better fit for standard Shopify and Shopify Plus stores because it's built specifically for ecommerce merchandising out of the box. Algolia is a better fit if you're on a headless or custom Shopify setup and have developers who want full control over the search experience.

How much does Klevu cost compared to Algolia?

Neither publishes standard pricing today. Klevu sells Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery on a custom quote basis since its merger into Athos Commerce, so there's no public price list. Algolia offers a free tier to start, then moves to custom usage-based plans (Grow, Grow Plus, Premium/Elevate) where cost scales with search volume and traffic. Get quotes from both and model your expected traffic before comparing totals.

Is Algolia worth it for a small Shopify store?

It can be, if you have developer resources and want to invest in a custom search experience early. But for most small stores without an engineering team, the setup and maintenance cost usually outweighs the benefit versus an ecommerce-native tool like Klevu, or Shopify's built-in search paired with a merchandising app.

Do Klevu and Algolia both use AI for search ranking?

Yes, both offer AI-driven ranking. Klevu's ranking is self-learning and tuned specifically for ecommerce signals like clicks and conversions. Algolia offers AI-powered ranking on its Grow Plus plan, layered on top of its core fast-search API.

What's the difference between Klevu or Algolia and an AI-visibility tool like Buffy?

Klevu and Algolia both improve search on your own site, helping shoppers already on your store find products faster. Buffy addresses a separate problem: off-site AI visibility, making sure your products show up correctly when shoppers ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, or other AI tools for recommendations. Many DTC brands end up needing both, on-site search and off-site AI visibility.
