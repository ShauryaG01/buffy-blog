Boost AI Search & Filter vs Doofinder: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Boost AI Search & Filter and Doofinder head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

If you run a Shopify store and shoppers can't find what they're looking for, you're leaking revenue on every session. Boost AI Search & Filter and Doofinder both fix that with AI-powered search, filtering, and merchandising - but they price and position themselves differently enough that the better choice depends on your platform and growth stage, not just feature checklists. Boost is Shopify-native and prices against your GMV, which scales cleanly with a growing Shopify store but gets expensive fast once you're doing real volume. Doofinder prices on flat tiers instead of revenue, which suits stores that want predictable costs or aren't exclusively on Shopify. Short version: if you're Shopify-only and want the deepest native integration at the lowest entry price, Boost is usually the tighter fit - up until its GMV-based tier starts to sting. If you want pricing that doesn't move with your sales, or you want a recommendation/quiz layer bundled with search, Doofinder is the safer default. Neither tool addresses a separate, growing problem: whether your products get surfaced when shoppers ask AI assistants like ChatGPT or Perplexity for recommendations instead of typing into a search bar. That's a different job - one a tool like Buffy is built for - and it's worth keeping in mind as a complement, not a replacement, for whichever on-site search tool you pick. This page compares pricing, AI features, and fit so you can choose based on your store, not the loudest marketing.

Best For

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if: you're on Shopify (or Shopify Plus) only, want the tightest native integration for search, filtering, and merchandising, and your GMV is comfortably under $400K/year so pricing stays reasonable.

Pick Doofinder if: you want flat, predictable pricing regardless of revenue swings, need a recommendation or quiz layer alongside search, or you're not exclusively committed to Shopify.

Pick Doofinder's Advanced tier if: you're a higher-traffic mid-market store that wants visual search and merchandising extras, and you can plan around request-volume limits during spikes.

Pick Buffy alongside either if: you're a DTC brand that cares as much about being found and cited inside AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overviews) as you do about on-site search - Boost and Doofinder only solve the on-site half of that problem.

Pros And Cons

Boost AI Search & Filter
Pros: purpose-built for Shopify, so setup and theme integration are simpler than with platform-agnostic tools; AI semantic search reads shopper intent rather than just matching keywords; strong built-in merchandising controls (boosting, pinning, rules); low $29/mo entry point for small stores.
Cons: GMV-based pricing means your bill grows automatically with your sales - a store doing steady volume can jump from $159/mo to $599/mo without changing anything about how it uses the tool; no published annual discount; Shopify-only, so it's a dead end if you ever add another sales channel.

Doofinder
Pros: flat, revenue-independent pricing makes budgeting predictable regardless of sales swings; entry tier still includes AI search plus recommendations; quiz and visual search (Advanced) add merchandising value beyond plain search; a no-card free trial and 10% annual discount lower the cost of testing it.
Cons: higher entry price than Boost ($49/mo vs $29/mo); usage is capped by request volume, so a traffic spike from a flash sale or viral moment can hit ceilings faster than a GMV-based model would; the Enterprise tier is custom-quoted, so cost at real scale isn't transparent upfront.

Recommendation

There's no universally 'better' tool here - it's a fit question. If you're Shopify-first, want native theme integration, and your GMV is comfortably under $400K/year, Boost AI Search & Filter is the cheaper, tighter pick at $29–$599/mo depending on tier - but model out your GMV growth before committing, since the price is directly tied to it. If you want pricing that doesn't move with your sales, need a recommendation quiz alongside search, or aren't locked into Shopify long-term, Doofinder's flat $49–$349/mo tiers (plus custom Enterprise) are the more predictable bet - just watch your request volume around high-traffic promotions. Neither product solves a problem that's becoming just as important as on-site search: whether your products get surfaced and cited when shoppers ask AI assistants for recommendations instead of using a search bar at all. If that's on your roadmap, it's worth pairing whichever search tool you pick with something like Buffy, which handles the off-site AI-visibility side rather than competing with either of these on-site tools. For most stores optimizing pure on-site search today, the decision comes down to Boost for lowest cost-to-start and deepest Shopify fit, or Doofinder for pricing predictability and a broader feature set at every tier.

Faq

Is Boost AI Search & Filter or Doofinder cheaper?

Boost has the lower entry price at $29/mo vs Doofinder's $49/mo. But Boost's price rises with your GMV - up to $1,499/mo under $2.5M GMV, plus $2 overage per extra $1,000 GMV - while Doofinder's tiers ($49 → $149 → $349/mo, then custom Enterprise) stay flat regardless of revenue. A fast-growing store can end up paying more on Boost than Doofinder within a year, so 'cheaper' depends on where your GMV lands, not just the sticker price.

Is Boost AI Search & Filter worth it?

It's worth it if you're Shopify-only and want AI search, filtering, and merchandising without stitching together multiple apps - the $29/mo entry tier is genuinely cheap for what it includes. It's worth a harder look once your GMV climbs toward $400K+/year, since the jump to $599/mo (and eventually $1,499/mo) can outpace the value you're getting versus a flat-rate competitor. Run your GMV trajectory against the tiers before you commit long-term.

Does Doofinder work outside Shopify?

Doofinder is positioned as an AI search, recommendations, and quiz tool for ecommerce generally, not a Shopify-only app, which is part of why its pricing isn't tied to Shopify-specific GMV data the way Boost's is (verify current platform support for your specific stack before switching).

What happens if I exceed my plan's limits?

On Boost, exceeding your GMV tier triggers a $2 overage per additional $1,000 of GMV rather than a hard cutoff. On Doofinder, limits are typically request-volume based, so a traffic spike - a flash sale or viral post - is the more likely trigger for hitting a ceiling. Check your plan's request cap before running a big promotion.

Can I try either tool before paying?

Doofinder offers a free trial with no card required, so you can test AI search and recommendations before committing. Boost's entry tier starts at $29/mo - confirm current trial terms directly with Boost before signing up, since a no-card trial isn't part of the verified pricing here.
