Klevu vs Searchanise: Which Shopify Search App Wins?

Enterprise AI vs plug-and-play - the two compared feature by feature.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

Short answer: if you're comparing Klevu vs Searchanise for Shopify search, the two aren't really fighting for the same buyer. Searchanise is the fast, self-serve pick - a plug-and-play search and filter app with a free tier and a $19/mo Tier 2 that most small stores land on. Klevu is the enterprise-grade alternative: AI-powered semantic search and merchandising, sold as a custom, quote-based deal since its merger into Athos Commerce, with no public pricing published.

For a solo founder or lean growth team running a catalog of a few hundred SKUs, that difference matters more than any feature list. Searchanise gets you live in an afternoon, scales its price with your product count (not your traffic), and comes with a 7-day trial to test before you commit. Klevu is built for teams - professional growth and merchandising teams, whether in the US, India, or elsewhere - who need self-learning ranking, semantic query understanding, and dedicated onboarding, and who have the budget and sales cycle patience for a custom quote.

The short verdict: pick Searchanise if you want affordable, working search live now. Pick Klevu if search and merchandising quality is a genuine growth lever for a larger catalog and you can absorb an enterprise sales process. Read on for the side-by-side.

Best For

Pick Searchanise if you're under a few thousand SKUs and want search live today without a sales call - the free plan and $19/mo Tier 2 cover most small catalogs comfortably.

Pick Klevu if you're running a large or fast-growing catalog where relevance and merchandising quality directly move revenue, and you have budget for a custom enterprise contract rather than a self-serve monthly fee.

Pick Searchanise if pricing predictability matters most - since tiers are set by product count, you can forecast your cost months in advance without a sales conversation.

Pick Klevu if you already have (or plan to hire) someone owning onsite search and merchandising - its self-learning ranking rewards ongoing tuning that a plug-and-play app isn't built to offer.

Pros And Cons

Klevu
Pros: AI semantic search that's built to understand intent, synonyms, and typos rather than relying on exact matches; self-learning ranking that adapts to shopper behavior over time; merchandising controls aimed at larger or multi-storefront catalogs.
Cons: No public pricing - you can't self-serve a quote, so budgeting requires a sales conversation; positioned squarely at mid-market and enterprise, which can be more than a small store needs; onboarding and implementation are more involved than a plug-and-play app.

Searchanise
Pros: Free plan plus a 7-day trial on paid tiers means you can validate it against your real catalog before paying; published, transparent pricing from $19 to $349/mo makes budgeting simple; fast install with unlimited filters and AI personalization included on paid tiers.
Cons: Priced by product count rather than traffic or usage, so a growing catalog can push you into a pricier tier even if search volume stays flat; less deep AI merchandising than enterprise platforms like Klevu; as your SKU count climbs, worth re-checking which tier you land on and what it includes (verify current pricing).

Recommendation

If you need a straight answer: Searchanise wins on accessibility and price transparency, Klevu wins on AI depth and merchandising power. There's no single winner because they're not solving the same problem.

Most stores under a few thousand SKUs should start with Searchanise - the free plan and 7-day trial let you validate it against your actual catalog before paying anything, and the $19–$349/mo range is disclosed upfront with no sales call required. If you outgrow it, you'll know because you're hitting filter or personalization limits, not because pricing surprised you.

Larger and Shopify Plus merchants - especially professional growth or merchandising teams in markets like the US and India managing complex, high-SKU catalogs - should put Klevu on the shortlist alongside other enterprise search vendors, but budget time for a quote-based sales process since there's no self-serve pricing page to check.

One more thing worth flagging: if what you actually need is AI-driven discovery on-site plus AI visibility off-site - how your products get surfaced by AI shopping assistants and answer engines, not just your own search bar - that's a different problem than either of these apps solves alone. It's the gap tools like Buffy are built for, worth a look if on-site search is only half of what you're trying to fix.

Bottom line: Searchanise for fast, affordable, self-serve search; Klevu for enterprise AI merchandising once your scale and budget justify it.

Faq

Is Searchanise cheaper than Klevu?

Yes, by a wide margin for most stores. Searchanise publishes transparent pricing - a free plan, then $19 up to $349/mo across 7 tiers based on product count. Klevu doesn't publish pricing at all; its Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery plans are custom quotes, so you'd need to talk to sales to know actual cost. Unless you're running an enterprise catalog where Klevu's AI depth pays for itself, Searchanise will almost always be the cheaper path.

Does Klevu have a free plan?

No. Klevu offers two plans, Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery, both custom-priced with no published free tier. Searchanise, by contrast, does have a free plan plus a 7-day trial on its paid tiers - if starting for free matters to you, that's a real difference between the two.

Is Searchanise worth it for a small Shopify store?

For most small stores, yes. The free plan and low entry tiers (starting at $19/mo) get you fast, unlimited-filter search with basic AI personalization, without an enterprise sales process. It's worth it while your catalog is modest and you want working search now. It's less clearly worth it once your product count grows large enough that you're paying for a higher tier mostly because of SKU count, not because you need more features - that's the point to reassess.

Which is better for a large or fast-growing catalog: Klevu or Searchanise?

Klevu is built for that scenario - its AI semantic search and self-learning ranking are designed to keep relevance high as catalog size and query complexity grow, and its merchandising tools target multi-storefront, high-SKU operations. Searchanise can technically handle a larger catalog too, but since its pricing scales with product count, cost keeps climbing even though the underlying feature depth doesn't necessarily grow to match enterprise-level needs.

Can I switch from Searchanise to Klevu later, or do I need to commit now?

You can switch later. Most Shopify search apps are swappable without re-platforming, though budget time to re-index your catalog and rebuild any custom merchandising or filter rules. A common path is starting on Searchanise while your catalog and budget are small, then evaluating Klevu (or another enterprise search vendor) once you've got the scale where a custom AI merchandising contract clearly pays for itself.
