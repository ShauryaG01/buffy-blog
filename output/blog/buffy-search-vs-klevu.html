Buffy Search vs Klevu: Conversational AI vs Enterprise Search

An honest comparison for Shopify merchants weighing conversational AI search against Klevu.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 6 min read

Overview

This page is for Shopify and DTC merchants comparing on-site search tools, specifically conversational AI search (Buffy Search) against Klevu's enterprise-grade AI search and merchandising suite. The short version: Buffy Search is built for DTC and growth teams that want a conversational, chat-like search experience live in about 5 minutes, plus visibility into how their products show up in ChatGPT and Gemini, starting at $49/mo with a free tier and no credit card required. Klevu is built for mid-market and enterprise catalogs that need deep merchandising rules, self-learning ranking, and a search team to configure it, at custom quote-based pricing since its merger with Athos Commerce. If you're a solo Shopify brand wanting to ship fast and cheap, Buffy fits. If you're running a large, complex catalog with a dedicated merchandising team and budget for an enterprise contract, Klevu is the safer long-term bet. Neither is a bad choice; they're built for different buyers, and the wrong fit shows up fast, either as an enterprise tool you're underusing or a lightweight tool that can't handle your SKU complexity.

Best For

Pick Buffy Search if you're a Shopify or DTC brand that wants conversational, chat-style search live fast, without a lengthy implementation, and you also care about how your products appear in AI answer engines like ChatGPT and Gemini. Pick Klevu if you run a large, complex catalog (think tens of thousands of SKUs across regions or brands) and need granular merchandising rules, a dedicated implementation team, and are comfortable with a custom quote instead of published pricing. Pick Klevu over Buffy specifically if your current pain point is merchandising control (boosting, burying, faceted rules at scale) rather than the shopper-facing search experience itself. Pick Buffy over Klevu if budget predictability matters: knowing you'll pay $49 to $199/mo (or start free) beats waiting on a sales quote, especially for teams still validating whether AI search moves their conversion numbers.

Pros And Cons

Buffy Search pros: transparent, low-commitment pricing ($49 to $199/mo, plus a genuinely free tier with no credit card); fast Shopify installation; conversational search feels native to how shoppers already talk to AI tools; bundles on-site search with off-site AI-visibility monitoring, which most search vendors don't offer at all. Buffy Search cons: newer entrant with a shorter track record than established enterprise players; Shopify-first, so less proven on other platforms; likely lighter merchandising tooling for very large or complex catalogs.

Klevu pros: mature, enterprise-grade semantic search with self-learning ranking that improves over time; strong merchandising toolkit for teams that need fine-grained control over what surfaces where; backed by Athos Commerce's broader enterprise commerce stack, which can matter for large organizations wanting one vendor relationship. Klevu cons: no public pricing, so every deal starts with a sales conversation and quote cycle; positioned and priced for mid-market/enterprise, which can be more implementation and cost than a smaller DTC brand needs; less focused on the emerging problem of AI-engine visibility outside the storefront.

Recommendation

If you're a DTC or Shopify-first brand trying to improve on-site conversion with a conversational search experience, and you want to see pricing and be live within days, Buffy Search is the more practical starting point, especially given its free tier and the added benefit of tracking your AI-engine visibility, a problem Klevu doesn't really address. If you're running an enterprise or mid-market catalog with real merchandising complexity (multiple brands, regions, or thousands of SKUs needing rule-based curation), Klevu's maturity and merchandising depth justify the quote-based sales process and higher likely spend. The honest failure mode to avoid: don't sign an enterprise search contract to solve a problem a $49 to $99/mo tool already handles, and don't expect a lighter conversational search tool to replace a dedicated merchandising team's rule engine at enterprise scale. Match the tool to your catalog complexity and team size, not to brand reputation alone.

Faq

How much does Klevu cost compared to Buffy?

Buffy has published pricing: a free tier (no credit card required), then Starter at $49/mo, Growth at $99/mo, and Scale at $199/mo. Klevu does not publish pricing; both its Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery plans are quote-based, a practice that became more pronounced since its merger with Athos Commerce. If budget predictability matters to you, that difference alone is often decisive.

Is Buffy Search worth it for a small Shopify store?

For a small or early-stage Shopify store, Buffy Search is generally worth trying because the free tier requires no credit card, so you can validate whether conversational search actually lifts conversion before spending anything. It's worth it if you want a fast install and care about AI-engine visibility alongside on-site search. It's less of a fit if your catalog is huge and you already know you need heavy merchandising rules, in which case evaluate Klevu instead.

What's the core difference between Buffy Search and Klevu?

Buffy Search is built around conversational, chat-like on-site search plus off-site monitoring of how your products appear in AI tools like ChatGPT and Gemini, aimed at DTC and Shopify brands. Klevu is an enterprise AI search and merchandising platform (part of Athos Commerce) built around semantic search and self-learning ranking, aimed at mid-market and enterprise retailers with more complex catalogs and dedicated merchandising needs.

Does Klevu work with Shopify?

Klevu is generally positioned as an enterprise search and merchandising platform that supports major ecommerce platforms including Shopify, though implementation depth and timeline will vary by plan and integration needs (verify current pricing and platform-specific setup details directly with Klevu). Buffy, by contrast, is built Shopify-first with a 5-minute install as a core part of its pitch.

Is enterprise search like Klevu overkill for a DTC brand?

Often, yes. If your catalog is a few hundred to a few thousand SKUs and your team doesn't have a dedicated merchandising function, Klevu's depth (and the quote-based sales cycle to get there) can be more infrastructure than you need. That's the gap tools like Buffy Search are built to fill: transparent pricing starting at $49/mo, a free tier to test first, and a faster path to a working conversational search experience without an enterprise implementation project.
