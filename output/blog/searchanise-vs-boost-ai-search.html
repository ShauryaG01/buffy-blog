Searchanise vs Boost AI Search & Filter: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Searchanise and Boost AI Search & Filter head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 6 min read

Overview

Searchanise and Boost AI Search & Filter both fix the same problem on Shopify: native search is weak, search users convert at a much higher rate than browsers, and both apps promise fast setup. Where they diverge is pricing logic. Searchanise charges by catalog size across 7 tiers, starting free and moving to $19/mo, $39/mo, $89/mo, $139/mo, $209/mo, and $349/mo as product count grows. Boost charges by store GMV across 4 tiers: $29/mo entry, $159/mo under $50K GMV, $599/mo under $400K GMV, and $1,499/mo under $2.5M GMV, plus $2 in overage per extra $1,000 of GMV once you cross a bracket. That single difference decides which tool fits which store. A lean catalog with modest traffic pays less, longer, on Searchanise, since the bill only moves when SKU count moves. A store with a smaller catalog but fast-growing revenue will outgrow Boost's brackets quickly, since the bill tracks sales, not products. Short version: Searchanise is the cheaper, more predictable pick for small to mid-size catalogs. Boost is built for stores that need heavier AI merchandising and semantic search as GMV climbs, and are prepared for the bill to climb with it. Neither tool addresses discovery that happens off your store, in AI shopping assistants and answer engines, which is a separate problem worth solving alongside whichever search app you choose.

Best For

Pick Searchanise if you run a lean catalog (roughly under a few thousand SKUs) and want search and filtering live fast without tying your bill to sales performance. The $19/mo tier, or the free plan for very small stores, covers most small operations comfortably, and the 7-day trial gives you room to test before committing.

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if your store is already doing meaningful volume and you need AI-driven merchandising and semantic search that can flex with a growing catalog and traffic. GMV-based pricing means you pay more as you earn more, which works fine if your margins support a $159 to $1,499/mo range, less fine if you're revenue-heavy but margin-thin.

Pick Buffy if search inside your store isn't the whole problem: you also want products surfacing in AI shopping assistants and answer engines before shoppers ever land on your site, which is outside what either Searchanise or Boost is built to do.

Pick neither yet if you're pre-launch or running a few hundred SKUs with light traffic. Your Shopify theme's native search plus a basic synonym fix will likely hold until you have real search volume worth optimizing.

Pros And Cons

Searchanise
Pros: Cheapest entry point in this comparison at $19/mo, with a genuine free plan and a 7-day trial to test first. Unlimited filters are available even on lower tiers, AI personalization is included, and pricing is predictable since it tracks catalog size, something you control directly.
Cons: As your product count grows you can jump tiers, and price, even if traffic and revenue haven't grown to match. Merchandising depth and semantic search sophistication generally trail tools built specifically around GMV-scale stores.

Boost AI Search & Filter
Pros: Strong AI semantic search and merchandising controls built for stores that need to actively curate discovery, not just index a catalog. Tier pricing is aligned to GMV brackets, so early-stage stores aren't paying scale-tier prices before they've reached scale.
Cons: The bill grows every time revenue does, with $2 overage per extra $1,000 GMV once a tier is exceeded. The jump between brackets ($159 to $599 to $1,499/mo) can land as a surprise at renewal if you crossed a GMV threshold, and billing is monthly only with no stated annual discount.

Recommendation

For most small to mid-size Shopify stores prioritizing cost control, Searchanise is the safer default: cheaper entry, a real free plan, and pricing tied to catalog size rather than something that moves with every strong sales month. Boost earns its higher price once you genuinely need AI merchandising and semantic search sophistication to match six or seven-figure GMV, and you've budgeted for a bill that scales alongside revenue rather than product count.

Either way, on-site search only solves half of discovery in 2026. Shoppers increasingly start research in AI assistants and answer engines before they land on any Shopify store, and that's outside what Searchanise or Boost touches. If that off-site AI visibility gap matters for your brand, that's where Buffy fits, working alongside whichever on-site search tool matches your catalog size and budget.

Faq

Which is cheaper, Searchanise or Boost AI Search & Filter?

Searchanise is cheaper to start: $19/mo (or free on its entry plan) versus Boost's $29/mo. But list price only tells part of the story. Searchanise's bill rises with product count across 7 tiers up to $349/mo, while Boost's bill rises with GMV across 4 tiers up to $1,499/mo plus overage. A small catalog with high sales volume will likely pay less on Searchanise; a small-catalog, high-revenue store could hit Boost's higher brackets sooner than expected.

Is Boost AI Search & Filter worth the GMV-based pricing?

It depends on your margins and how much you value active AI merchandising. If your store needs semantic search and curated discovery to match a growing catalog and you can absorb a bill that climbs with revenue (up to $1,499/mo at the top tier shown, plus $2 overage per extra $1,000 GMV), it's a reasonable trade. If you're margin-thin or your revenue is volatile, a flat, catalog-based pricing model like Searchanise's is easier to forecast.

Does Searchanise have a free plan?

Yes. Searchanise offers a free tier alongside a 7-day trial, making it accessible for very small stores before they need to move up to the $19/mo tier or higher as their catalog grows.

Which one has better AI features?

Both apps market AI capability. Searchanise includes AI personalization within its instant search and filtering. Boost leans more heavily into AI semantic search and merchandising controls aimed at stores that want to actively shape discovery, not just index products faster. Neither app's public materials support ranking one as definitively 'smarter'; the practical difference is that Boost's pricing and positioning target stores that need to lean on AI merchandising as they scale, while Searchanise treats AI as a feature layered onto a simpler, cheaper core.

Can I switch from Searchanise to Boost, or vice versa, later?

Generally yes. Both are installed as Shopify apps rather than deep platform rebuilds, so migrating means reconfiguring search indexing, filters, and merchandising rules in the new tool rather than a full replatform. Worth planning around a low-traffic window and testing the new setup before fully cutting over, since search and filter behavior directly affects conversion.
