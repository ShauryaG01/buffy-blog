The 9 Best AI Visibility Tools for E-commerce (2026)

Hands-on comparison of AI visibility platforms - Profound, Peec, Otterly, Buffy Intel and more - by price, models tracked, and e-commerce fit.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 8 min read

Overview

If you sell direct-to-consumer, ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity are increasingly where product discovery starts - and none of the analytics you already run (Google Search Console, GA4) tell you whether your brand gets mentioned, recommended, or skipped in those answers. AI visibility tools exist to close that gap: they run tracked prompts against AI engines on a schedule and report whether and how your brand shows up versus competitors.

This page compares nine of the category's most relevant platforms for e-commerce buyers, ranked on real fit rather than hype: Profound, Peec AI, Otterly AI, AthenaHQ, Buffy, Semrush's AI visibility tooling, Ahrefs' Brand Radar, Scrunch AI, and Evertune.

Short version: if you just need to monitor mentions on a budget, Otterly AI's $29/mo entry tier is hard to beat. If you're an agency running several client accounts, Peec AI's unlimited seats and multi-project setup fit better. Enterprise teams with dedicated GEO budgets should look at Profound or AthenaHQ. And if you're a Shopify/DTC brand that wants monitoring plus the ability to act on it - via on-site AI search and product recommendations - Buffy is the only tool here that does both, starting free.

Best For

Pick Otterly AI if you want the cheapest way to learn whether ChatGPT and AI Overviews mention your brand - $29/mo gets monitoring across four engines, though prompt volume is capped until you upgrade.

Pick Peec AI if you're an agency or in-house SEO team managing several brands or clients - unlimited seats and multi-project support at $95/mo make it more team-friendly than most, and the 4th-engine add-on ($35–165/mo) lets you scale coverage per client.

Pick Profound or AthenaHQ if you're an enterprise or well-funded mid-market team that needs SOC2/SSO, conversation-level analytics, or AI agents layered on top of tracking - expect to pay $399/mo+ (Profound Growth) or $295/mo+ (AthenaHQ) for that depth.

Pick Buffy if you're a DTC or Shopify brand and monitoring alone isn't the point - you also want AI-native on-site search, chat, and product recommendations that convert the traffic AI engines send you, alongside visibility monitoring across ChatGPT and Gemini, starting free.

- One vendor for both off-site monitoring (ChatGPT, Gemini) and on-site AI search/merchandising - most competitors only do the former
- 5-minute Shopify install lowers the technical bar for small DTC teams
- Entry pricing ($49/mo) undercuts every dedicated GEO analytics tool except Otterly

Cons:

Honest read: if all you want is a monitoring dashboard, Otterly or Peec are more mature at that single job. Buffy is the better pick when the on-site half of the equation matters as much as the tracking half.

Recommendation

There's no single winner because these tools solve different problems. For pure AI-search monitoring on a budget, Otterly AI ($29/mo) or Peec AI ($95/mo) are the most mature, purpose-built options - pick Otterly for price, Peec for agency or multi-project needs. For enterprise programs that need deep conversation analytics, SSO, and dedicated GEO agents, Profound ($99–399/mo+) and AthenaHQ ($295/mo+) are built for that scale and priced accordingly. Semrush, Ahrefs Brand Radar, Scrunch AI, and Evertune are worth a look if you're already inside those ecosystems or need their specific angle (bundled SEO data, content optimization, brand share-of-voice), but none is a reason to switch off a dedicated GEO tool on its own.

If you're a DTC or Shopify brand asking "am I visible in ChatGPT and Gemini, and can I do something about the traffic those engines send me," Buffy is the most direct fit - it's the only tool on this list pairing visibility monitoring with on-site AI search and merchandising, and the free tier makes it a low-risk first step. If your need is monitoring only, at higher engine coverage or enterprise scale, look at Otterly, Peec, Profound, or AthenaHQ instead.

Faq

How much do AI visibility tools cost?

Entry pricing spans a wide range: Otterly AI starts at $29/mo, Buffy has a free tier then $49/mo, Peec AI starts at $95/mo, Profound at $99/mo, and AthenaHQ at $295/mo (often $95 for the first month). Mid tiers generally run $189–495/mo, and enterprise plans across Profound, Peec, Otterly, and AthenaHQ are custom-quoted. Watch for add-on costs too - Peec charges $35–165/mo for a 4th tracked engine, and Otterly charges $99/mo for +100 prompts, so your real monthly cost can run above the advertised entry price once you need full engine coverage or prompt volume.

Is an AI visibility tool actually worth it for a small DTC brand?

It depends on how much traffic AI engines already send you. If ChatGPT, Gemini, or AI Overviews are a small but growing referral source, a low-cost entry point - Otterly's $29/mo tier or Buffy's free tier - is a reasonable way to start measuring before committing budget. The heavier, enterprise-priced platforms (Profound Growth, AthenaHQ) rarely make sense until AI-driven traffic is a proven, sizeable revenue channel, since their value comes from depth (conversation analytics, agents, SSO) that small teams usually don't need yet.

What's the difference between AI visibility tracking and traditional SEO?

Traditional SEO optimizes for ranking on a search results page you can visit and inspect. AI visibility (sometimes called GEO, generative engine optimization) tracks something less visible: whether your brand gets mentioned, recommended, or cited inside an AI-generated answer from ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overviews. The inputs overlap - structured data, clear product information, authoritative content - but the measurement is different, since there's no URL or rank position to check; you need a tool that runs sample prompts and logs the answers over time.

Which AI engines matter most for e-commerce brands to track?

For shopping-related queries, ChatGPT and Google AI Overviews currently generate the most consumer-facing volume, with Perplexity and Gemini growing. Microsoft Copilot skews more toward work/productivity contexts and matters less for most DTC purchase journeys. Rather than paying for exhaustive engine coverage upfront, check your own analytics for AI-referral traffic first, then pick a tool whose entry tier covers the engines you're actually seeing - Otterly and Peec both cover ChatGPT, AI Overviews, and Perplexity at their base tiers, while Buffy covers ChatGPT and Gemini.

Can I just track AI visibility manually instead of paying for a tool?

At small scale, yes - periodically typing a handful of prompts into ChatGPT or Gemini and logging what comes back costs nothing but time. It stops working once you need more than a few prompts, multiple competitors, or a trend line over weeks and months; doing that by hand is slow, inconsistent (answers vary run to run), and leaves you without historical data. Tools earn their price by automating the repeated prompt runs and giving you comparable, dated results you can act on - that's the main thing you're paying for, not the prompts themselves.
