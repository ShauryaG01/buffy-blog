Klevu vs Boost AI Search & Filter: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Klevu and Boost AI Search & Filter head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 7 min read

Overview

If you're weighing Klevu against Boost AI Search & Filter, you're almost certainly a Shopify (or Shopify Plus) merchant trying to fix weak on-site search, filtering, or merchandising - the kind of gap that quietly costs conversions every day. These two tools solve the same problem from opposite ends of the market. Klevu, now part of Athos Commerce, is an enterprise-grade AI search and discovery platform sold on custom quotes with no public pricing - built for mid-market and enterprise catalogs that need deep, self-learning merchandising logic. Boost AI Search & Filter is Shopify-native, self-serve, and transparently priced from $29/mo, scaling through GMV-based tiers as your store grows. Short version: for the typical DTC brand or professional growth marketer running a single Shopify storefront - whether based in the US, the UK, or India - Boost is the faster, cheaper, more predictable starting point. Klevu earns its place when you've outgrown Shopify-only tooling, need custom merchandising rules across multiple channels, and have the budget and procurement patience for an enterprise sales cycle. Neither is universally 'better' - the right pick depends on catalog complexity, GMV, and how much you value pricing transparency over enterprise depth. If your bigger issue isn't just on-site search but also getting your products surfaced in AI shopping assistants and off-site discovery, that's a different problem these two tools don't address - worth flagging before you commit budget to either.

Best For

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if you run a single Shopify store and want AI-powered search, filtering, and merchandising you can sign up for today, with clear published tiers ($29 to $1,499/mo) rather than a sales call. It's the right default for most DTC brands and professional growth marketers still scaling toward the seven-figure GMV range.

Pick Klevu if you're a mid-market or enterprise merchant with a large, complex catalog - especially across multiple storefronts or regions (US, EU, India, etc.) - and need custom merchandising rules, self-learning ranking tuned by a vendor team, and are comfortable with quote-based procurement instead of self-serve pricing.

Pick Boost if predictable, itemized monthly billing matters more than bespoke configuration - GMV tiers are transparent even if they rise with sales.

Pick Klevu if you're already evaluating, or invested in, the broader Athos Commerce ecosystem and want discovery tooling that extends beyond a single Shopify storefront.

If what you actually need is on-site AI search paired with off-site AI-visibility - making sure your products surface correctly when shoppers ask AI assistants for recommendations, not just when they search your own site - that's a gap neither Klevu nor Boost is built to close, and it's the specific niche Buffy is designed for.

Pros And Cons

Klevu
Pros: Self-learning AI ranking that improves with catalog and behavior data; strong merchandising controls for complex, high-SKU catalogs; backed by Athos Commerce's broader enterprise commerce tooling; suited to multi-channel discovery beyond a single Shopify theme.
Cons: No public pricing anywhere - every evaluation starts with a sales conversation; quote-based procurement slows down testing and budgeting; likely overkill (and overpriced) for a single-storefront DTC brand under seven figures in revenue.

Boost AI Search & Filter
Pros: Transparent entry pricing at $29/mo with clearly published GMV tiers up to $1,499/mo; purpose-built for Shopify, so setup is fast without custom integration work; AI semantic search, filtering, and merchandising bundled in one app; monthly billing with no long-term lock-in.
Cons: GMV-based pricing means your search tool's cost scales with your success - $2 per extra $1,000 GMV over your tier cap adds up fast for a store growing quickly; Shopify-only, so it won't help brands running multi-platform or headless storefronts; higher tiers ($599–$1,499/mo) are a real cost for mid-size stores, even if still cheaper than an enterprise Klevu contract.

Recommendation

For most DTC founders and growth marketers on Shopify, Boost AI Search & Filter is the more defensible starting point: transparent pricing from $29/mo, Shopify-native setup, and monthly billing let you test and budget for AI search without a sales cycle. The tradeoff is real - GMV-based tiers mean your search bill grows alongside your revenue, and a fast-growing store can find itself jumping from $159/mo to $599/mo or higher faster than expected. Klevu is the right call once you've genuinely outgrown Shopify-only tooling: large or multi-channel catalogs, custom merchandising logic, or enterprise SLAs where a quote-based engagement is a feature, not a bug, of getting dedicated support. Don't choose Klevu for the brand name if a $29–$159/mo Boost plan already covers your catalog size - and don't stay on Boost past the point where GMV overages start costing more than a Klevu contract would. Either way, both tools stop at your site's front door; if AI-driven shopping assistants finding and recommending your products matters as much as your own search bar, that's a separate problem worth solving alongside whichever of these two you pick.

Faq

How much do Klevu and Boost AI Search & Filter cost?

Boost AI Search & Filter starts at $29/mo and scales through GMV-based tiers: $159/mo under $50K GMV, $599/mo under $400K GMV, and $1,499/mo under $2.5M GMV, with a $2 overage per extra $1,000 GMV above your tier cap, billed monthly only. Klevu has no public pricing - its Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery plans are quote-based since its merger into Athos Commerce, so you'll need to request a quote to get a number.

Which is cheaper, Klevu or Boost AI Search & Filter?

Boost is cheaper to start and easier to budget for, since its pricing is public and tiered from $29/mo. Klevu's actual cost is unknown until you go through its sales process, but enterprise quote-based AI search tools typically run well above Boost's published tiers - the tradeoff is that Klevu's pricing is negotiated to fit larger, more complex deployments rather than a fixed self-serve tier.

Is Boost AI Search & Filter worth it for a growing Shopify store?

Yes, for most stores under a few million in GMV - you get AI semantic search, filtering, and merchandising for a predictable monthly fee, with no long-term contract. The caveat is that as your GMV climbs, so does your bill, both through the higher published tiers and the $2-per-$1,000 overage past your tier cap. Worth it if search quality is driving conversions; worth reviewing annually as your GMV grows to make sure the tier you're on still makes sense.

Does Klevu work with Shopify?

Klevu is built to integrate with Shopify and other major ecommerce platforms as part of its enterprise discovery offering, but it's positioned for mid-market and enterprise merchants rather than as a lightweight Shopify app. If you're Shopify-only and want the fastest, most self-serve setup, Boost is the more purpose-built fit; Klevu makes more sense if Shopify is one storefront among several in a larger catalog strategy.

What happens to Boost's price as my store grows?

Boost's pricing is GMV-tiered, so your monthly fee steps up as your store's gross merchandise value crosses each threshold ($50K, $400K, $2.5M), and if you exceed your current tier's cap mid-cycle, you're charged $2 for every additional $1,000 in GMV until you upgrade. It's transparent, but it means a fast-growth quarter can bump your search bill meaningfully - worth modeling against your revenue forecast rather than assuming a flat monthly cost.
