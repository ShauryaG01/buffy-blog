Fast Simon vs Searchspring: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Fast Simon and Searchspring head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

Fast Simon and Searchspring both sell AI-powered site search and merchandising for Shopify and Shopify Plus stores, but they sit in different places on the buyer spectrum. Fast Simon publishes real plan pricing starting at $40/mo, with a free tier for very small stores, which makes it the easier of the two to evaluate without a sales call. Searchspring doesn't publish pricing at all - every deal is a custom quote tied to a 12-month contract, which signals enterprise-grade merchandising depth but also a slower, more sales-driven buying process.

The short version: Fast Simon is the better fit if you want to self-serve, see pricing upfront, and scale through clearly defined tiers as traffic grows. Searchspring is the better fit if you're already at real enterprise scale, need granular merchandising rules and a dedicated implementation team, and don't mind a 12-month commitment. Neither tool addresses how your products show up in AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, or AI Overviews - that's a separate discovery problem, and one worth considering alongside either of these if AI-driven traffic is growing in your mix. That's where a tool like Buffy comes in, not as a search replacement but as the AI-visibility layer neither vendor covers.

Best For

  • Pick Fast Simon if you're a mid-market Shopify store that wants AI-driven search and merchandising with pricing you can see and budget against before ever talking to sales.
  • Pick Searchspring if you're an established or enterprise retailer that needs granular merchandising rules, dedicated onboarding, and can commit to a 12-month contract in exchange for deeper customization.
  • Pick Fast Simon over Searchspring if speed-to-launch and self-serve setup matter more to you than a dedicated implementation team walking you through configuration.
  • Pick Buffy alongside either one if your growth problem isn't just on-site search - you also want visibility into how your products surface (or don't) in AI answer engines, which neither Fast Simon nor Searchspring measures.

Pros And Cons

Fast Simon
Pros: transparent published pricing across five tiers; a free tier lets very small stores start immediately; strong AI search and merchandising feature set purpose-built for Shopify; upsell and cross-sell tooling included rather than bolted on.
Cons: session-based pricing means cost scales with traffic, not just feature usage, so a viral spike can push you into a higher tier; the top Enterprise tier still requires a custom quote, so full transparency disappears right when stakes are highest.

Searchspring
Pros: deep, enterprise-tested merchandising and site search backed by the resources of Athos Commerce; usage-based custom quotes can be tailored to complex catalogs and multi-brand setups that off-the-shelf tiers don't fit well.
Cons: zero public pricing means every evaluation starts with a sales cycle rather than a self-serve trial; the 12-month contract removes flexibility if the tool underdelivers; it's generally reported as one of the pricier options in this category, which is a real barrier for smaller teams.

Recommendation

For most Shopify and Shopify Plus stores in the mid-market - optimizing conversion but not yet running a dedicated enterprise merchandising team - Fast Simon is the more defensible default. You can see the full pricing ladder ($40 → $100 → $500/mo, custom above that), start on the free tier, and get AI search and merchandising live without a procurement cycle. Searchspring earns its keep at the top end: if you have a large, complex catalog, a merchandising team that wants granular rule-building, and budget for a 12-month commitment, its enterprise pedigree under Athos Commerce is a legitimate reason to go through the custom-quote process instead. What neither tool solves is AI-engine visibility - if your traffic increasingly comes from ChatGPT, Perplexity, or AI Overviews rather than traditional search, that's a gap worth covering separately, which is where Buffy fits for DTC brands as a complement, not a competitor, to either search vendor.

Faq

How much does Fast Simon cost compared to Searchspring?

Fast Simon publishes exact pricing: a Free tier, then Starter at $40/mo, Essential at $100/mo, Growth at $500/mo, and a custom Enterprise tier, all on a session-based model. Searchspring publishes no pricing at all - both its Essential and Expert plans are custom-quoted, usage-based, and tied to a 12-month contract. If seeing a number before you talk to sales matters to you, Fast Simon is the only one of the two that offers that.

Is Searchspring worth the cost for a Shopify store?

It depends on scale. Searchspring is generally reported as one of the more expensive options in this category, and the 12-month contract raises the stakes of getting it wrong. It's worth it if you have a large catalog and a merchandising team that will actually use its deeper rule-building; it's likely overkill - and pricier than necessary - for a store that just needs solid AI-driven search.

Does Fast Simon have a free plan?

Yes. Fast Simon offers a free tier before its paid plans start at $40/mo (Starter). That makes it one of the few tools in this category you can trial live on your own store without a sales conversation first.

Which is better for a small or growing Shopify brand: Fast Simon or Searchspring?

Fast Simon, in most cases. Its published pricing and session-based tiers scale more predictably for a growing store, and you're not locked into a 12-month contract before you know whether it works. Searchspring's custom-quote model and enterprise focus tend to fit brands with a dedicated merchandising function, not startups that need to iterate quickly on a budget.

Do I still need something else if I use Fast Simon or Searchspring?

Both tools focus on on-site search and merchandising - helping shoppers who are already on your store find products faster and buy more. Neither addresses whether your products get surfaced in AI answer engines like ChatGPT or Perplexity, which is an increasingly separate discovery channel. If that's part of your growth plan, it's a distinct problem - the category Buffy is built for - rather than something either search vendor covers.
