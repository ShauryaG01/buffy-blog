Boost AI Search & Filter vs Searchspring: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Boost AI Search & Filter and Searchspring head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 6 min read

Overview

Boost AI Search & Filter and Searchspring both replace Shopify's native search, but they serve different scales of business. Boost is built for growing Shopify and Shopify Plus DTC brands, priced by GMV starting at $29/mo. Searchspring is enterprise-grade site search and merchandising from Athos Commerce, sold through custom quotes on 12-month contracts, aimed at mid-market and enterprise retailers with dedicated ecommerce teams. If you're a DTC founder or a professional growth team, whether you're based in the US, UK, or India, running under roughly $2.5M GMV on Shopify and want AI semantic search live in days without a sales call, Boost is the more practical entry point. If you're a larger retailer with a merchandising team that needs deep personalization, testing infrastructure, and dedicated implementation support, Searchspring's higher price and contract length can pay for itself. Short version: Boost wins on speed, pricing transparency, and cost predictability at DTC scale. Searchspring wins on enterprise depth once you have the team and budget to use it well.

Best For

Pick Boost if you're a Shopify or Shopify Plus DTC brand under roughly $2.5M in annual GMV and want AI search live fast without a procurement process.

Pick Boost if predictable, published pricing matters more to you than deep customization. Even with GMV overages, you know the tiers going in, which makes budgeting easier for a growth-stage team.

Pick Searchspring if you're a mid-market or enterprise retailer with an in-house merchandising team that needs personalization, testing, and configuration options beyond what an app-store tool typically offers.

Pick Searchspring if you're comfortable with a 12-month contract and a custom-quote sales process in exchange for dedicated implementation and support at scale.

Pros And Cons

Boost AI Search & Filter
Pros: Fast, self-serve setup built specifically for Shopify's catalog structure. AI semantic search, filtering, and merchandising bundled into one tool. Transparent starting price at $29/mo with published GMV tiers, so you know roughly what you'll pay before you sign up.
Cons: Pricing is tied to GMV, so your bill climbs automatically as sales grow, not just as you add features. Once you're past the $2.5M GMV tier, you're into per-unit overage pricing ($2 per extra $1,000 GMV). Monthly billing only, no annual commitment option.

Searchspring
Pros: Purpose-built for larger catalogs and complex merchandising needs. Deep personalization and site search capability that's earned its reputation with mid-market and enterprise retailers. Part of Athos Commerce, so it sits inside a broader commerce stack for teams that want that.
Cons: No public pricing, you have to go through a sales process to know what you'll pay (verify current pricing). 12-month contract is a real commitment for a team that's still iterating. Reported by users as expensive relative to app-store alternatives, and onboarding is slower and more sales-led than a self-serve tool.

Recommendation

Bottom line: for most DTC Shopify brands under roughly $2.5M GMV, Boost AI Search & Filter is the more practical choice. Transparent pricing starting at $29/mo, fast self-serve setup, and AI search and filtering built specifically for Shopify's catalog structure make it easy to test and easy to budget for. The tradeoff is that GMV-based pricing grows with your revenue, so model the $159 to $1,499/mo tiers, plus the $2-per-$1,000 overage, against your growth forecast before committing.

Searchspring earns its keep at the other end of the market. Retailers with dedicated merchandising teams, complex catalogs, and the budget for a custom quote and 12-month contract will get deeper personalization and enterprise support than an app-store tool typically offers. It's often overkill, and can be overpriced, for a single-brand DTC store still finding product-market fit.

One more consideration: on-site search is only half of discovery today. If you're also thinking about how your products surface in AI answer engines like ChatGPT or Perplexity, that's a separate problem that neither Boost nor Searchspring solves. Buffy is built specifically for that off-site AI-visibility layer and pairs naturally with an on-site tool like Boost for DTC brands that want both covered. For the core question in this comparison, though: choose Boost for speed and cost control at DTC scale, choose Searchspring once you've outgrown app-store tooling and need enterprise merchandising.

Faq

How much does Boost AI Search & Filter cost?

Boost uses GMV-based pricing with four published tiers: Entry at $29/mo, $159/mo for stores under $50K GMV, $599/mo for stores under $400K GMV, and $1,499/mo for stores under $2.5M GMV. Above that, you pay $2 for every extra $1,000 in GMV. It's billed monthly only, with no annual plan currently offered.

How much does Searchspring cost?

Searchspring doesn't publish pricing. It sells Essential and Expert plans through custom, usage-based quotes tied to a 12-month contract, so actual cost depends on your catalog size, traffic, and negotiated terms. Verify current pricing directly with their sales team before assuming a number.

Is Boost AI Search & Filter worth it for a small Shopify store?

If you're roughly under $50K to $400K in GMV and Shopify's native search isn't converting well, yes: $29 to $159/mo for AI semantic search and merchandising is a relatively low-risk way to test whether better search moves revenue. Just watch how fast your GMV grows, since the pricing tier (and your bill) moves up with it.

Which is better for Shopify Plus stores, Boost or Searchspring?

It depends on scale and team, not platform. A Shopify Plus store still in DTC-growth mode, roughly under $2.5M GMV, is usually better served by Boost's faster setup and lower, more predictable cost. A Shopify Plus enterprise retailer with a merchandising team and budget for a 12-month contract will typically get more depth and support from Searchspring.

Do Boost and Searchspring actually replace Shopify's built-in search?

Yes, both are designed to replace or significantly upgrade Shopify's native search and filtering with AI-driven relevance, synonym handling, and merchandising controls. Neither changes your underlying product catalog structure; they layer better search, filtering, and ranking logic on top of it.
