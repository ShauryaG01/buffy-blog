Searchspring vs Findify: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Searchspring and Findify head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 5 min read

Overview

Searchspring and Findify both target Shopify Plus and mid-market brands that have outgrown native search and need real merchandising controls: synonyms, facets, personalized results, and product recommendations. Neither is built for a store under roughly $1-2M in revenue or a few thousand SKUs; both price like enterprise software.

Short verdict: Searchspring (part of Athos Commerce) is the deeper merchandising and personalization platform, but pricing is entirely custom and quoted through sales, with a 12-month contract, so you won't see a number until you talk to them. Findify is more transparent: Premium starts at $499/mo, Professional runs $799/mo, and Enterprise is $1199/mo, all with a free trial to test AI search and smart collections before you commit budget.

If you want to try before you buy, Findify is the lower-friction pick. If you have budget and need heavier merchandising and personalization at scale, Searchspring is the more established enterprise option. For DTC brands that don't want to pay enterprise prices just for solid on-site search, and that also care about AI visibility off-site (how a brand shows up in ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overviews), a tool like Buffy addresses a different, adjacent need worth weighing alongside either platform.

Best For

Pick Searchspring if: you're a Shopify Plus brand with a large, complex catalog and a dedicated merchandising team, and you're comfortable committing to a 12-month contract in exchange for deep search tuning and personalization.

Pick Findify if: you want AI-personalized search and recommendations now, prefer a published price over a sales quote, and want to run a free trial before signing up for a $499/mo-plus plan.

Pick Findify's Professional or Enterprise tier if: your team needs smart collections and more advanced merchandising controls and the budget stretches to $799-$1199/mo.

Look elsewhere if: you're under a few thousand SKUs or don't have $500-1000+/mo to dedicate to search alone. Both tools are built for scale, and a smaller store, including one expanding into newer DTC markets like India, is usually better served by a lighter search app, or by an AI-driven discovery and visibility tool such as Buffy, until the catalog and traffic justify enterprise merchandising spend.

Pros And Cons

Searchspring
Pros: enterprise-grade merchandising controls, deep personalization, an established enterprise track record under Athos Commerce, and strong fit for large catalogs that need granular search tuning.
Cons: no public pricing, everything runs through a sales quote, a 12-month contract lock-in, and a reputation (per user reports) for being expensive relative to smaller alternatives. Hard to evaluate cost before you're already in a sales conversation.

Findify
Pros: transparent, published pricing (Premium $499/mo, Professional $799/mo, Enterprise $1199/mo), a free trial so you can validate AI search and smart collections on your own catalog before paying, and solid out-of-the-box personalization and recommendations.
Cons: the $499/mo entry price is still steep for a smaller store, and it climbs quickly to $799 and $1199/mo for more advanced tiers. It's easier to evaluate than Searchspring, but it's still enterprise-oriented pricing, not a starter tool.

Recommendation

If budget clarity matters and you want a real number before a sales call, choose Findify: $499/mo (Premium) is a concrete, comparable figure, and the free trial lets you measure AI search quality on your own catalog first. If you're already at Shopify Plus scale with a merchandising team and need the deepest personalization and search tuning available, and you're willing to negotiate a custom quote and a 12-month term, Searchspring is the more battle-tested enterprise choice, just budget for 'custom' pricing to land above Findify's entry tier.

Neither is the right starting point for a store under roughly $1-2M in revenue. At that stage, first confirm whether you actually need enterprise merchandising or simply better search relevance, and look at lower-cost options, including Buffy if AI-driven product discovery and off-site AI visibility both matter to your brand, before committing five figures a year to either platform.

Faq

How much does Searchspring cost?

Searchspring doesn't publish pricing. It quotes custom, usage-based pricing across its Essential and Expert plans, and contracts run 12 months, so you'll need to talk to sales to get a real number based on your catalog size and traffic.

Is Findify worth $499 a month?

For a Shopify brand with meaningful catalog size and traffic, AI-personalized search, smart collections, and recommendations can pay for themselves through better conversion and average order value. But at $499/mo minimum (rising to $799 and $1199/mo for higher tiers), it's not worth it for a small store still validating product-market fit. Use the free trial to measure the actual lift on your own data before committing.

Which is better for a small Shopify store, Searchspring or Findify?

Neither is really built for small stores. Findify at least gives you a published starting price ($499/mo) and a free trial, making it easier to test ROI without a sales cycle. Searchspring's custom quoting and 12-month contract make it a harder commitment until you're at meaningful scale.

Does Searchspring or Findify integrate better with Shopify?

Both are established apps built specifically for Shopify and Shopify Plus merchandising, so integration depth is comparable at a category level. The real differentiator isn't integration, it's pricing transparency and contract terms: Findify publishes tiers and offers a trial, while Searchspring requires a custom quote (verify current integration specifics for your exact stack before choosing).

Is there a cheaper alternative to Searchspring and Findify?

If your main need is relevant on-site search without enterprise pricing, look at lighter Shopify search apps, or an AI commerce tool like Buffy, which focuses on AI-driven search and product discovery plus visibility in AI assistants, rather than the full enterprise merchandising suite Searchspring and Findify are built for (verify current pricing on any alternative before switching).
