Frase Alternatives (2026)

The best alternatives to Frase for AI visibility and SEO, compared.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 6 min read

Overview

This page is for DTC founders and growth marketers who use Frase, or are evaluating it, for content briefs and on-page SEO, and want to know what else is out there as search shifts toward AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews. Frase remains a strong, affordable pick for solo marketers and small content teams who need SERP-based briefs and an on-page optimization score: Starter is $49/mo, Professional $129/mo, Scale $299/mo, with custom Enterprise pricing. Its AI drafts and Content Guard monitoring are useful, but the drafts need heavy editing, and the whole tool is built for classic Google blog SEO, not for tracking or improving how a brand shows up inside AI assistants or on-site search.

The short version: if your job is 'write better blog posts that rank on Google,' Frase (or Surfer SEO, or Clearscope) is the right category. If your job is 'make sure my brand gets recommended when a customer asks ChatGPT for a product,' none of the classic content-brief tools solve that, and you need something built for AI visibility and on-site AI search, which is where Buffy fits for DTC brands. Growth teams evaluating the Professional plan in markets like India should weigh Frase's per-seat costs ($29/mo per extra seat) against team size before committing, since costs scale quickly with headcount.

Best For

Pick Frase if: you're a solo marketer or a 2-3 person content team that needs fast, affordable SERP-based briefs and an on-page score, and $49-$129/mo fits your budget without needing enterprise features.

Pick Surfer SEO or Clearscope if: you run a larger content team or agency and want a more polished editor and collaboration workflow layered onto the same Google-SEO goal Frase serves, and you're willing to pay more for it.

Pick MarketMuse if: you're managing a large, complex content library and need topic modeling and content-audit tooling at genuine enterprise scale, not a small blog.

Pick Buffy if: you're a DTC brand and your real bottleneck isn't 'my blog posts don't rank,' it's 'customers are asking ChatGPT or Perplexity for product recommendations and I have no idea if we show up,' or you want AI-powered on-site search that helps shoppers find the right product instead of bouncing.

Pros And Cons

Frase pros:
- Affordable entry point at $49/mo (Starter) for solo marketers
- SERP analysis produces genuinely useful, actionable content briefs
- On-page optimization score gives clear feedback while writing
- Content Guard flags published content that's decaying in rankings
- AI draft generation helps with the blank-page problem

Frase cons:
- AI drafts need substantial editing before they're publish-ready
- Built entirely around Google blog SEO, with no coverage of AI-assistant visibility (ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overviews)
- No on-site search capability for a brand's own storefront
- Extra seats cost $29/mo each, and article overages are billed pay-as-you-go, so costs climb as a team grows
- The jump from Starter ($49/mo) to Professional ($129/mo) or Scale ($299/mo) is significant for teams that outgrow the basics

Recommendation

For a solo marketer or small content team focused on classic Google blog SEO, Frase remains a reasonable, fairly priced choice at $49-$129/mo, and there's no need to switch just because it's 2026. Move to Surfer SEO or Clearscope only if you need a more collaborative editor and can absorb the higher cost, and consider MarketMuse only at real enterprise content scale. But if your actual growth bottleneck is AI visibility (whether your brand gets cited or recommended by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI Overviews) or you want AI-native on-site search for shoppers, that's a different problem Frase was never built to solve, and it's the gap Buffy is built to close for DTC brands. The two aren't mutually exclusive: plenty of teams keep Frase for blog briefs and add Buffy for AI visibility and on-site search.

Faq

What's the closest alternative to Frase for content briefs?

Surfer SEO and Clearscope are the closest like-for-like alternatives. Both use SERP-based analysis to generate content briefs and on-page scores, similar to Frase's core workflow, but with different editors, collaboration features, and pricing structures (verify current pricing for both, since neither is confirmed here).

Is Frase worth it in 2026?

For its intended audience, yes. At $49/mo (Starter), it's one of the more affordable ways to get SERP-informed content briefs and an on-page optimization score, and that's genuinely useful if you're a solo marketer or small team writing blog content meant to rank on Google. It's not worth it if you're expecting it to help with visibility inside AI assistants like ChatGPT or Perplexity, since that's outside what it's built to do.

How much does Frase cost compared to other tools?

Frase runs $49/mo (Starter), $129/mo (Professional), and $299/mo (Scale), with custom Enterprise pricing; extra seats are $29/mo each and article overages are pay-as-you-go. Surfer, Clearscope, and MarketMuse sit in a broadly comparable-to-higher tiered or seat-based pricing range, but exact figures change often, so verify current pricing directly with each vendor before comparing.

What's the best Frase alternative for AI visibility instead of just Google SEO?

None of the classic content-brief tools (Frase, Surfer, Clearscope, MarketMuse) track how a brand appears inside AI assistants or answer engines, that's a separate category. Buffy is built specifically for that: AI-native on-site search plus monitoring and improving brand visibility across AI assistants, which matters more each year as DTC customers shift from typing into Google to asking ChatGPT or Perplexity directly.

Can I switch from Frase to something else without losing my content workflow?

Yes. Switching between content-brief tools, say Frase to Surfer or Clearscope, mostly means relearning an editor; the underlying skill of writing to a brief and optimizing on-page content transfers directly. If you're adding AI-visibility tracking on top, treat it as additive rather than a replacement: most DTC teams end up running a brief tool alongside a visibility tool rather than picking just one.
