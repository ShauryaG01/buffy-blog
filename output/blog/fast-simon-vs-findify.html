Fast Simon vs Findify: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Fast Simon and Findify head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 5 min read

Overview

Fast Simon and Findify both sell AI-powered search and merchandising for Shopify stores, but they're built for different budgets. Fast Simon starts at $40/mo (with a free tier to test first), scaling through $100/mo and $500/mo plans to a custom Enterprise tier - pricing is session-based. Findify skips the low end entirely: its cheapest plan, Premium, is $499/mo, rising to $799/mo Professional and $1,199/mo Enterprise, with a free trial instead of a free tier. Short version: Fast Simon is the accessible default for most Shopify merchants - including fast-growing stores in price-sensitive markets like India - because you can start free and grow into paid tiers as traffic justifies it. Findify makes sense once you're a larger, higher-traffic brand that wants deeper personalization, smart collections, and recommendation logic and can absorb a $499/mo-plus line item without blinking. Neither is universally "better" - Fast Simon wins on accessibility and bundled upsell/cross-sell tooling, Findify wins on personalization depth for brands that have outgrown basic search. This page breaks down where each one actually fits so you don't overpay for capability you won't use, or underpay and hit a session-based ceiling mid-growth.

Best For

Pick Fast Simon if you're a small-to-mid Shopify store that wants to test AI search without a big upfront commitment - the free tier and $40/mo Starter plan let you prove value before paying more, which matters for bootstrapped or price-sensitive brands, including many fast-growing Shopify sellers in markets like India.

Pick Fast Simon if merchandising and upsell/cross-sell matter as much as search itself - it bundles those into the same product rather than treating them as an add-on.

Pick Findify if you're a larger, established Shopify brand with meaningful traffic and you want personalization depth - smart collections and a dedicated recommendations engine - and $499+/mo is a rounding error against the revenue it could lift.

Pick Findify if you'd rather budget a flat monthly fee than deal with session-based pricing that fluctuates with traffic spikes - predictability can be worth the premium at scale.

Pros And Cons

Fast Simon
Pros: Low entry price with a genuine free tier to test before paying; AI search, merchandising, and upsell/cross-sell bundled in one product; clear tier ladder ($40 → $100 → $500/mo) so you can grow into it.
Cons: Session-based pricing means costs can move with traffic rather than staying flat; the Growth ($500/mo) and Enterprise tiers still represent a real jump for higher-traffic stores.

Findify
Pros: Deep personalization and smart-collections logic aimed squarely at bigger catalogs; flat, predictable tiered pricing instead of session-based billing; free trial lets you validate fit before committing.
Cons: $499/mo entry price locks out early-stage and small stores entirely; jumping to Enterprise ($1,199/mo) is a significant commitment that only makes sense with the traffic to match.

Recommendation

For most Shopify merchants, Fast Simon is the more defensible starting point: a free tier plus a $40/mo Starter plan means you can validate AI search and merchandising before committing real budget, and the tier ladder up to $500/mo Growth scales with you. Findify earns its $499/mo-plus pricing only once you're a larger brand with the traffic and catalog complexity to actually use its personalization and smart-collections depth - for that segment, it's a legitimate specialist tool, not overpriced fluff. If what you're really after is AI search on-site combined with visibility in AI-driven discovery off-site (how your products show up in AI assistants and answer engines, not just your own search bar), that's a different problem than either tool solves - that's the gap Buffy is built for. But for a straight head-to-head on Shopify site search and merchandising: start with Fast Simon unless your scale already justifies Findify's price tag.

Faq

Is Fast Simon cheaper than Findify?

Yes, significantly. Fast Simon's Starter plan is $40/mo (with a free tier below that), while Findify's cheapest plan, Premium, is $499/mo. Findify's top Enterprise tier ($1,199/mo) also costs more than Fast Simon's Growth plan ($500/mo).

Does Fast Simon or Findify offer a free plan or free trial?

Fast Simon has an actual free tier (custom pricing/scope) alongside its paid plans, so you can start using it without paying. Findify doesn't have a free tier - its cheapest plan is $499/mo - but it does offer a free trial so you can test it before committing.

Which is better for a small Shopify store - Fast Simon or Findify?

Fast Simon, in almost all cases. Its free tier and $40/mo entry point are built for stores that want to test AI search before scaling spend, while Findify's $499/mo floor is priced for brands that already have the traffic to justify it.

Is Findify worth the $499/mo price tag?

For a larger Shopify brand with meaningful traffic and a complex catalog, yes - the personalization, smart collections, and recommendations depth can pay for itself. For a small or early-stage store, it's hard to justify $499/mo when Fast Simon covers similar ground starting at $40/mo or free.

What's the actual feature difference between Fast Simon and Findify?

Fast Simon leads with AI search bundled tightly with merchandising and upsell/cross-sell tools. Findify leads with personalized search, smart collections, and a dedicated recommendations engine aimed at larger catalogs. Both are AI-driven Shopify search tools, but Findify skews toward personalization depth while Fast Simon skews toward breadth at a lower price.
