Boost AI Search & Filter Review (2026): Pricing, Features, and Who It Fits

Boost AI Search & Filter reviewed for Shopify merchants - real pricing, App Store ratings, and best-fit store size.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 6 min read

Overview

Boost AI Search & Filter (Boost Commerce) is a Shopify app that adds AI-powered search, faceted filtering, and merchandising controls to a store's product discovery experience. This review is for Shopify merchants sizing it up against native search and other search/filter apps, using its actual 2026 pricing tiers rather than marketing copy.

Short version: it's a genuinely capable AI search and merchandising tool for Shopify, well-suited to stores with catalogs large enough that keyword search alone hurts conversion. The catch is the pricing model: plans are tied to GMV, not features or query volume, so the bill grows automatically as the store grows, with a $2 per extra $1,000 GMV overage on top. If you're comfortable with pricing that tracks revenue, it's a strong choice. If you want cost tied to usage instead of sales, compare it against flat-fee alternatives before committing.

Best For

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if: you run a Shopify store with a catalog large enough that keyword search misses relevant products, and you want AI semantic search, filters, and merchandising rules without adding a separate search vendor outside Shopify.

Pick Shopify's native search if: you're pre-revenue or early-stage with a small catalog (under a few hundred SKUs) and don't yet need faceted filtering or merchandising rules. Save the spend until search is actually costing you conversions.

Pick Klevu or Algolia if: you need search that spans more than one storefront, want deep control over relevance ranking, or you're a larger brand where a flat or usage-based pricing model is preferable to one tied to GMV.

Pick Buffy if: on-site search is only half the problem, you also want your products showing up when shoppers ask AI assistants and answer engines for recommendations, and you'd rather solve on-site AI search and off-site AI-visibility with one vendor than stitch together two.

Pros And Cons

Pros
- AI semantic search understands intent and synonyms, not just exact keyword matches, which typically helps conversion on catalogs with hundreds-plus SKUs.
- Faceted filtering and merchandising controls (pinning, boosting, rule-based sorting) are built specifically for Shopify's data model, so setup tends to be more plug-and-play than general-purpose search infrastructure.
- The $29/mo entry plan gives small stores a low-cost way to try AI search before scaling into higher tiers.

Cons
- Pricing is GMV-based, not usage-based: a store doing $2.5M in GMV pays $1499/mo regardless of how much search traffic it actually generates, and crosses into $2-per-$1,000-GMV overage beyond that.
- The jump between tiers is steep ($29 to $159 to $599 to $1499), so a growing store can face a materially higher bill for the same feature set.
- Monthly billing only, with no annual plan mentioned, so there's no obvious lever to lock in a lower rate.

Recommendation

Boost AI Search & Filter earns its place on the shortlist for Shopify merchants whose catalog has outgrown keyword search: the AI semantic search, filtering, and merchandising feature set is real and Shopify-native, not a thin wrapper. The bottom line, though, is that the pricing model matters as much as the feature list. Because plans are GMV-based with a $2-per-$1,000-GMV overage, the cost of running Boost climbs automatically as the store grows, independent of whether search usage grows at the same rate. Before committing, model what the bill looks like at your next GMV tier, not just your current one. If that growth-linked pricing is a dealbreaker, compare it against flatter-fee alternatives like Searchanise or Searchspring. And if the real gap is that your products aren't showing up when shoppers ask AI assistants for recommendations, not just weak on-site search, that's a different problem, one that a tool like Buffy is built to address alongside on-site search, not one Boost is trying to solve.

Faq

How much does Boost AI Search & Filter cost?

Boost AI Search & Filter uses GMV-based pricing: $29/mo for the entry plan, $159/mo for stores under $50K GMV, $599/mo for stores under $400K GMV, and $1499/mo for stores under $2.5M GMV. Stores that exceed their tier's GMV cap pay a $2 overage per extra $1,000 in GMV. Billing is monthly only.

Is Boost AI Search & Filter worth it for a small Shopify store?

For a very small or early-stage store with a simple catalog, probably not yet. Shopify's native search is free and adequate until your catalog and traffic reach a point where keyword search starts losing sales. Once you have enough SKUs that customers are filtering, refining, or abandoning search results, the $29/mo entry plan becomes a reasonable low-cost way to test whether AI search improves conversion before scaling into the higher GMV tiers.

How does Boost AI Search & Filter pricing scale as a store grows?

Pricing is tied to GMV, not query volume or feature usage. As your store crosses $50K, $400K, and $2.5M in GMV, you move into progressively more expensive tiers ($159, $599, and $1499/mo respectively), and if you outgrow your current tier's GMV cap mid-cycle, Boost charges $2 for every additional $1,000 in GMV. That means the bill grows automatically with revenue, so it's worth forecasting your GMV a tier or two ahead when budgeting.

How is Boost AI Search & Filter different from Shopify's built-in search?

Shopify's native search handles basic keyword matching and comes free with any plan, but it lacks the AI semantic understanding, faceted filtering, and merchandising controls (like pinning or rule-based boosting) that Boost adds. The tradeoff is cost: native search is free, Boost starts at $29/mo and rises with GMV.

Do I still need something like Buffy if I already have Boost AI Search & Filter?

It depends on what problem you're solving. Boost focuses on improving search and filtering inside your own storefront. If your goal is also to be found and recommended when shoppers ask AI assistants like ChatGPT or Perplexity for product suggestions, that's off-site AI-visibility, a different problem than on-site search relevance. Buffy is built to cover both on-site AI search and off-site AI-visibility for DTC brands, so it's worth considering as a complement, or alternative, if on-site search alone isn't the full gap you're trying to close.
