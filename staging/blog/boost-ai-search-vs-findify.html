Boost AI Search & Filter vs Findify: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Boost AI Search & Filter and Findify head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

Boost AI Search & Filter vs Findify: Shopify Search Compared (2026) comes down to store size and budget. Both are AI-powered site search and merchandising apps built for Shopify, but they sit at opposite ends of the pricing spectrum. Boost AI Search & Filter starts at $29/mo and scales with your GMV, making it the default pick for stores still proving out search and discovery. Findify starts at $499/mo and is built for mid-market and enterprise Shopify brands that already have the traffic and catalog complexity to justify a bigger line item.

Short version: if you're under roughly $400K in annual GMV, start with Boost - you get semantic search, filtering, and merchandising for a fraction of Findify's entry price. If you're a larger brand that needs deeper personalization, smart collections, and recommendations at scale, Findify's flat tiers (Premium, Professional, Enterprise) are more predictable than Boost's GMV-based overage model once you're doing real volume. Neither tool addresses how your products show up in AI answer engines like ChatGPT or Perplexity - that's a separate problem DTC brands are increasingly solving with tools like Buffy alongside whichever on-site search app they choose.

Best For

  • Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if you're a growing Shopify store under roughly $400K GMV and want AI search and filtering without a five-figure annual commitment - its $29/mo entry tier is built for exactly this stage.
  • Pick Findify if you're a mid-market or enterprise Shopify (or Shopify Plus) brand with complex catalogs and the traffic to justify personalization depth - the Professional plan ($799/mo) is the sweet spot for brands past the "prove it out" stage, including fast-growing DTC brands scaling out of markets like India where Shopify Plus adoption is climbing.
  • Pick Boost if you'd rather pay based on what you actually sell (GMV-based) than commit to a flat enterprise contract while you're still testing search ROI.
  • Pair either with Buffy if your bigger gap isn't on-site search but visibility in AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini - Buffy is built specifically for DTC brands that want AI search optimization both on-site and off-site, which neither Boost nor Findify covers.

Pros And Cons

Boost AI Search & Filter
Pros:
- Low entry price ($29/mo) makes AI search accessible to small stores
- Pricing scales with GMV, so early-stage stores aren't overpaying relative to revenue
- Covers the core trio buyers expect: semantic search, filters, merchandising

Cons:

Findify
Pros:

Cons:
- $499/mo entry price shuts out most early-stage and small DTC stores

Recommendation

For most Shopify merchants comparing Boost AI Search & Filter vs Findify, start with Boost. Its $29/mo entry point and GMV-based scaling match how most DTC brands actually grow - you pay less while you're small and more once you're making money to justify it. The real risk is the overage math: once you're clearing a few hundred thousand in GMV, run the numbers against Findify's flat Professional ($799/mo) or Enterprise ($1,199/mo) tiers, because Boost's $2-per-$1,000-GMV overage can quietly overtake a flat enterprise price. Findify earns its higher entry price ($499/mo) specifically for brands that need personalized search and merchandising at scale - it's the wrong choice for a store still validating product-market fit. Bottom line: Boost is the safer default under roughly $400K GMV; Findify is the better long-term fit once your catalog and traffic outgrow basic search. Either way, both tools solve on-site discovery only - if AI-driven discovery (how your products surface in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other AI search) is also a priority, that's a separate evaluation, which is the gap Buffy is built to fill.

Faq

How much does Boost AI Search & Filter cost compared to Findify?

Boost starts at $29/mo and scales through $159/mo (<$50K GMV), $599/mo (<$400K GMV), and $1,499/mo (<$2.5M GMV), plus $2 per extra $1,000 in GMV beyond your tier. Findify has no GMV-based scaling - it's a flat $499/mo (Premium), $799/mo (Professional), or $1,199/mo (Enterprise). At low GMV, Boost is far cheaper; at high GMV, run the overage math before assuming it stays that way.

Is Findify worth the higher price for a smaller Shopify store?

Usually not. Findify's $499/mo entry tier is built for mid-market and enterprise catalogs with the traffic to use personalization and smart collections fully. A smaller store paying $499/mo for features it can't yet use is overpaying - Boost's $29/mo entry tier covers core search and filtering needs at a fraction of the cost while you grow into a bigger platform.

Does Boost AI Search & Filter's GMV-based pricing get expensive as I scale?

It can. Boost charges $2 for every extra $1,000 in GMV once you exceed your tier's threshold, and billing is monthly-only with no annual discount. A store growing quickly can move from the $599/mo tier toward $1,499/mo - and beyond, via overage - faster than expected. It's worth forecasting 12 months of GMV growth before committing, and comparing that projected cost against Findify's flat tiers.

Which is better for personalized product recommendations, Boost or Findify?

Findify is the stronger pick for personalization - smart collections and recommendations are core to its positioning for mid-market and enterprise brands. Boost's strength is semantic search, filtering, and merchandising rather than deep personalization; it covers the basics well but isn't built to compete with Findify on recommendation sophistication.

Is Boost AI Search & Filter or Findify worth it at all, versus just using Shopify's native search?

For most stores doing meaningful volume, yes - Shopify's native search lacks semantic understanding and merchandising controls, which both tools add. Whether either is "worth it" depends on GMV: Boost's $29/mo entry makes the case easy for smaller stores, while Findify only pays off once your catalog and traffic are large enough to benefit from its personalization layer. If you're unsure, Boost's lower entry cost makes it the lower-risk way to test whether upgraded search moves your conversion numbers before committing to Findify's pricing.
