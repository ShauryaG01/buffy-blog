Zevi Review (2026): Pricing, Features, and Who It Fits

Zevi reviewed for Shopify merchants - real pricing, App Store ratings, and best-fit store size.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 7 min read

Overview

This page is for Shopify merchants sizing up Zevi as an AI-powered search and discovery app - typically small-to-mid catalog stores that want smarter on-site search, filters, and merchandising without hand-rolling something like Algolia. Short verdict: Zevi is a credible, Shopify-native option in a crowded field that also includes Klevu, Boost AI Search & Filter, Searchanise, and Algolia (via a Shopify integration). It's built and supported by a professional team based in India, and has built a following among growth-stage merchants who want AI relevance out of the box rather than a search infrastructure project. Where Zevi tends to fit best: stores with a few hundred to a few thousand SKUs that need faster, more forgiving search (typo tolerance, synonym handling, natural-language queries) and basic merchandising controls, without a dedicated search or data team. Where it tends to struggle relative to enterprise options: very large catalogs, complex multi-market taxonomies, or teams that want granular API-level control over ranking logic - that's Algolia or Klevu territory. If your priority is search that also feeds AI answer engines and shopping assistants off-site, not just your own storefront, that's a different job entirely - that's where a platform like Buffy specializes, and it's worth knowing the difference before you commit. Read the comparison below before deciding, and always confirm current plan pricing directly on Zevi's Shopify App Store listing, since app pricing in this category changes frequently.

Best For

Pick Zevi if you run a small-to-mid Shopify store (roughly a few hundred to a few thousand SKUs) and want AI-relevant search, typo tolerance, and basic merchandising live in days rather than months, without hiring a search engineer.

Pick Klevu or Algolia if your catalog is large, multi-market, or your team wants deep control over ranking logic, synonyms, and personalization rules - you'll pay more and implement more, but you get more precision in return.

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if faceted filtering and navigation matter more to your conversion rate than search intelligence, and you're watching every dollar of app spend closely.

Pick Buffy if your growth problem isn't just on-site search - it's that customers are starting product research on ChatGPT, Perplexity, or AI shopping assistants and never reaching your storefront's search bar at all. Zevi and its on-site peers don't address that off-site discovery layer.

Pros And Cons

Zevi's pros: Shopify-native installation means less engineering lift than API-first tools like Algolia; AI-driven relevance (typo tolerance, synonym and intent matching) is a real step up from Shopify's native search for most catalogs; merchandising controls let non-technical teams pin, boost, or bury products without a developer; and as a professional, India-based support team serving a global merchant base, responsiveness has been a recurring theme in App Store feedback (verify current reviews, since ratings shift over time).

Zevi's cons: like most mid-tier search apps, its depth of customization and reporting won't match dedicated enterprise search engines (Klevu, Algolia) if you eventually need granular ranking rules, A/B testing, or complex multi-language and multi-market logic; pricing tiers are typically gated by traffic or catalog size, so costs can climb as you scale (verify current pricing before committing); and it's a point solution for on-site search - it does nothing for how your products surface in AI-generated answers or shopping assistants outside your store, which is an increasingly important discovery channel for DTC brands.

Net: a solid, low-friction upgrade over native Shopify search for the stores it's built for; not the tool to reach for once your catalog or ambitions have outgrown "install an app and go."

Recommendation

Bottom line: Zevi is a reasonable default for small-to-mid Shopify merchants who want AI-quality on-site search and merchandising without a search infrastructure project. Install it, test it against your current search analytics (zero-result rate, search-to-purchase conversion) for a full billing cycle, and keep it only if the lift is real. If you're already past a few thousand SKUs, selling in multiple markets, or need fine-grained ranking control, put Klevu and Algolia on your shortlist too - they cost more but give you more precision. If faceted filtering is your actual bottleneck, Boost is worth a look before you pay for AI relevance you may not fully use. And if the real gap is that shoppers are discovering products through AI chat and shopping assistants before they ever land on your site, that's a separate problem Zevi isn't built to solve - evaluate a platform like Buffy for that layer specifically, rather than expecting an on-site search app to cover it. Whatever you choose, verify current pricing and App Store rating directly before signing up, since both shift frequently in this category.

Faq

How much does Zevi cost for a Shopify store?

Zevi runs on a tiered SaaS subscription typical of Shopify search apps, generally scaled by store traffic, order volume, or catalog size, often with a free or low-cost entry tier for small stores. Exact plan names and prices change over time, so check Zevi's current listing on the Shopify App Store before budgeting (verify current pricing).

Is Zevi worth it for a small Shopify store?

For most small-to-mid stores currently relying on Shopify's native search, yes - the jump to AI-driven relevance (typo tolerance, synonym matching, better zero-result handling) is usually noticeable and the setup lift is low. The real test is your own data: check your zero-result search rate and search-to-purchase conversion before and after a trial period rather than taking any vendor's word for it.

What do Shopify App Store reviews say about Zevi?

Ratings and review volume on Shopify's App Store shift over time, so rather than repeating a specific score here, pull it up directly before deciding. Look at both the overall rating and how recent the reviews are, plus how the Zevi team responds to negative reviews - that's often a better signal of support quality than the star average alone.

How is Zevi different from Klevu or Algolia?

Zevi is built to be Shopify-native and quick to install, aimed at merchants who want AI search results without an implementation project. Klevu and Algolia are more powerful and more configurable, but they demand more setup work and typically cost more - they make sense once your catalog complexity or customization needs outgrow an out-of-the-box app.

Does Zevi help with AI search visibility outside my Shopify store, like ChatGPT or Google AI Overviews?

No - Zevi is an on-site search and merchandising tool, focused on the experience once a shopper is already on your storefront. It doesn't address how your products get surfaced in AI answer engines, AI shopping assistants, or other off-site discovery channels. If that's the gap you're trying to close, look at a platform built specifically for AI-visibility, such as Buffy, alongside whichever on-site search tool you choose.
