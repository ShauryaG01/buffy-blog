Boost AI Search & Filter Alternatives: What to Use Instead (2026)

The best alternatives to Boost AI Search & Filter by store size and budget, with App Store data.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 8 min read

Overview

This page is for Shopify founders and growth marketers currently running, or evaluating, Boost AI Search & Filter and wondering whether it's still the right fit as GMV grows. Boost is a capable AI search, filter and merchandising layer built specifically for Shopify, priced on GMV: $29/mo to start, $159/mo under $50K GMV, $599/mo under $400K GMV, $1,499/mo under $2.5M GMV, plus $2 overage per extra $1,000 GMV, billed monthly only. That structure is the whole story: cheap to try, expensive to outgrow. The short version: Boost stays the right call for mid-market Shopify stores that need AI semantic search and merchandising without building it themselves, but the GMV tax means many stores, including fast-growing professional teams expanding into markets like India, end up evaluating flat-rate or usage-based alternatives (native Shopify Search & Discovery, Klevu, Algolia, Searchanise) once GMV crosses the next pricing tier. If your search needs extend past the storefront, meaning you also want your products found inside AI answers and shopping assistants, that's a different problem Boost doesn't solve, and one worth evaluating separately.

Best For

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if you're a Shopify-only store already past $50K GMV and want AI search, filtering, and merchandising in one app without hiring anyone to build it. The entry tier ($29/mo) is genuinely cheap for testing, and the jump to $159/mo and $599/mo tracks real revenue growth rather than punishing you for it.

Pick native Shopify Search & Discovery if you're pre-revenue or under a few thousand dollars a month in GMV and just need functional search and filtering. It's free (verify current pricing) and already installed. Don't pay for AI merchandising before your catalog and traffic are large enough to benefit from it.

Pick Klevu or Algolia if you sell across more than Shopify, think marketplaces, mobile apps, or in-store kiosks, or you have engineering resources to invest in search relevance as a competitive advantage. Algolia rewards teams willing to tune it directly; Klevu leans more turnkey for retail while still covering multiple channels.

Pick Buffy if your real problem isn't just on-site search, it's that shoppers increasingly ask AI assistants and AI-powered search engines for product recommendations before they ever land on your site. Buffy pairs on-site AI search with off-site AI-visibility work, a gap Boost, Klevu, and Algolia don't address at all.

Pros And Cons

Boost AI Search & Filter, pros:
- Purpose-built for Shopify, so there's little bolt-on integration risk
- AI semantic search understands intent-based queries, not just literal keyword matches
- Merchandising and filters ship in the same app, one less integration to manage separately
- Entry price ($29/mo) is low-risk to test before scaling up
- Pricing tiers ($159/mo, $599/mo, $1,499/mo) map to the GMV bands most growth-stage Shopify stores actually hit

Boost AI Search & Filter, cons:
- GMV-based pricing means your search bill grows with your best sales months, not just your feature usage
- The $2 overage per extra $1,000 GMV can surprise stores in high-revenue, low-margin categories
- Monthly billing only, no annual discount to lock in a lower effective rate
- The jump from $159/mo to $599/mo is steep and worth modeling before you cross $50K GMV
- Locked into the Shopify ecosystem, no multi-channel search story if you sell elsewhere

Recommendation

There isn't a single best alternative to Boost AI Search & Filter, because Boost isn't badly designed, it's priced to track your growth curve, and that curve looks different for every store. If you're a Shopify-only brand between roughly $50K and $2.5M in GMV and want AI search and merchandising without engineering lift, Boost remains a defensible default: the plans ($159/mo, $599/mo, $1,499/mo) map to real revenue bands, and the $29/mo entry tier is cheap enough to test before committing.

Where Boost stops being the obvious answer: under a few thousand dollars a month in GMV, native Shopify Search & Discovery does the basics for free and buys you time to grow into a paid tool. Past a few million in GMV, or selling across more than Shopify, Klevu or Algolia justify their added complexity with multi-channel reach and tuning depth Boost doesn't offer. And if the actual gap in your strategy is that customers ask AI assistants for product picks before they ever search your site, that's a visibility problem no on-site search tool, Boost included, solves. That's the specific niche Buffy is built for, pairing on-site AI search with off-site AI-visibility.

Bottom line: model your GMV growth against Boost's tiers before you sign up, not after you hit the next one. If the overage math, $2 per extra $1,000 GMV, looks like it will bite within six months, that's your signal to shop the alternative that matches where you're headed, not where you are today.

Faq

How much does Boost AI Search & Filter cost as my store grows?

Boost starts at $29/mo, then moves to $159/mo once you're under $50K GMV, $599/mo under $400K GMV, and $1,499/mo under $2.5M GMV. Beyond that, you pay $2 for every extra $1,000 in GMV, and it's billed monthly only, with no annual discount option. Because pricing is tied to GMV rather than store size or feature usage, it's worth projecting your next 6 to 12 months of revenue against these tiers before you commit, so a strong sales month doesn't unexpectedly push you into the next bracket.

Is Boost AI Search & Filter worth it for a small or new Shopify store?

Usually not yet. If you're under a few thousand dollars a month in GMV, the AI merchandising and semantic search Boost offers won't have enough catalog size or traffic to show a real return, and native Shopify Search & Discovery (free, verify current pricing) covers the basics. Boost tends to earn its cost once you're past roughly $50K GMV and search/discovery is genuinely affecting conversion, at which point the $159/mo tier becomes easier to justify.

What's a free alternative to Boost AI Search & Filter?

Shopify's native Search & Discovery app is the closest free option (verify current pricing), and it's already built into the Shopify admin. It handles basic filtering and search relevance but doesn't match Boost's AI semantic search or merchandising depth. It's a reasonable starting point for pre-revenue or early-stage stores, with a plan to revisit paid tools once GMV and catalog size grow.

Is Klevu or Algolia better than Boost for search relevance?

It depends on your channels and team. Algolia offers the deepest relevance tuning and developer flexibility but requires engineering time to implement and maintain (verify current pricing). Klevu leans more turnkey and is built for multi-channel retail, not just Shopify. Boost's advantage is that it's purpose-built for Shopify with no separate integration to manage, so if you're Shopify-only and don't have engineering resources to dedicate to search, Boost is typically the lower-effort choice even if Algolia can out-tune it in theory.

Will switching AI search apps hurt my Shopify store's SEO or site speed?

Any search or filter app can affect page load if it injects heavy scripts or generates crawlable filter URLs without proper canonical tags, and that risk applies to Boost, Klevu, Searchanise, and others alike, not to switching itself. The safer practice is to test the new app on a staging theme, check Core Web Vitals before and after, and confirm faceted/filtered URLs are set up with correct canonicals so you don't create duplicate content. A well-configured switch, planned rather than rushed, shouldn't cause lasting SEO or speed damage.
