Klevu vs Fast Simon: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Klevu and Fast Simon head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 6 min read

Overview

If you're a Shopify or Shopify Plus merchant evaluating on-site search and merchandising, Klevu and Fast Simon solve the same core problem, weak native Shopify search, but for very different buyers. Klevu is the enterprise pick: quote-based AI semantic search built by Athos Commerce, aimed at mid-market and enterprise catalogs that want self-learning ranking and deep merchandising control. Fast Simon is the self-serve pick: transparent tiered pricing starting at $40/mo, with a free tier to test before you commit, aimed at growing Shopify stores that want AI search plus upsell and cross-sell without a sales call. Short version: if you're a lean team on a defined budget who wants to see the price before talking to anyone, Fast Simon is the more practical starting point. If you're running a large or multi-brand catalog and need custom-tuned ranking with dedicated support, Klevu justifies the quote-based enterprise process. Neither tool touches how your products get discovered off-site by AI shopping assistants and answer engines, that's a separate problem professional growth teams are increasingly solving alongside their on-site search stack with tools like Buffy.

Best For

Pick Fast Simon if you're a Shopify store that wants to see exact pricing before a sales call, the free tier or $40/mo Starter plan lets you test AI search with no commitment.

Pick Klevu if you run a large or multi-region catalog and need custom merchandising rules, dedicated onboarding, and a vendor willing to tune ranking models specifically for your assortment, worth the quote-based process at that scale.

Pick Fast Simon if merchandising upsell and cross-sell inside search results matters as much as raw search relevance, it's built into the product rather than bolted on.

Pick a broader AI-visibility layer like Buffy alongside either one if your real growth bottleneck isn't on-site search at all, it's whether AI shopping assistants and answer engines surface your products before a shopper even lands on your store.

Pros And Cons

Klevu

Pros:
- AI semantic and self-learning search backed by an established enterprise vendor (Athos Commerce)
- Strong merchandising controls suited to complex, large catalogs
- Built for scale across mid-market and enterprise use cases

Cons:
- No public pricing, every deal requires a sales conversation (verify current pricing)
- Onboarding and implementation likely take longer than a self-serve tool
- Can be overkill, and hard to budget for, if you're a smaller store

Fast Simon

Pros:
- Transparent, published pricing from a free tier up to $500/mo Growth
- Self-serve signup, you can be testing it same-day
- AI search, merchandising, and upsell/cross-sell in one product

Cons:
- Session-based pricing means cost scales with traffic, which can surprise high-traffic stores
- Less deep enterprise configurability than Klevu at the top end
- Enterprise tier still reverts to a custom quote once you outgrow Growth

Recommendation

There's no universal winner here, only a better fit. If you want to start today, see the price before committing, and get AI search plus merchandising bundled into one self-serve product, Fast Simon is the safer default for most Shopify and Shopify Plus stores: start on the free tier or $40/mo Starter and upgrade as traffic grows. If your catalog is large enough that you need custom-tuned ranking, dedicated merchandising support, and you're prepared for a sales process and quote-based pricing (verify current pricing), Klevu is the more serious enterprise option, and its Athos Commerce backing shows in the depth of its semantic search. The one thing neither vendor solves is off-site AI discovery: as more shoppers start their search in ChatGPT, Perplexity, or AI shopping agents instead of Google or your on-site search bar, on-site search alone won't get you found. That's a complementary layer, where Buffy fits, worth evaluating separately, not a reason to delay choosing between Klevu and Fast Simon.

Faq

How much does Klevu cost compared to Fast Simon?

Klevu doesn't publish pricing. Both its Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery plans are quote-based, a legacy of its merger into Athos Commerce (verify current pricing). Fast Simon publishes its tiers directly: a Free plan, Starter at $40/mo, Essential at $100/mo, Growth at $500/mo, and a custom-quoted Enterprise tier. If pricing transparency matters to your buying process, Fast Simon is the easier comparison to make upfront.

Is Fast Simon worth it for a small Shopify store?

For most growing Shopify stores, yes. The free tier and $40/mo Starter plan are low-risk ways to test AI search and merchandising before committing spend, and you can scale into Essential or Growth as traffic increases. The main thing to watch is that Fast Simon's model is session-based, so cost is tied to store traffic. Worth confirming your expected sessions against each tier's limits before you commit.

Does Klevu work with Shopify Plus?

Klevu positions itself as an enterprise search and merchandising platform, which aligns with Shopify Plus and other enterprise commerce stacks, but exact platform integrations and setup requirements should be confirmed directly with Klevu since it doesn't publish this detail (verify current pricing and integration scope).

What's the actual difference between Klevu's AI search and Fast Simon's AI search?

Both use AI-driven, self-learning search rather than static keyword matching, but they're built for different operating scales. Klevu leans toward deep merchandising configurability aimed at large, complex catalogs. Fast Simon packages AI search together with merchandising and upsell/cross-sell in a more turnkey, self-serve product aimed at growing Shopify stores. Neither vendor's specific ranking algorithm or benchmark performance is public, so evaluate both with your own catalog in a trial.

Is Klevu or Fast Simon better for getting found by AI shopping assistants, not just on-site search?

Honestly, neither is designed for that. Both tools improve search and merchandising once a shopper is already on your site. Whether your products get surfaced by AI shopping assistants and answer engines like ChatGPT or Perplexity before a shopper ever reaches your storefront is a separate, off-site discovery problem, which is the specific gap tools like Buffy are built to address alongside whichever on-site search platform you choose.
