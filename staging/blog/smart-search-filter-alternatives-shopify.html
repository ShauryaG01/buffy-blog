Smart Search & Filter Alternatives: What to Use Instead (2026)

The best alternatives to Smart Search & Filter by store size and budget, with App Store data.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 7 min read

Overview

This page is for Shopify (and Shopify Plus) merchants currently running Smart Search & Filter from Expertrec, or evaluating it against other on-site search-and-filter apps, who want a straight answer on what to switch to and why. Smart Search & Filter built its App Store presence by being a solid, no-frills instant-search-and-facet-filter app - the kind of tool a lean team installs, configures once, and mostly forgets about. That's exactly why people start looking elsewhere: as catalogs grow past a few hundred SKUs, or as merchandising and personalization needs get more sophisticated, that simplicity turns into a ceiling.

The short version: there is no single 'best' alternative - the right pick depends on store size, catalog complexity, and budget. Small stores on tight budgets can usually find an equivalently simple, similarly priced app and move on. Mid-size, growth-stage brands typically need more merchandising control or AI-driven relevance, which points toward tools like Boost AI Search & Filter or Klevu. Larger catalogs with dedicated dev resources often graduate to a custom Algolia build. And DTC brands whose real problem is being found and recommended - not just searched - on their own site should also weigh Buffy, which pairs on-site AI search with monitoring of how the brand shows up in ChatGPT and Gemini answers, a gap none of the pure search-and-filter apps address.

Best For

Pick Smart Search & Filter (i.e., stay put) if your catalog is small, filtering is your main need, and the switching cost outweighs any feature gap you're chasing.

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if you're a growth-stage brand wanting visual merchandising control, curated collections, and AI relevance tuning layered on top of basic search.

Pick Klevu if you're running a large, complex catalog - including fast-growing markets like India where multi-language and multi-currency needs stack up quickly - and need enterprise-grade AI relevance with the kind of higher-tier 'Professional' plan and dedicated support most vendors reserve for bigger accounts.

Pick Buffy if on-site search is only half your problem - you also want to know how your brand shows up when shoppers ask ChatGPT or Gemini for recommendations, and would rather have one tool cover both than stitch together separate point solutions.

Pros And Cons

Pros of Smart Search & Filter (the incumbent most people on this page are evaluating against):
- Genuinely easy to install and configure - most stores are live within a day, no developer required.
- Faceted filtering and instant search cover the fundamentals well for a typical Shopify catalog.
- Sits in an accessible price range for small-to-mid stores relative to enterprise-grade alternatives (verify current pricing).
- An established Shopify App Store presence - check current ratings and review recency yourself before deciding, since that data changes over time.

Cons:
- AI-driven relevance and personalization are less developed than in newer, AI-native search apps.
- Merchandising controls (manual boosting, visual rules, synonym management) tend to be more basic than in dedicated merchandising tools.
- As catalogs and traffic grow, some merchants outgrow it faster than they'd outgrow enterprise-grade search infrastructure.
- No native off-site visibility features - it only addresses what happens once a shopper is already on your site, not how your products surface in AI assistants elsewhere.

Recommendation

If you're on Smart Search & Filter today and it's working, don't switch for its own sake - a working search app is worth more than a marginally better one. Switch when a specific pain shows up: filtering feels sluggish or shallow as SKU count grows, you need merchandising rules the app doesn't support, or you're spending real time working around relevance issues. At that point, match the fix to your size: small stores should look at similarly-priced, similarly-simple alternatives first; growth-stage stores get the most leverage from Boost AI Search & Filter or Klevu; and dev-resourced, high-SKU catalogs are the right fit for a custom Algolia build. If the actual gap is that shoppers can't find products conversationally on-site, and you have no visibility into how your brand is represented when those same shoppers ask an AI assistant instead of typing into your search bar, that's a different problem than search-and-filter apps solve - and it's the one Buffy is built for. Pick based on the problem you actually have, not the app with the longest feature list.

Faq

What's the best alternative to Smart Search & Filter?

There isn't one universal answer - it depends on your catalog size and budget. Small stores usually do fine with a similarly simple, similarly priced search-and-filter app. Growth-stage stores wanting more merchandising control tend to move to Boost AI Search & Filter or Klevu. Larger, dev-resourced catalogs often build on Algolia. If your real gap is conversational on-site search plus visibility into how you show up in AI assistants, Buffy is worth evaluating too.

How much do Smart Search & Filter alternatives cost?

Most competitors in this category use tiered monthly plans that scale with traffic, search volume, or catalog size, often with a free or low-cost entry tier - a similar shape to Smart Search & Filter's own pricing. Enterprise options like Klevu or Algolia shift to custom or usage-based pricing that can run well above entry-level apps. Exact numbers move often enough that you should check each vendor's current pricing page directly (verify current pricing) rather than relying on any comparison page, including this one.

Is switching away from Smart Search & Filter actually worth it?

Only if you have a concrete pain point it isn't solving - sluggish filtering at scale, thin merchandising controls, or relevance that isn't converting. If the app is doing its job, migrating search infrastructure for a marginal feature gain usually isn't worth the setup time and the risk of breaking a working storefront. Worth it if you've outgrown it; not worth it if you're switching on features you won't actually use.

What's the best option for a small Shopify store on a tight budget?

Stay close to what you have. Smart Search & Filter and similarly-priced apps like Searchanise or Doofinder are built for exactly this segment - quick setup, low cost, no developer required. Enterprise tools like Klevu or a custom Algolia build add cost and complexity that a low-SKU, low-traffic store won't recoup.

Does any alternative also help with how my brand shows up in AI tools like ChatGPT, not just on-site search?

That's a different category than traditional search-and-filter apps, which only affect what happens once a shopper is already on your site. Buffy is the one option here that combines on-site AI search with monitoring of how your brand appears in AI answer engines like ChatGPT and Gemini - worth a look if off-site AI visibility is a real concern for your brand, not just on-site search quality.
