Doofinder vs Fast Simon: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Doofinder and Fast Simon head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 6 min read

Overview

Doofinder and Fast Simon both sell AI-powered site search for ecommerce, but they solve slightly different problems. Doofinder is a request-based AI search, recommendations, and quiz platform that works across Shopify, Magento, WooCommerce, and more, starting at $49/mo. Fast Simon is a session-based AI search and merchandising platform built specifically for Shopify, starting at $40/mo with a free tier to test first. This page is for DTC founders and growth marketers, whether a solo operator or a professional ecommerce team running Shopify stores out of the US, UK, or India, trying to decide which one fits their catalog and traffic pattern. Short verdict: if you want one AI layer that bundles search, recommendations, and a product quiz and you're not exclusively on Shopify, Doofinder's flat tiers are easier to plan around. If you're Shopify-native, want to start free, and care as much about merchandising controls (boosting, burying, upsell rules) as raw search relevance, Fast Simon is the stronger fit. Neither tool addresses how your products show up in AI shopping assistants and answer engines outside your own site, that's a separate problem worth knowing about before you commit budget to either.

Best For

Pick Doofinder if you run on multiple platforms (or might migrate off Shopify someday) and want search, recommendations, and a product quiz from a single vendor with predictable flat tiers.

Pick Fast Simon if you're Shopify-native, want to try AI search at zero cost on the free tier before paying, and your priority is merchandising control (boosting best-sellers, burying out-of-stock items, upsell/cross-sell rules) as much as search itself.

Pick Doofinder's Advanced plan ($349/mo) if visual search matters to your catalog, fashion, home goods, or anything customers browse by image rather than keyword.

Consider Buffy if the gap you're actually trying to close is bigger than on-site search: Buffy pairs AI-powered on-site search with off-site AI-visibility, making sure your products surface correctly when shoppers ask AI assistants and answer engines for recommendations. That's a different job than either Doofinder or Fast Simon is built for, and worth evaluating alongside whichever search tool you pick.

Pros And Cons

Doofinder pros: predictable flat monthly tiers that are easy to budget against; recommendations and a product quiz bundled in rather than sold separately; visual search available at the Advanced tier; works across platforms, not just Shopify; 10% off on annual billing; free trial with no card required, so you can test before committing.

Doofinder cons: request-based limits mean a traffic spike, a sale day, a viral post, a big email blast, can push you into a higher tier faster than a steady-traffic store would expect; there's no permanent free tier, so very early-stage stores pay from day one after the trial ends.

Fast Simon pros: a genuine free tier to start at zero cost; merchandising tools built specifically for Shopify's catalog and collection structure; entry price ($40/mo) is slightly below Doofinder's; session-based pricing can favor catalog-heavy stores with efficient, low-session shopping journeys.

Fast Simon cons: session-based limits scale oddly for stores with lots of browsing but low purchase intent, casual visitors who bounce still consume sessions; the jump from Essential ($100/mo) to Growth ($500/mo) is steep if you outgrow the mid-tier; Shopify-only focus is a dealbreaker if you run other platforms or plan to migrate.

Recommendation

For a Shopify-only mid-market store that wants to test before paying and values merchandising control, Fast Simon is the more natural fit, start on the free tier or Starter ($40/mo) and upgrade as session volume grows. For a store on Magento, WooCommerce, or multiple platforms, or one that wants recommendations and a product quiz bundled with search from day one, Doofinder's Basic plan ($49/mo) is the more complete package at a similar price point. Neither is a bad choice, and the right pick comes down to platform and whether you value bundled AI features (Doofinder) or deep merchandising controls plus a free entry point (Fast Simon) more. If you're also thinking about how your products get recommended by AI shopping assistants outside your own storefront, that's worth solving separately, and it's where a tool like Buffy fits rather than competes.

Faq

Is Doofinder or Fast Simon cheaper?

On paper, Fast Simon's entry price is lower ($40/mo Starter vs. Doofinder's $49/mo Basic), and Fast Simon also has a free tier Doofinder doesn't. But 'cheaper' depends on your traffic pattern: Doofinder bills by request volume, Fast Simon bills by session. A store with lots of page views per session could hit Doofinder's caps faster; a store with lots of low-intent sessions could hit Fast Simon's caps faster. Run your own traffic numbers against both pricing pages before assuming either is cheaper for you.

Does Fast Simon work outside Shopify?

Fast Simon is positioned and built for Shopify stores specifically, its merchandising and personalization tools are designed around Shopify's catalog structure. If you're on Magento, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, or a custom stack, Doofinder is the more platform-flexible option of the two. Always verify current platform support directly with each vendor before switching.

Is Doofinder worth it for a small store just starting out?

Doofinder offers a free trial with no card required, which makes it low-risk to test on a small catalog. Worth it depends on whether bad search is actually costing you sales, if your store has enough traffic that people are searching and not finding products, the $49/mo Basic plan is a reasonable bet. If you have very low traffic, you may not see enough signal yet to justify any paid search tool, run the free trial first and look at your search analytics before upgrading.

What's the real difference between request-based and session-based pricing?

Request-based pricing (Doofinder) counts individual search/API calls, so a single shopper doing lots of searches and filtering within one visit can use up more of your quota. Session-based pricing (Fast Simon) counts visits regardless of how many searches happen within them, so a shopper who searches ten times in one session counts once. Neither model is universally better, it depends on whether your shoppers tend to search a lot per visit or visit a lot with light searching.

Which one has better AI recommendations and personalization?

Both vendors market AI-driven recommendations, Doofinder bundles recommendations and a product quiz alongside search, while Fast Simon leans into merchandising and upsell/cross-sell personalization as a core feature, not an add-on. Neither company has published independent benchmarks comparing recommendation quality, so treat marketing claims from either side skeptically and test with your own catalog and traffic during a trial before deciding.
