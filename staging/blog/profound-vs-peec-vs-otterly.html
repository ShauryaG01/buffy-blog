Profound vs Peec vs Otterly: AI Visibility Tools Head-to-Head

The three best-known AI visibility platforms compared on models tracked, refresh cadence, and price.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 7 min read

Overview

If you're a DTC founder or growth marketer trying to figure out how visible your brand is inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews, Profound, Peec AI, and Otterly AI are the three names you'll keep running into. All three do the same core job - track how often and how favorably AI answer engines mention your brand versus competitors - but they're built for different budgets and different teams, which matters more than any feature checklist.

Short version: Profound is the enterprise pick - the deepest analytics, SOC2/SSO for procurement, and Profound Agents, but its $99/mo Starter plan only tracks ChatGPT, so real multi-engine coverage means paying for Growth at $399/mo. Peec AI is the agency and SEO-team pick - daily tracking, unlimited seats on every plan, and strong multi-project management starting at $95/mo. Otterly AI is the budget pick - genuine multi-engine monitoring from $29/mo, well suited to a solo founder or lean team that wants the visibility signal without seat limits or an enterprise sales cycle.

Worth knowing upfront: none of these three tools touch your actual website. They're monitoring and analytics platforms, full stop. If you're a DTC brand that also wants your product pages and content to be easier for AI engines to find and cite in the first place, that's a separate job - one Buffy focuses on from the on-site side - and it pairs with any of these three rather than competing with them.

Best For

Pick Profound if you're an enterprise or larger mid-market team that needs board-ready reporting, SOC2/SSO for procurement, and conversation-level analytics - and you have budget past the $99/mo entry tier (ChatGPT-only) to reach the $399/mo Growth plan or custom Enterprise pricing for real multi-engine coverage.

Pick Peec AI if you're an SEO agency or in-house team - including professional marketing teams managing clients across the US, UK, or India - juggling several brand or client projects and want daily-refresh tracking across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews with unlimited seats baked into every plan, starting at $95/mo.

Pick Otterly AI if you're a solo founder, small DTC team, or anyone testing whether AI-visibility monitoring is worth paying for at all - Lite starts at $29/mo with genuine multi-engine tracking, though check the prompt-count limits before you commit.

Pick Buffy alongside any of the three if tracking your AI visibility isn't actually the goal - improving it is. These platforms tell you how you're showing up in AI answers; Buffy works on the on-site side (product pages, content, structured data) to make your brand easier for those same engines to find and cite in the first place.

Recommendation

There's no universal winner here - the right tool depends on team size and budget, not features.

If you're optimizing for price and multi-engine breadth per dollar, Otterly AI wins for most early-stage DTC brands: $29/mo buys real coverage across four AI engines, which is hard to beat at that price. If you're an agency or in-house SEO team managing multiple brands and want daily refresh plus unlimited seats without per-user pricing games, Peec AI at $95/mo is the more mature workflow tool. If you're generating enough AI-driven traffic value to justify enterprise reporting, SSO, and deeper conversation analytics to a CMO or board, Profound earns its premium - just budget for Growth ($399/mo), since the $99/mo Starter tier is ChatGPT-only and will undersell the category to stakeholders.

One caveat applies to all three: none of them change your actual visibility - they measure it. If your product pages, FAQs, and content aren't structured for AI engines to parse and cite, no amount of monitoring moves the needle. That's the gap Buffy is built to close on the on-site side, and it's a reasonable pairing with whichever of these three fits your monitoring budget.

Faq

What's the cheapest way to start tracking AI visibility?

Otterly AI's Lite plan at $29/mo is the lowest entry price among the three, and unlike a lot of budget monitoring tools it covers four engines out of the gate (ChatGPT, AI Overviews, Perplexity, Copilot) rather than just one. Peec AI's Starter plan ($95/mo) and Profound's Starter plan ($99/mo) both cost more, and Profound's entry tier only tracks ChatGPT - you'd need Growth ($399/mo) for broader coverage.

Is Profound worth the price compared to Peec or Otterly?

It depends on team size, not preference. For enterprise teams that need SOC2/SSO and conversation-level analytics to report up to a board, Profound is worth it - but only from the Growth plan ($399/mo) up, since Starter ($99/mo) is ChatGPT-only. For a DTC brand under a few million in revenue, Peec ($95/mo) or Otterly ($29/mo) likely deliver comparable day-to-day signal at a fraction of the cost.

Which tool tracks the most AI engines out of the box?

Otterly AI's entry tier covers the most without an add-on: ChatGPT, AI Overviews, Perplexity, and Copilot. Peec AI covers three (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) with a paid add-on for a 4th engine like Gemini. Profound's entry Starter plan tracks ChatGPT only, expanding at higher tiers.

Do Profound, Peec, or Otterly help me actually rank higher in AI answers, or just monitor it?

Just monitor it. All three are analytics platforms - none of them edit, optimize, or publish content on your site. If you want the other half of AI search, making your product pages, specs, and FAQs easier for AI engines to cite in the first place, that's a separate category (Buffy focuses on this on-site side) and it's meant to be used alongside a monitoring tool like these, not instead of one.

Can I switch between these tools later, or am I locked in once I pick one?

Switching is low-risk - none of these tools touch your website's code or content, so there's nothing to migrate on your side. You'll lose the historical trend data specific to that platform, but a common path is starting with the cheapest option (Otterly, $29/mo) to validate that AI-visibility monitoring is worth tracking, then moving up to Peec or Profound later if you need deeper analytics, more engines, or enterprise features.
