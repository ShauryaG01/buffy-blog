Gorgias Alternatives: What to Use Instead (2026)

The best alternatives to Gorgias by store size and budget, with App Store data.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 7 min read

Overview

If you're weighing Gorgias alternatives and trying to figure out what to use instead in 2026, here's the short version up front. Gorgias built its reputation as the customer-support helpdesk made for Shopify and BigCommerce brands - ticketing, live chat, and automation rules that pull order and shipping data straight into the conversation. That's still true, and it's still the default pick for a lot of DTC teams. But it isn't the only answer, and it isn't the right answer for everyone.

If you're a growing Shopify brand that needs order-aware automation and can absorb cost as ticket volume scales, Gorgias remains a solid, defensible choice. If you're earlier-stage and cost-sensitive, general helpdesks like Freshdesk or Help Scout cover the ticketing basics for less. If you want something ecommerce-specific but priced or built differently than Gorgias, Richpanel and Reamaze are the closest like-for-like swaps. If you've outgrown ecommerce-only tools - multiple brands, channels, or a bigger support org - Zendesk or Intercom scale further, at enterprise cost and complexity.

One thing worth separating out before you shop: if the real frustration isn't ticket volume but customers not finding the right product on-site, or not showing up when someone asks an AI assistant for a recommendation, that's a discovery problem, not a helpdesk problem - no tool on this list, including Gorgias, solves that.

Best For

Pick Gorgias if you're a Shopify-first (or BigCommerce) brand doing real order volume and want automations built around order and shipping data out of the box - it's still the most ecommerce-native option here.

Pick Freshdesk or Help Scout if you're pre- or early-revenue and need dependable ticketing without paying for ecommerce features you're not using yet. Both are cheaper to start with and simpler to configure.

Pick Richpanel or Reamaze if you like what Gorgias does but want a different pricing curve or a lighter self-service/marketing bundle - they're the closest direct swaps for a DTC support use case.

Pick Zendesk or Intercom if you've outgrown ecommerce-only tools - multiple brands or channels, a bigger support org, or a need for proactive lifecycle messaging beyond ticket resolution - and can absorb the added cost and setup work.

One more case worth naming honestly: if the actual bottleneck isn't your support queue but weak on-site product search, or your products not surfacing when shoppers ask AI tools like ChatGPT or Perplexity for recommendations, none of the above fixes that. That's a different job than a helpdesk - it's the gap a tool like Buffy, built for on-site AI search and off-site AI-visibility, is meant to address, and it's worth solving as its own problem rather than expecting a support-tool swap to cover it.

Pros And Cons

Gorgias pros:
- Genuinely deep Shopify/BigCommerce integration - agents see order, subscription, and shipping details next to the ticket without hunting for them elsewhere.
- Automation rules are built around ecommerce scenarios (refunds, order status, subscription changes) rather than generic support templates.
- Email, live chat, and social channels (like Instagram/Facebook DMs) unify into one inbox, which matters once support spans more than email.

Gorgias cons:
- Pricing scales with tickets/conversations, so cost can rise faster than a founder expects as the brand grows - budget for where volume is headed, not just where it is now (verify current pricing).
- It's more tool than a very small or pre-PMF store typically needs; the ecommerce automation only pays for itself once volume is meaningful.
- Some teams report that rule-building and inbox configuration get more complex as automations accumulate, creating a maintenance tax for a lean support team.

Recommendation

There's no single 'best Gorgias alternative' - the right swap depends on why you're actually looking. If cost is the trigger and your support needs are still simple, Freshdesk or Help Scout will save money with little functional loss. If you like Gorgias's ecommerce-first approach but want a different price curve, Richpanel or Reamaze are the most direct replacements. If you've outgrown ecommerce-only tooling entirely, Zendesk or Intercom give you more room, at a real cost and complexity increase.

For most Shopify-native DTC brands still doing meaningful order volume, Gorgias remains defensible: it's not the cheapest option, but the order-data integration is hard to replicate cheaply elsewhere. Don't switch on price alone without confirming the alternative's automations actually reach into your order and shipping data the way Gorgias's do - that's usually where the real value sits, not the inbox UI.

And if what's actually broken is product discovery rather than support quality - shoppers not finding what they want on-site, or not showing up when AI assistants get asked for recommendations - treat that as its own problem. A helpdesk swap, Gorgias or otherwise, won't fix it.

Faq

How much does Gorgias cost compared to alternatives like Zendesk or Freshdesk?

Pricing models differ more than the raw numbers: Gorgias and Richpanel typically price by ticket or conversation volume, Zendesk and Help Scout price per agent seat, and Intercom often mixes seats with usage. In practice, Freshdesk and Help Scout tend to have the lowest entry cost, Zendesk and Intercom skew pricier at scale, and Gorgias/Richpanel/Reamaze sit in an ecommerce-focused middle range. Exact tiers change often, so check each vendor's current pricing page before deciding (verify current pricing) rather than relying on last year's numbers.

Is Gorgias worth it for a small Shopify store just starting out?

Not always. If your ticket volume is still low, Gorgias's order-aware automations don't have enough volume to pay off yet, and you may be paying for depth you don't need. Many early-stage stores do fine starting with a simpler, cheaper tool like Help Scout or Freshdesk and revisiting Gorgias once order volume and multichannel support (chat, social DMs, email) actually create the workload its automations are built for.

What's the best free or low-cost Gorgias alternative?

Freshdesk and Help Scout generally have the lowest entry cost among mainstream helpdesk tools, and most vendors in this space offer some kind of trial or entry tier. That said, 'free' or 'starter' tiers in this category are usually capped on seats, tickets, or features rather than being fully free at real usage - so verify what's actually included in the current free/low tier before assuming it covers your volume.

Which Gorgias alternative has the best AI features?

Most major players - Gorgias, Zendesk, Freshdesk, Intercom - now market some form of AI ticket summarization, suggested replies, or chatbot deflection. This part of the category moves fast enough that a specific 'best AI' claim goes stale quickly. Rather than trusting marketing copy, ask each vendor for a live demo using your own real tickets and judge accuracy and time saved directly.

Are there good Gorgias alternatives for teams outside the US, like professionals in India?

Yes - Gorgias, Zendesk, Freshdesk, and Richpanel are all usable by teams anywhere, including growing DTC and support teams based in India. What matters more than geography is the pricing model: per-agent-seat tools like Freshdesk or Help Scout tend to be more predictable for lean, cost-conscious teams than conversation-volume pricing, which can scale unpredictably as support volume grows. Weigh that against how much ecommerce-specific automation you actually need before choosing.
