AI Search Optimization: Ranking Inside ChatGPT, Gemini & Perplexity

The on-page, schema, and citation tactics that make AI search engines surface your brand.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 7 min read

Overview

AI Search Optimization (often called answer engine optimization, or AEO) is the practice of structuring your brand's on-page content, structured data, and off-site citations so that ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Perplexity can retrieve, understand, and cite you inside a generated answer. This is a different game than ranking on a results page: these tools don't hand back ten blue links, they synthesize one answer with a handful of citations, and if you're not in that citation list, your Google ranking doesn't matter - you're simply not part of the conversation. This matters right now because a growing share of product research ("best electrolyte mix for keto," "X vs Y skincare serum") is happening inside chat interfaces instead of a search bar. Analytics vendors like Similarweb, Semrush, and Adobe have all flagged AI-referral traffic as one of the fastest-growing new traffic sources since 2024, even though it's still a small slice of total traffic for most sites today. The good news: the mechanics that get you cited are learnable and largely within your control - clean structured data, answer-first content, crawlable pages for AI bots, and a base of third-party citations that these engines trust more than your own homepage copy ever will. This isn't unique to physical DTC products, either. A professional services firm in India trying to get recommended by ChatGPT for "best export compliance consultant" is working against the exact same retrieval mechanics as a supplement brand chasing "best electrolyte drink." Treat AI search as a new, addressable channel with its own rules, not a black box you can't influence.

What To Know

Each engine sources answers differently, and the differences dictate your tactics. ChatGPT's search function blends its own crawler (OAI-SearchBot) with a Bing-index partnership, plus whatever it absorbed during training via GPTBot; it favors clearly structured, recently-updated pages and heavily weights Reddit, Wikipedia, and review aggregators. Perplexity crawls the live web on every query with PerplexityBot and shows its work - every claim gets a numbered footnote back to a source, so getting cited is a direct function of being crawlable and quotable. Gemini's answers (and Google's AI Overviews) are grounded primarily in Google's own search index, so classical technical SEO - crawlability, indexation, structured data - still gates whether you're even eligible to be pulled in; the Google-Extended token in robots.txt separately controls whether Google can use your content for model training, distinct from search grounding. Across all three, the underlying shift is from ranking to retrieval: the model doesn't rank your page, it decides whether a passage from your page answers the question well enough to quote. That makes a few concepts non-negotiable: entity clarity (does the model know what your brand is and does), structured data (schema.org markup that removes ambiguity), and third-party corroboration (models are trained to trust independent confirmation over brand-authored claims, so a mention on a review site or forum often outweighs a claim on your own product page). E-E-A-T signals - author expertise, citations, consistent facts across the web - still matter because they're the same signals these models use to judge source credibility.

Step By Step

  1. Audit your current AI visibility. Ask ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity your top 10-15 category questions ("best [category] for [use case]") and record whether you're mentioned, misdescribed, or absent.
  2. Check your robots.txt. Confirm you're not blocking GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, or Google-Extended - many sites block these by accident via broad crawler rules meant for scrapers.
  3. Add an llms.txt file at your root domain. This emerging standard gives AI crawlers a clean, prioritized summary of what your site is and which pages matter most.
  4. Implement structured data on every commerce-relevant page: Product, Offer, AggregateRating, Organization, FAQPage, and BreadcrumbList schema. This removes ambiguity about price, availability, and identity that models otherwise have to guess at.
  5. Rewrite key pages answer-first. Open comparison, ingredient, and "how it works" pages with a direct 2-3 sentence answer before the narrative - this is the passage models lift and quote.
  6. Build a real FAQ layer with FAQPage schema, answering the exact questions you found in step 1 in plain, quotable language.
  7. Strengthen entity signals off-site: a complete Wikidata entry, consistent business info across Crunchbase, LinkedIn, and directories, and a Wikipedia presence if you qualify - these are reference points models use to disambiguate your brand.
  8. Earn third-party citations deliberately. Pitch reviewers, get into "best of" roundups, and participate authentically in relevant Reddit and forum threads, since these are exactly the sources Perplexity and ChatGPT lean on for corroboration.
  9. Monitor and iterate monthly. Re-run your audit prompts, track which pages get cited, and adjust content and schema accordingly - a tool like Buffy can automate this tracking across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity so you're not manually re-asking prompts every cycle.

Common Mistakes

Blocking AI crawlers without realizing it: security plugins and generic "block bad bots" robots.txt rules often catch GPTBot, PerplexityBot, and ClaudeBot alongside real scrapers, quietly making your entire site invisible to AI search. Audit your robots.txt explicitly for these user agents, don't assume a generic rule is safe.

Treating AI search optimization as a one-time project: these engines re-crawl and re-index constantly, and citation patterns shift with every model update. Brands that optimize once and move on lose visibility within months.

Over-indexing on owned content: publishing more blog posts about yourself doesn't move the needle if there's no independent corroboration. Models are trained to discount self-interested claims, so a page that only cites itself is weaker than one small mention on a trusted third-party review site.

Burying the answer: long, SEO-padded intros before the actual answer make it harder for a model to extract a clean, quotable passage. If a human has to scroll to find your answer, so does the retrieval system.

Stuffing schema with unverifiable claims: adding AggregateRating or Review schema with inflated or fake data is both a Google Search Essentials violation and a fast way to get flagged by AI systems that increasingly cross-check structured data against visible page content.

Assuming Gemini equals classic Google SEO: because Gemini grounds heavily in Google's index, some teams assume nothing else is needed. But grounding into an answer is a separate eligibility gate from ranking - a page can rank on page one and still never get pulled into an AI Overview if it isn't structured for extraction.

Ignoring category forums and Reddit: dismissing these as "low value" ignores that Perplexity and ChatGPT actively cite them as primary sources for comparison and recommendation queries.

Faq

How do I get my brand mentioned in ChatGPT answers?

Make sure OAI-SearchBot and GPTBot aren't blocked in robots.txt, add clear structured data (Product, Organization, FAQPage) to your key pages, and build up independent mentions on review sites, Reddit, and comparison articles - ChatGPT's search leans heavily on corroborated third-party sources rather than brand-authored claims alone.

Does Google Gemini use the same ranking factors as Google Search?

Mostly, yes - Gemini's grounded answers and Google's AI Overviews draw primarily from Google's existing search index, so crawlability, indexation, and structured data still gate whether you're eligible to appear. But ranking well doesn't guarantee you get pulled into a generated answer; that also depends on whether a passage on your page directly and clearly answers the query.

What is llms.txt and do I need one?

llms.txt is an emerging standard, hosted at your domain root, that gives AI crawlers a concise, prioritized map of what your site covers and which pages matter most. It's not yet universally adopted by every AI engine, but it's low-effort to add and helps crawlers understand your site faster and more accurately than parsing your full sitemap.

How does Perplexity decide which sources to cite?

Perplexity crawls the live web per query via PerplexityBot and prioritizes sources that directly and concisely answer the question, are recently updated, and are corroborated elsewhere on the web. Pages with a clear, quotable answer near the top and confirmed by independent sites (reviews, forums, publishers) get cited far more often than self-promotional pages with no outside corroboration.

Do I need structured data or schema markup for AI search optimization?

Yes - structured data is one of the highest-leverage tactics available because it removes ambiguity for the model. Product, Offer, AggregateRating, Organization, and FAQPage schema tell an AI engine exactly what you sell, what it costs, and what questions you answer, rather than forcing it to infer that from unstructured page copy.
