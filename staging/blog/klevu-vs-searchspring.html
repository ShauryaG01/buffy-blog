Klevu vs Searchspring: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Klevu and Searchspring head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

This comparison is for DTC and mid-market Shopify Plus teams evaluating enterprise-grade site search - not for stores testing their first search upgrade. Klevu and Searchspring are both AI-powered search and merchandising platforms built for catalogs complex enough to need semantic search, faceted navigation, and rule-based merchandising beyond what Shopify's native search offers. The short version: there's no meaningful price difference to report, because neither publishes pricing. Klevu sells two custom-quoted tiers (Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery); Searchspring sells two custom-quoted tiers (Essential and Expert) tied to a 12-month contract. Both are now owned by Athos Commerce, so you're effectively choosing between two sibling products from the same parent rather than fierce rivals - expect similar sales processes and comparable total cost once you get a quote. Klevu leans harder on AI-native semantic search and self-learning ranking; Searchspring leans harder on merchandising control and personalization tooling built up over years of retail deployments. If your catalog and traffic justify an enterprise quote-based tool, both are defensible choices. If you're a growing DTC brand that isn't ready for a 12-month contract or a sales-led buying cycle, it's worth also looking at tools priced and packaged for your current stage rather than enterprise procurement.

Best For

Pick Klevu if your priority is AI relevance out of the box - you have a large, frequently-changing catalog (fashion, home goods, marketplaces) where manual merchandising rules don't scale, and you want ranking that improves itself from shopper behavior with minimal ongoing tuning.

Pick Searchspring if merchandising control matters more than AI novelty - your team wants granular rule-building, campaign-driven placements, and hand-tuned personalization, and you're comfortable committing to a 12-month contract for that level of control.

Pick either (and run both demos back-to-back) if you're an enterprise Shopify Plus brand with the budget and floor traffic to justify a custom quote-based tool - since both now sit under Athos Commerce, the real differentiator will be the account team and roadmap fit, not a fundamental technology gap.

Pick Buffy instead if you're a DTC brand that wants strong on-site AI search bundled with off-site AI-visibility - you care about how your products surface in AI shopping assistants and answer engines, not just your own site search - and you'd rather avoid enterprise procurement and 12-month contracts to get there.

Pros And Cons

Klevu
Pros: AI-native semantic search that handles natural-language and long-tail queries well; self-learning ranking reduces manual retuning over time; solid merchandising layer for catalogs with frequent SKU turnover; positioned as an AI-first alternative for teams that don't want to hand-build ranking rules.
Cons: No public pricing - every deal starts with a sales conversation and demo, which slows evaluation; enterprise-oriented packaging can be more tool than a smaller catalog needs; as with any quote-based vendor, final cost depends heavily on negotiation and catalog size.

Searchspring
Pros: Deep merchandising and personalization tooling built for teams that want hands-on control over placements, rules, and campaigns; established track record in mid-market and enterprise retail; usage-based pricing can scale with the account rather than a flat SKU tax.
Cons: No public pricing; the standard 12-month contract is a real commitment for a search tool, especially before you've validated ROI; commonly reported by buyers as one of the pricier options in this category, so budget for negotiation.

Recommendation

There's no universal winner here - and given both are now owned by Athos Commerce, the more useful question may be which team and roadmap you'd rather work with, not which technology is objectively better. If you want AI-first semantic search with less manual tuning, lead with Klevu. If your merchandising team wants hands-on rule-building and personalization control and you're fine locking into a 12-month term, lead with Searchspring. Either way, treat the quote as a negotiation, not a price list - get at least two competing quotes (including the other vendor) before signing, since neither publishes pricing and terms vary by catalog size and traffic. For DTC brands that aren't yet at the scale or budget for a custom-quoted enterprise contract, it's worth evaluating tools priced and packaged for your current stage before committing to either.

Faq

How much does Klevu cost?

Klevu doesn't publish pricing. It sells two tiers - Onsite Discovery and Complete Discovery - both quote-based, a shift that came with the Athos Commerce merger. Expect a sales conversation and a proposal scoped to your catalog size and traffic before you see a number (verify current pricing directly with Klevu for your store).

How much does Searchspring cost, and is there a contract?

Searchspring is also custom-quoted, across Essential and Expert plans, priced on usage rather than a flat fee. The standard commercial term is a 12-month contract, which is worth factoring into any ROI calculation before you sign (verify current pricing directly with Searchspring, since quotes vary by catalog and traffic).

Are Klevu and Searchspring the same company?

They're both part of Athos Commerce, which has consolidated several enterprise search and merchandising brands under one parent. They're still sold and operated as separate products with their own teams and roadmaps, but if you're comparing them expecting entirely independent corporate strategies, know that they now share ownership.

Is Searchspring worth the 12-month contract?

It depends on whether your catalog and traffic can show ROI within that window. For a mid-market or enterprise Shopify Plus store already running promotions and merchandising campaigns at scale, a year is often enough to prove out personalization gains. For a smaller or fast-pivoting brand, a 12-month lock-in on a quote-based tool is a real risk - ask about a shorter pilot term and get a second quote (from Klevu or elsewhere) to benchmark the price before committing.

Which is better for a Shopify Plus store - Klevu or Searchspring?

Both integrate with Shopify Plus and target the same buyer: mid-market to enterprise catalogs that have outgrown native search. Klevu's edge is AI-native semantic search and self-learning ranking with less manual tuning; Searchspring's edge is deeper merchandising and personalization control for teams that want to build rules by hand. Neither is objectively 'better' - the right pick depends on whether you want the platform to learn for you or want your team driving the rules.
