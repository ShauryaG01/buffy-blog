Searchanise vs Findify: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Searchanise and Findify head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 5 min read

Overview

Searchanise vs Findify: Shopify Search Compared (2026) comes down to one variable: budget matched to catalog size. Searchanise starts on a free plan and scales from $19/mo by product count, which makes it the default pick for small to mid-size Shopify stores that want fast, accurate search and unlimited filtering without enterprise overhead. Findify starts at $499/mo (Premium) and moves up to $799/mo (Professional) and $1,199/mo (Enterprise), built for mid-market and enterprise Shopify brands that need deep on-site personalization, smart collections, and recommendation engines tied to a real merchandising strategy. If you're a growth marketer in India or any lean team running a growing catalog, Searchanise's free tier and $19/mo entry point removes most of the pricing risk. If you're managing a high-SKU catalog with a dedicated growth team where personalization is a proven revenue lever rather than a nice-to-have, Findify's Professional and Enterprise tiers can justify the spend. Short version: match the tool to your catalog size and personalization ambition, not the other way around. Most stores reading this fall into the Searchanise camp; a smaller set of larger brands genuinely need what Findify sells.

Best For

Pick Searchanise if you run a small-to-mid Shopify store, want instant search and filtering live within a day, and need to stay under roughly $19-$89/mo while you validate the ROI of search upgrades. The free tier makes this close to zero-risk to try first.

Pick Searchanise if you want to test AI-driven search personalization without committing to a large monthly line item. The 7-day trial and free plan let you prove out value before you pay anything.

Pick Findify if you're a mid-market or enterprise Shopify brand with a large catalog and a dedicated CRO or growth team that will actively operate personalized merchandising, smart collections, and recommendations rather than just switching them on and forgetting them.

Pick Findify if personalized search and recommendations are core to your growth strategy and $499-$1,199/mo is a rounding error against the conversion lift you're already seeing from similar tools, since Findify's Professional and Enterprise tiers are priced for that level of ambition, not for stores still proving out the category.

Pros And Cons

Searchanise pros: a genuinely free tier plus a $19/mo entry price makes it low-risk to adopt; unlimited filters and instant search cover what most Shopify stores actually need; AI personalization is included even at lower tiers; 7 tiers mean you can grow into higher plans instead of re-platforming.

Searchanise cons: pricing by product count means a fast-growing catalog can push you up tiers (and cost) quickly; because it's priced for breadth, its personalization and merchandising depth is lighter than a tool built specifically for that job.

Findify pros: built specifically for personalization at scale, smart collections and recommendation engines are core features rather than add-ons; flat pricing per plan gives predictable costs regardless of how large the catalog gets within that tier.

Findify cons: a $499/mo minimum prices out small and early-stage stores; the jump to $799/mo (Professional) and $1,199/mo (Enterprise) only pays off if you have the traffic and team to use the deeper features; there's no low-cost way to trial the platform long-term the way Searchanise's free tier allows.

Recommendation

For most DTC founders reading this, Searchanise is the right call: free to start, $19/mo to go live with real filtering and AI personalization, and room to grow through its 7 tiers as your catalog scales. Reserve Findify for the point where your catalog and traffic genuinely justify $499/mo or more and you have a team that will actively use its merchandising and recommendation tools, not just enable them. One adjacent note: if your goal extends beyond on-site search, for example wanting your products surfaced accurately in AI shopping assistants and answer engines, that's outside what either tool covers, and it's the specific gap Buffy is built to close. But for search and filtering on your Shopify storefront specifically, this is a two-horse race between Searchanise and Findify, and the deciding factor is simply your catalog size and budget, not brand reputation or feature marketing.

Faq

How much does Searchanise cost compared to Findify?

Searchanise has a free plan, then paid tiers at $19/mo, $39/mo, $89/mo, $139/mo, $209/mo, and $349/mo, priced by product count across 7 tiers. Findify has no free tier and starts at $499/mo (Premium), rising to $799/mo (Professional) and $1,199/mo (Enterprise). Searchanise is priced for stores still growing into their search needs; Findify is priced for brands that have already validated personalization ROI and need it at scale.

Is Findify worth the $499/mo starting price?

It depends on your catalog size and traffic. If personalized search and recommendations move meaningful revenue for a mid-market or enterprise Shopify brand, $499/mo (or more at the Professional and Enterprise tiers) is easy to justify. For stores with smaller catalogs or early-stage traffic, that's a lot of fixed cost for capabilities you can test more cheaply first with Searchanise's AI personalization at $19-$39/mo.

Which is better for a small Shopify store: Searchanise or Findify?

Searchanise, mainly because of its free tier and $19/mo entry price, plus pricing that scales with product count rather than charging a flat mid-market fee from day one. Findify's $499/mo minimum makes more sense once a store has outgrown that stage.

Does Searchanise offer AI-powered search?

Yes. Searchanise includes AI personalization alongside instant search and unlimited filtering, even at its lower-priced tiers, which is part of why it's a strong fit for stores that want modern search behavior without enterprise pricing.

Can I switch from Searchanise to Findify later as I grow?

Many stores do move from a lighter search tool to a more specialized personalization platform as catalog size and traffic increase. Expect to budget time for re-configuring search, filters, and merchandising rules during that switch, and verify current migration and integration details directly with each vendor before committing, since specifics can change.
