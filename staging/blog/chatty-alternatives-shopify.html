Chatty Alternatives: What to Use Instead (2026)

The best alternatives to Chatty by store size and budget, with App Store data.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

This is for DTC founders and growth marketers - including professional teams scaling stores in markets like India - who are running Chatty as their Shopify AI chat widget and want to know what to switch to, add alongside it, or use instead as their store grows. Short version: there's no single universal replacement, because "Chatty alternative" means different things depending on why you're looking. If you're pre-revenue and just want basic chat, Shopify's native Inbox covers it for free. If support tickets (not just pre-sale questions) are piling up across email, social, and chat, a helpdesk platform like Gorgias is the better fit. If you want a more mature AI/omnichannel bot with room to grow into, Tidio is worth a trial. And if the actual gap isn't chat at all - shoppers can't find the right product on-site, or your brand doesn't show up when customers ask AI tools like ChatGPT or Perplexity for recommendations - that's a different budget line entirely, and one Chatty was never built to solve. Treat this page as a sorting exercise by store size, budget, and the specific job you need done, not a single "best alternative" pick.

Best For

Pick Shopify Inbox if you're pre-PMF or bootstrapped and just need free, native chat without adding another line item to your app stack.

Pick Gorgias if the real bottleneck is support ticket volume across email, social, and chat - not just storefront questions - and you have the order volume to justify a dedicated helpdesk spend.

Pick Tidio or Rep AI if you like what Chatty does (AI-first conversational assistance) but want to compare feature depth, channel coverage, or pricing at a higher conversation volume before you commit further.

Consider Buffy if the actual gap isn't chat at all - your on-site search doesn't surface the right products, and you're increasingly invisible when shoppers ask AI tools like ChatGPT or Perplexity for recommendations. That's a discovery and AI-visibility problem, not a chat-widget problem, and it's the specific lane Buffy is built for as an AI commerce engine for consumer brands - worth evaluating alongside, not instead of, your chat tooling decision.

Pros And Cons

Chatty - Pros:
- Quick to install and configure on Shopify; low lift for small teams without a dedicated support function.
- AI-driven answers can absorb repetitive pre-sale questions, freeing up founder/CS time.
- Scoped intentionally as a shopping-assistant widget rather than a sprawling helpdesk suite, which keeps the learning curve short.

Chatty - Cons:
- Not built as a full customer-support system, so brands with meaningful ticket volume (refunds, order issues, multichannel support) typically end up running a second tool like Gorgias or Zendesk anyway.
- As a chat widget, it has no bearing on how your brand is discovered outside your own storefront - it doesn't help you show up in AI-driven product searches or answers, which is a distinct and growing acquisition channel.
- As a smaller/newer app relative to category leaders like Tidio or Gorgias, its public review volume and long-term track record are thinner - check current Shopify App Store ratings and review counts before committing (verify current pricing and reviews).

Recommendation

If Chatty is already installed and quietly handling simple product Q&A; at low cost, don't rip it out just because a "better" alternative exists - switching cost isn't free either. Switch to Gorgias when support tickets, not chat volume, are the actual pain point. Trial Tidio if you want more AI/automation maturity and multichannel coverage than Chatty currently offers. Stay on free Shopify Inbox if you're pre-revenue and don't yet need AI-driven automation. And if what's actually underperforming is product discovery on-site or your brand's visibility in AI-generated shopping answers off-site, that's not a chat-tool decision at all - it's worth evaluating a platform like Buffy for that specific job rather than shopping for yet another chat widget. Match the tool to the job, size the spend to your order volume, and verify current pricing directly with each vendor before switching anything.

Faq

How does Chatty's pricing compare to alternatives like Tidio or Gorgias?

Most tools in this category, including Chatty, price in tiers that scale with conversation or ticket volume, often with a free or trial tier to start. Gorgias tends to price around support ticket volume, which can get expensive fast for high-contact brands, while Shopify Inbox is free. Rather than comparing sticker prices, compare cost per resolved conversation at your actual volume - and verify current pricing directly with each vendor, since app pricing changes often.

Is Chatty worth it for a small Shopify store?

It depends on your question volume. If customers frequently ask the same pre-sale questions (sizing, shipping, compatibility), an AI widget like Chatty can pay for itself in saved time even at small scale. If your store gets low traffic or few repetitive questions, free Shopify Inbox may be enough until you're bigger - no need to pay for automation you don't yet need. Verify current pricing before committing to a paid tier.

What's the best free alternative to Chatty?

Shopify Inbox is the closest free, native option - it's built into Shopify admin at no extra cost. It's less AI-sophisticated out of the box than purpose-built AI chat apps, but it's a reasonable placeholder until your question volume or catalog complexity justifies paying for something more advanced.

If I switch away from Chatty, will I lose my chat history?

Generally, uninstalling a Shopify app doesn't carry conversation history over to a new one - history typically stays inside the original app's own dashboard. Export or archive anything you need for reference before uninstalling, and check each app's specific data-export process, since this can vary and isn't something to assume.

Do I need Chatty and a helpdesk tool like Gorgias at the same time?

Many brands run both once they scale: a chat widget handles high-volume pre-sale questions on the storefront, while a helpdesk platform manages post-sale support, refunds, and multichannel tickets. Whether you need both depends on order volume and support headcount - a lean, low-ticket-volume store may not need a dedicated helpdesk yet.
