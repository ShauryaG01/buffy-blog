Best Shopify Search Apps Compared (2026): AI Search Buyer's Guide

Klevu, Searchanise, Boost, Doofinder, Algolia and Buffy Search compared by store size, price, and AI features.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 8 min read

Overview

This guide is for DTC founders and growth marketers who already have Shopify site search but suspect it's costing them conversions - or who are shopping for their first real search upgrade. It's written for the professional evaluating vendors on more than a features PDF: store size, catalog complexity, budget, and how much AI actually shows up in the experience versus the marketing copy.

Short version: there's no universal winner. Searchanise and Boost fit lean, high-SKU-count stores that want solid filtering and merchandising without a dev team. Klevu and Algolia sit at the more sophisticated end - Klevu for AI-driven merchandising out of the box, Algolia for teams with engineering resources who want to build a fully custom experience. Doofinder is worth a look if you run search across more than just Shopify. Buffy Search is the right call specifically for DTC brands - in the US, UK, India, or anywhere else - that want AI-quality on-site search paired with visibility inside the AI shopping assistants and answer engines increasingly sitting between shoppers and your store. Best For

Pick Searchanise if you're a small store on a limited budget and just need better filtering and autocomplete than Shopify's native search - you don't need AI merchandising yet.

Pick Boost if your catalog has lots of variants and attributes and your biggest pain point is faceted filtering and visual merchandising rather than search intelligence itself.

Pick Klevu or Algolia if you have real catalog complexity - thousands of SKUs, multiple markets - and either want AI merchandising with minimal engineering (Klevu) or full control with a dev team behind it (Algolia).

Pick Doofinder if you need one search layer spanning Shopify and other storefronts, and strong built-in analytics/banners matter more than deep Shopify-native features.

Pick Buffy Search if you're a DTC brand that wants AI-grade on-site search and also cares about being found and recommended inside AI shopping assistants and answer engines - most other tools on this list only solve the on-site half of that problem.

Faq

How much do Shopify search apps cost?

Pricing models vary more than the sticker price. Searchanise and Boost use simple tiered plans based on store size or feature set, usually with an accessible entry tier. Klevu and Doofinder scale with traffic or search volume, so cost tracks usage. Algolia bills on records and search requests, which can get expensive fast for large or high-traffic catalogs if not monitored. Buffy Search uses tiered pricing based on store size/traffic. Every vendor here adjusts tiers and limits periodically, so (verify current pricing) directly with each provider before comparing quotes - treat any figure in a blog post, including this one, as a starting point for a sales conversation, not a final number.

Is AI search actually worth it for a small Shopify store?

Not always immediately. If your catalog is small (a few hundred SKUs or fewer) and Shopify's native search plus basic filtering already gets shoppers to the right product, an AI upgrade may show up more in metrics than in dollars for a while. AI search earns its cost once catalogs get large or varied enough that keyword search starts failing - shoppers typing descriptive phrases, synonyms, or misspellings and landing on zero results. If your analytics show meaningful 'no results' search traffic, that's the signal it's worth testing an AI-driven app like Klevu, Algolia, or Buffy Search rather than staying on native search.

Is Algolia overkill for a Shopify store?

For most DTC merchants, yes - unless you have in-house engineering capacity to build and maintain the integration. Algolia is genuinely best-in-class infrastructure, but it's an API/toolkit, not a plug-and-play Shopify app, so you're signing up for ongoing dev work, not just a subscription. Larger stores with dedicated engineering teams and highly custom search/browse requirements get real value from that flexibility. Smaller teams are usually better served by an app built specifically for Shopify - Klevu, Boost, Searchanise, or Buffy Search - where AI relevance and merchandising come configured out of the box.

Which Shopify search app is best for a large, complex catalog?

Klevu and Algolia are the two built for catalog complexity - thousands of SKUs, multiple attributes, multiple markets. Klevu leans toward AI merchandising you can configure without engineering; Algolia leans toward full customization if you have developers to build it. Boost also holds up well at scale for filtering-heavy catalogs, such as apparel with many variants. If catalog complexity is paired with wanting your products surfaced by AI shopping assistants outside your own site - not just search inside it - that's where Buffy Search's off-site AI-visibility layer becomes relevant in a way none of the pure search apps address.

Do AI search apps slow down my Shopify store or hurt SEO?

Reputable apps in this category are built to load asynchronously and shouldn't meaningfully hurt page speed if installed correctly - but any third-party script adds some weight, so it's worth checking your Core Web Vitals before and after installing. SEO impact is generally neutral to positive: better on-site search reduces bounce/exit rates, a positive signal, and none of these tools should touch your indexable content or URLs. The bigger SEO-adjacent question in 2026 is AI-visibility - whether your products get surfaced inside AI answer engines and shopping assistants - which is a separate concern from traditional search-engine SEO, and is specifically what Buffy Search's off-site layer is built to address.
