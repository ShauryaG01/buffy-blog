Profound Alternatives: 6 AI Visibility Tools Compared (2026)

Profound is the enterprise leader - here is what it costs, where it shines, and the alternatives for DTC budgets.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

"Profound Alternatives: 6 AI Visibility Tools Compared (2026)" is for DTC founders and growth marketing professionals who've heard Profound is the category leader for AI-search visibility and want to know whether it's worth the price - or whether a leaner tool fits their budget better.

Short version: Profound earns its enterprise reputation. Profound Agents, conversation-level analytics, and SOC2/SSO make it a strong pick for larger teams tracking brand mentions across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other engines. But its $99/mo Starter plan tracks ChatGPT only - the multi-engine coverage that makes Profound worth talking about starts at $399/mo Growth, with Enterprise a custom quote on top of that. For most DTC brands, that's a lot to commit before you've proven AI search visibility moves revenue.

The more realistic starting point for a DTC budget is a dedicated monitoring tool - Otterly AI at $29/mo or Peec AI at $95/mo both track the engines that matter without enterprise pricing. If you want visibility tracking to translate into actual changes on your site rather than just a dashboard of mentions, a tool like Buffy pairs AI-visibility tracking with on-site optimization aimed at how AI engines describe and cite your product pages. Below, six tools compared on pricing, engine coverage, and where each genuinely wins.

Best For

Pick Profound if you're an enterprise or larger mid-market team with budget for $399+/mo and need conversation-level analytics, SOC2/SSO, and dedicated Profound Agents - the entry $99/mo tier is really just a taste of what makes it worth choosing.

Pick Otterly AI if you're an early-stage DTC brand or solo marketer who mainly needs a low-cost signal on whether you're showing up in ChatGPT and AI Overviews - $29/mo Lite is the cheapest credible entry point in this comparison.

Pick Peec AI if you run an agency or manage AI-visibility tracking across several client or brand accounts - unlimited seats and multi-project support at $95/mo make it the most agency-friendly option here.

Pick Buffy if you're a DTC brand that wants AI-visibility tracking to lead somewhere - pairing it with on-site work on product and content pages so AI engines have better material to cite you from, rather than a monitoring dashboard alone.

Pros And Cons

Profound
Pros: broadest enterprise feature set of the group - Profound Agents, conversation-level analytics, and SOC2/SSO that procurement teams ask for; genuinely strong multi-engine tracking once you're on Growth or Enterprise.
Cons: the $99/mo Starter plan tracking ChatGPT only feels like a lead-gen tier rather than a real starting product; cost scales quickly ($99 → $399 → custom) as soon as you need more than one engine; more platform than most DTC teams need day one.

Budget alternatives (Otterly AI, Peec AI)
Pros: real multi-engine tracking (ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overviews, and more) at a fraction of Profound's Growth-tier price; Peec AI's unlimited seats and Otterly's unlimited team members remove the per-user tax bigger platforms charge.
Cons: neither does anything on-site - they tell you where you stand in AI answers but leave the fixing to you; entry tiers cap prompt/query volume, so costs still climb as tracking needs grow.

Recommendation

If you're already spending $399+/mo elsewhere on brand analytics and need enterprise governance, conversation-level insight, and Profound Agents, Profound is the defensible choice - it's the most complete platform in this set, and the price reflects that. But for most DTC brands evaluating this page, the honest answer is: don't start with Profound's entry tier. It tracks one engine and mainly demonstrates what you're missing at the next tier up.

Start with Otterly AI ($29/mo) if budget is the constraint, or Peec AI ($95/mo) if you're managing multiple brands or client accounts and want unlimited seats. Both give you real multi-engine visibility data without enterprise pricing. Graduate to Profound or AthenaHQ once you have evidence that AI-search visibility is moving revenue and you need the deeper analytics and governance features to match a bigger program. And if what you actually want is visibility data that turns into on-site changes - not just another dashboard - that's the gap Buffy is built to close for DTC brands specifically.

Faq

How much does Profound cost?

Profound has three tiers: Starter at $99/mo, Growth at $399/mo, and a custom-priced Enterprise plan. Prices are billed annually. The important caveat is that Starter only tracks ChatGPT - multi-engine tracking, which is Profound's core strength, starts at the Growth tier.

Is Profound worth it for a small DTC brand?

Usually not at the entry tier. $99/mo for ChatGPT-only tracking is expensive relative to Otterly AI ($29/mo) or Peec AI ($95/mo), both of which cover more engines out of the box. Profound becomes worth it once you need Growth-tier multi-engine data, Profound Agents, or enterprise-grade SSO/SOC2 - features most small DTC teams aren't ready to use yet.

What's the cheapest Profound alternative?

Otterly AI, with a Lite plan at $29/mo. It covers ChatGPT, AI Overviews, and Perplexity tracking with unlimited team members, though prompt volume is limited at that tier and additional engines (Claude, Gemini, AI Mode) cost extra.

Which Profound alternative is best for agencies managing multiple client accounts?

Peec AI. Its multi-project structure and unlimited seats (starting at $95/mo) are built for teams tracking AI visibility across several brands at once, which is a meaningfully different use case than a single in-house marketing team.

Do any Profound alternatives include on-site AI-search optimization, not just tracking?

Most of the tools here - Profound, Peec AI, Otterly AI, AthenaHQ - are monitoring and analytics platforms; they tell you how you're showing up in AI answers but don't change your site. Buffy is built to pair that visibility tracking with on-site optimization aimed at how AI engines describe and cite your product pages, which is the more direct fit for DTC brands trying to act on the data, not just collect it.
