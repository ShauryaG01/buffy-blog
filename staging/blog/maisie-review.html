Maisie Review (2026): Pricing, Features, and Who It Fits

Maisie reviewed for Shopify merchants - real pricing, App Store ratings, and best-fit store size.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

Maisie is an AI chatbot app for Shopify stores, built to automate FAQ answers, order-status replies, and abandoned-cart nudges inside an on-site chat widget (and often SMS/Messenger). This review is for Shopify merchants comparing Maisie against other on-site chat and support-automation tools before spending budget and dev time installing another app on top of Shopify.

Short verdict: Maisie is a reasonable pick if you're a small-to-mid store that wants a low-lift chatbot to absorb repetitive "where's my order," shipping, and return questions, plus some cart-recovery messaging, without hiring a dedicated support agent. It's not built to make your products discoverable inside AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews, and its ticketing/agent-collaboration depth is generally lighter than dedicated helpdesk platforms. Treat any specific price or plan name you see on its App Store listing as a starting point only - Shopify app pricing shifts often, so verify current pricing before building a budget around it. If your real bottleneck is AI-driven on-site product search or visibility in AI shopping answers rather than support-ticket volume, that's a different job, and worth evaluating as its own category.

Best For

Pick Maisie if you run a lean Shopify store and want a chatbot that can competently answer shipping, return, and order-status questions and nudge a few abandoned carts back to checkout - without adding headcount.

Pick Gorgias if your support volume has outgrown what a founder or one part-time agent can handle and you need real ticketing, agent assignment, and macros that pull live Shopify order data.

Pick Tidio if budget is the deciding factor and you want the cheapest possible live-chat-plus-basic-bot setup, and you're willing to accept lighter automation logic.

Pick Buffy if your actual constraint isn't support volume - it's that shoppers can't find the right product once they're on your site, or your brand doesn't show up when people ask AI assistants for recommendations. That's on-site AI search plus off-site AI-visibility, a genuinely different problem than what a support chatbot like Maisie solves, and forcing a support bot to fix a discovery problem usually disappoints.

Pros And Cons

Pros:
- Shopify-native setup that's generally quick to install and configure without heavy dev work.
- Handles high-volume repetitive questions (order status, shipping, returns) without routing every conversation to a human.
- Abandoned-cart chat nudges can recover incremental revenue that email alone misses.
- Lower learning curve than full helpdesk platforms - a reasonable first chatbot for a store that's never automated support before.

Cons:
- Reporting and agent-collaboration depth typically trail dedicated helpdesk tools like Gorgias or Zendesk - worth checking if you expect to outgrow a basic bot within a year.
- Chatbot app pricing on Shopify commonly gates by conversation or contact volume, so cost can climb with traffic; verify current pricing and read the fine print on overage charges before committing.
- It's a support/conversion tool, not a discovery tool - it won't help products surface in AI search answers or improve on-site search relevance.
- App Store rating, review count, and sentiment shift over time; check Maisie's current Shopify App Store listing directly rather than relying on secondhand claims about its rating.

Recommendation

Maisie is a sensible, low-friction choice if you're a small-to-mid Shopify store that needs a chatbot to absorb repetitive support questions and recover a few more abandoned carts, and you don't yet need full helpdesk infrastructure. It's the wrong tool if what you're actually chasing is AI-search visibility or a stronger on-site product-discovery experience - that's a separate category, and Buffy is built specifically for that job (on-site AI search plus showing up in AI shopping answers), not for ticket handling.

Before you commit: install the free tier or trial if one's available, run it against two weeks of your real FAQ volume, and verify current pricing directly on the Shopify App Store listing rather than trusting a plan name or price quoted elsewhere - Shopify app pricing pages change often, and this page intentionally avoids repeating specific numbers you should confirm yourself.

Faq

How much does Maisie cost?

Shopify chatbot apps like Maisie typically run on a freemium model - a limited free tier plus paid tiers priced by conversation or contact volume rather than a single flat fee. Exact plan names and prices change frequently on the Shopify App Store, so don't budget off an old screenshot or a competitor's claim - open Maisie's current listing and verify current pricing before you commit.

Is Maisie worth it for a small Shopify store?

If you're spending real time each week answering the same "where's my order" and return questions, a chatbot like Maisie is usually worth testing - it's low-effort to install and can plausibly pay for itself in reclaimed time plus a bit of cart-recovery revenue. It's less clearly worth it if your support volume is already low or if your real problem is on-site product discovery rather than answering repetitive questions.

What's the difference between Maisie and Gorgias?

Maisie is oriented around a chat widget with automated FAQ and cart-recovery flows - good for absorbing volume without much setup. Gorgias is a fuller helpdesk: ticketing, agent assignment, macros built on live Shopify order data, and reporting meant for a team, not a solo founder. Stores with growing support headcount tend to outgrow a pure chatbot and move toward Gorgias-style tooling.

Does Maisie replace the need for a human support agent?

Not entirely. It can deflect the repetitive, low-complexity questions (shipping timelines, return policy, order status), but anything nuanced - refund exceptions, damaged-item claims, angry customers - still needs a human. Think of it as reducing ticket volume, not eliminating support staff.

Does Maisie help with SEO or showing up in AI search answers?

No - Maisie is a support and conversion chatbot, not an SEO or AI-visibility product. It won't improve how your products rank in Google, nor how often your brand gets surfaced when someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity for a recommendation. If that's the goal, look at tools built specifically for on-site AI search and AI-answer visibility, such as Buffy, rather than expecting a support chatbot to do that job.
