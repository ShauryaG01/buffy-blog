Share of Model Voice: The AI-Era Share of Voice Metric

How often your brand appears in AI answers for your category, and how to move it.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Definition

Share of Model Voice (SOMV) is the percentage of AI-generated answers in your category that mention your brand, relative to competitors. When someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, or an AI Overview a question like "best organic sheets for hot sleepers" or "top skincare brands for sensitive skin," SOMV measures how often your name shows up in the response, where it appears in that response, and how that compares to the brands mentioned alongside you. It's the AI-era counterpart to traditional "share of voice" in search and PR, except the audience isn't a search results page or a media mention count, it's a language model deciding which brands are worth naming when a shopper asks for a recommendation. SOMV is typically calculated by running a fixed set of category and comparison prompts across multiple AI models on a recurring basis, logging every brand mention, and dividing your mention count by total mentions across all tracked brands for that prompt set. Some versions also weight mentions by position, since being named first in an AI answer functions differently than being buried in a list of five alternatives. For DTC brands, SOMV is a leading indicator of visibility in a research channel that customers increasingly use before they ever open Google or Amazon.

Why It Matters

AI assistants are becoming a real discovery step in the shopping journey, sitting upstream of the point where a customer types a brand name into a search bar or search Amazon directly. If your brand never comes up when someone asks an AI model for recommendations in your category, you may be losing consideration before you'd ever see it in your traditional funnel or analytics, because there's no click, impression, or session to measure, just an answer given without you in it. Unlike SEO rankings, which you can check anytime, model answers vary by prompt phrasing, model version, and even the day you ask, which makes SOMV harder to track passively and easier to miss if you're not deliberately monitoring it. For a Shopify merchant or DTC founder, low SOMV in a competitive category can mean well-funded or more established competitors are consistently the ones AI models recommend by default, regardless of product quality. Tracking SOMV over time gives you a directional read on whether your brand's presence in AI-mediated discovery is growing, flat, or shrinking relative to competitors, which matters most in categories where buyers are known to ask AI tools for comparisons or shortlists before purchasing. As with any emerging metric, treat SOMV as one input among several rather than a definitive scorecard, since methodology and prompt selection heavily influence the numbers, and there's no single standardized way to measure it yet across the industry.

How It Works

Measuring SOMV starts with building a prompt set: a representative list of questions real customers might ask an AI assistant in your category, covering direct recommendations ("best [category] for [use case]"), comparisons ("[Brand A] vs [Brand B]"), and problem-based queries ("how do I fix [pain point]"). That prompt set gets run repeatedly, ideally on a schedule, across the AI surfaces your customers actually use, which may include ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, or Google's AI Overviews, since each model draws on different training data and retrieval sources and can produce different answers to the same question. Every response is parsed for brand mentions, tallying not just whether your brand appears but where, first-mentioned, mentioned with caveats, or absent entirely, since position often correlates with how much weight a reader gives the mention. Your SOMV score for a given period is your mention count divided by total brand mentions across the prompt set, and tracking that ratio over weeks or months reveals trend direction rather than a single absolute truth. Because model outputs are non-deterministic and prompts can be phrased countless ways, most teams treat any single measurement as a sample rather than ground truth, and instead look for consistent patterns across repeated runs. Tools built for this, including Buffy, automate the prompt running, response parsing, and trend tracking so brands don't have to manually query multiple AI platforms and log results by hand.

Example

Picture a DTC bedding brand that wants to understand its standing in AI-driven recommendations. It builds a prompt set of roughly 20 queries a real shopper might type, things like "best cooling sheets for hot sleepers," "[Competitor] vs [Competitor] sheets," and "organic cotton sheet brands worth the price." Each week, it runs that prompt set against a few major AI assistants and logs which brands appear in each response, along with whether the brand was named first or mentioned only after several competitors. Over a month, the brand notices it appears in about a third of the "cooling sheets" answers but almost never shows up in the "organic cotton" comparison prompts, even though organic cotton is a core part of its product line. That gap points to a specific content or positioning problem: the brand's messaging and any content that discusses organic materials may not be detailed or authoritative enough for AI models to surface it as a match, even though its product qualifies. Acting on that signal might mean publishing more detailed, specific content about materials and sourcing, since AI models tend to pull from pages that answer the underlying question clearly rather than pages optimized purely for keywords. This is illustrative only; actual mention rates and prompt outcomes will vary by brand, category, and the AI models tested.

Faq

What is Share of Model Voice (SOMV)?

SOMV is the share of AI-generated answers in your category that mention your brand, compared to competitors, measured by running representative prompts across AI assistants and tracking mention frequency and position over time.

How is Share of Model Voice different from traditional share of voice?

Traditional share of voice tracks mentions across search results, media, or social platforms with clicks and impressions to measure. SOMV tracks mentions inside AI-generated answers, where there's no click or page view, only whether the model named your brand when asked.

How do I measure my brand's Share of Model Voice?

Build a list of category and comparison prompts your customers would realistically ask, run them repeatedly across AI assistants like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini, log every brand mention and its position, then calculate your mention count as a percentage of total mentions.

How can I improve my Share of Model Voice?

Focus on publishing clear, specific, well-sourced content that directly answers the questions your category's buyers ask, since AI models tend to surface brands with authoritative, detailed answers rather than keyword-optimized pages. Consistent monitoring also helps you spot which topics or use cases you're missing from entirely.

Which AI platforms should I track for Share of Model Voice?

Prioritize the AI surfaces your customers are most likely to use for research, commonly ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, and Google's AI Overviews, since mention rates and cited sources can differ meaningfully between them.
