Gorgias Review (2026): Pricing, Features, and Who It Fits

Gorgias reviewed for Shopify merchants - real pricing, App Store ratings, and best-fit store size.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

This review is for Shopify and Shopify Plus merchants deciding whether Gorgias should be their customer support helpdesk - and how it stacks up against Zendesk, Re:amaze, Richpanel, and adjacent tools like Buffy that solve a different problem (on-site AI search and off-site AI visibility, not ticketing).

Short verdict: Gorgias earns its reputation as the Shopify-native helpdesk. Order data, tags, and revenue attribution sit right inside the ticket, and its macros and automation rules cut real time off repetitive tickets (returns, WISMO, discount requests). The catch is pricing: Gorgias bills on ticket/conversation volume, so costs rise as your support load grows - a real difference from flat-seat pricing at some competitors. It's the right pick for DTC brands with meaningful order volume and a support team of two or more agents; it's often overkill, and pricier than needed, for pre-revenue or very early-stage stores handling a handful of tickets a day.

Best For

Pick Gorgias if you're a Shopify or Shopify Plus brand with a dedicated support agent (or team) and enough order volume that having customer and order data inline actually saves time - the automation and macros pay for themselves once you're past a few hundred tickets a month.

Pick Zendesk if you sell across multiple platforms (not just Shopify), run a larger or multi-department support org, or need enterprise-grade configurability and reporting beyond commerce-specific workflows.

Pick Richpanel or Re:amaze if you want Shopify-aware support at a lower price point and don't yet need Gorgias's full automation depth - a common choice for brands still validating product-market fit.

Pick Buffy if your bottleneck isn't support tickets but discovery and conversion - shoppers not finding the right product on-site, or your brand not showing up in AI-driven search and shopping assistants. Buffy solves a pre-purchase problem; Gorgias solves a post-purchase/support one, and most scaling DTC brands eventually need both.

Pros And Cons

Pros:
- Native Shopify integration surfaces order history, shipping status, and customer value directly in the ticket sidebar, saving agents from tab-switching.
- Automation rules and macros meaningfully reduce time spent on repetitive tickets like WISMO, returns, and discount codes.
- Revenue-attribution reporting ties support conversations to sales, useful for justifying headcount and proving support ROI to leadership.
- Multichannel coverage (email, live chat, social, and SMS via add-ons) consolidates channels DTC brands actually use.
- Consistently well-regarded in the Shopify App Store, with a large volume of merchant reviews (verify current rating).

Cons:
- Pricing is usage-based (tickets/conversations), so costs can climb faster than expected as order volume and support load grow - budget for this before committing (verify current pricing).
- Some merchants report a learning curve configuring automation rules and macros well; out-of-the-box setup undersells the platform's depth.
- Occasional merchant complaints about support responsiveness or billing/plan changes surface in App Store reviews - worth reading recent reviews, not just the aggregate rating.
- It's a helpdesk, not a discovery or search tool - it won't help with on-site product findability or AI-search visibility, which are separate problems.

Recommendation

Bottom line: Gorgias is a sound default helpdesk for Shopify-native DTC brands once you have real support volume and want commerce data inside every ticket - the automation and attribution reporting are genuinely differentiated for ecommerce, not generic helpdesk features repackaged. The risk isn't the product, it's the pricing model: because costs scale with tickets/conversations, run your own volume estimate against current pricing tiers (verify current pricing) before signing, especially if you're scaling fast. If you're pre-revenue or handling a handful of tickets a day, start with a lower-cost option like Re:amaze or Richpanel and graduate to Gorgias when volume justifies it. And remember Gorgias solves support, not discovery - if shoppers are bouncing before they ever open a ticket, that's a different tool category (like Buffy) and a different budget line.

Faq

How much does Gorgias cost in 2026?

Gorgias uses tiered, usage-based pricing keyed to ticket or conversation volume rather than a single flat fee, so the right plan depends on your monthly support load and the channels you use. Because pricing tiers and included-conversation limits change periodically, check Gorgias's pricing page directly for current numbers before budgeting (verify current pricing).

Is Gorgias only for Shopify stores?

Gorgias started as Shopify-native and that's still its strongest integration, but it also supports other ecommerce platforms. If you're not on Shopify or Shopify Plus, verify that your platform's integration has the same depth (order data, tagging) before assuming feature parity.

Is Gorgias worth it for a small or early-stage store?

Often not yet. Gorgias's value comes from automation and order-data lookups paying off at real ticket volume; a store handling only a handful of tickets a day may find a lower-cost tool like Re:amaze or Richpanel more cost-effective until support load grows.

How does Gorgias compare to Zendesk for a DTC brand?

Gorgias wins on Shopify-specific context (order data, revenue attribution) and ecommerce-tuned automation; Zendesk wins on enterprise configurability, multi-platform support, and depth for larger or multi-department support orgs. Single-platform Shopify brands usually lean Gorgias; multi-channel or larger orgs often lean Zendesk.

What do the App Store reviews say about Gorgias?

Gorgias generally holds a strong average rating on the Shopify App Store with a large number of merchant reviews, reflecting solid satisfaction with its Shopify integration and support automation (verify current rating). As with any tool, read recent reviews specifically - comments on pricing changes or support responsiveness tend to be more current than the aggregate score.
