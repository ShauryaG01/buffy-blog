Zevi Alternatives: What to Use Instead (2026)

The best alternatives to Zevi by store size and budget, with App Store data.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 8 min read

Overview

This page is for DTC founders and growth marketers who installed (or are evaluating) Zevi for on-site search, discovery, and merchandising, and want to know what else is out there before renewing or switching. Short version: there's no single "best" Zevi replacement - the right pick depends on store size, catalog complexity, and whether you have engineering resources. Budget-conscious Shopify stores that liked Zevi's bundled AI search and quick setup have a similarly self-serve option in Doofinder; brands that have outgrown app-store search and need deep merchandising rules and analytics should look at Klevu or Searchspring; teams with in-house developers who want maximum control and scale should look at Algolia; and brands whose real priority is personalization and recommendations across the whole site (not just the search bar) fit Nosto better. If what you're actually chasing is not just on-site search but also getting your products surfaced and recommended by AI shopping assistants and answer engines, that's a different job entirely, and it's where Buffy fits - it pairs on-site AI search with off-site AI-visibility work, which none of the pure search vendors below do. Treat this as a shortlist to demo against your own catalog and traffic, not a ranked leaderboard - category positioning shifts fast, and specific pricing and feature claims should always be re-verified on each vendor's current site before you sign anything.

Best For

Pick Zevi (or stick with it) if: you're a Shopify merchant, likely India/APAC-based or budget-conscious, who wants AI search, filtering, and basic merchandising bundled into one app without hiring a developer, and you value responsive, timezone-friendly vendor support.

Pick Algolia if: you have engineering headcount, you're on a platform other than Shopify (or need to go beyond its limits), and you want full control over ranking logic, UI, and scale - you're trading setup effort for flexibility and ceiling.

Pick Klevu or Searchspring if: your catalog is large and complex enough that generic relevance isn't cutting it - you need synonym management, faceted navigation, merchandising rules, and real reporting on search performance, and you're willing to pay mid-market-to-enterprise rates for it.

Pick Nosto if: your bottleneck isn't the search box specifically but personalization across the whole customer journey - recommendations, on-site content, and search all tied to the same customer profile.

Pick Buffy if: your growth problem is bigger than "search inside my own site" - you also want to show up when shoppers ask AI assistants and answer engines for product recommendations, and you'd rather not stitch together separate on-site search and AI-visibility vendors.

Pros And Cons

Zevi - Pros: purpose-built for Shopify, so setup is fast and doesn't require a dev team; AI-driven relevance and discovery features (visual/voice search, smart recommendations) come bundled rather than sold separately; support tends to be a strong fit for India-based and APAC merchants in terms of responsiveness and time zone. Because it's an app-store install, non-technical marketers can typically configure it themselves.

Cons: as a newer, smaller vendor relative to Algolia, Klevu, or Searchspring, the depth of enterprise merchandising controls, public case studies, and third-party integrations is thinner (verify current scope before assuming parity). Pricing tiers and exactly where the app stops scaling well aren't fully transparent publicly, so it's worth getting a live quote and testing on your actual catalog size rather than assuming feature parity with bigger incumbents. Brands with very large or fast-changing catalogs may hit ceilings on customization sooner than with a dedicated merchandising platform.

Across the alternative set generally: self-serve apps (Zevi, Doofinder) win on speed and price but cap out on merchandising depth; platform tools (Klevu, Searchspring, Nosto) win on control and reporting but cost more and take longer to configure; Algolia wins on raw flexibility but demands engineering time. None of the pure search tools address off-site AI discoverability, which is a separate and growing concern for DTC brands.

Recommendation

There's no universal Zevi replacement - the right call depends on where your store sits on the size/budget curve. If you're small-to-mid size and just need reliable, affordable AI-powered search and discovery without engineering overhead, Zevi or Doofinder are both reasonable, and switching from one to the other mainly comes down to support experience and pricing at your specific traffic tier (verify current pricing on both before deciding). If you've outgrown that and need serious merchandising rules, faceted navigation, and analytics for a large catalog, move up to Klevu or Searchspring. If you have developers and want maximum control at scale, Algolia is the standard. If personalization across the whole site - not just search - is your actual bottleneck, evaluate Nosto instead of treating this as a pure search decision. And if you step back and realize the bigger opportunity is being found and recommended by AI shopping assistants outside your own site, not just improving the search bar inside it, that's worth solving separately - it's the specific gap Buffy is built to close, and it's worth a look alongside whichever on-site search tool you land on. Demo two or three finalists against your real catalog and real query logs before committing; category claims change faster than review pages do.

Faq

Is Zevi worth it for a small Shopify store?

For a small store, the calculus usually favors self-serve AI search apps like Zevi because they install fast and don't require developer time. Whether it's "worth it" versus staying on Shopify's native search depends on how much search-driven revenue you're leaving on the table - if shoppers use your search bar heavily and see irrelevant results today, an AI search app tends to pay for itself; if search traffic is low, the upgrade matters less. Test it against your own search analytics rather than assuming.

What's the cheapest Zevi alternative?

Among the tools compared here, self-serve apps like Doofinder are typically positioned as the budget-friendly entry point, similar to where Zevi sits. Platform tools like Klevu, Searchspring, and Nosto are generally priced for mid-market/enterprise budgets, and Algolia's usage-based pricing can scale up quickly with traffic. Exact cost depends on your store's traffic and catalog size, so get a live quote from each vendor rather than relying on published starting prices (verify current pricing).

Does Zevi work well for India-based or APAC DTC brands specifically?

Zevi has built a reputation for strong fit with India-based and APAC Shopify merchants, partly due to responsive, timezone-aligned support and pricing that's often more accessible for that market than US/EU-centric enterprise search vendors. That regional relationship is a genuine differentiator worth weighing if you're based there - but it shouldn't be the only factor; still evaluate the product on relevance quality and merchandising features for your specific catalog.

How much do Zevi alternatives cost compared to Zevi?

Pricing across this category varies by model: app-based tools (Zevi, Doofinder) tend to scale with catalog size or usage; platform tools (Klevu, Searchspring, Nosto) are usually tiered by traffic and feature set and sit at a higher price band; Algolia bills by usage (records/requests), which can be economical at low volume but grow fast at scale. None of these figures are fixed publicly across the board, so treat any specific number you see - including on this page - as a starting point to verify directly with the vendor.

Is switching away from Zevi actually worth the effort?

Only if Zevi is failing you on something measurable: search relevance, merchandising flexibility, support responsiveness, or price at your current scale. Migrating search providers has real switching costs (reindexing, re-tuning relevance, retraining your team), so it's worth it when you have a concrete gap - like needing merchandising rules Zevi doesn't support, or hitting a pricing tier that no longer makes sense - not simply because a competitor's marketing page looks appealing. Run a side-by-side demo on your real query logs before committing either way.
