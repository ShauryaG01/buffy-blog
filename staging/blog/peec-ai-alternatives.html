Peec AI: Review, Pricing, and Alternatives

What Peec AI does well, its pricing, and when a DTC brand should pick something else.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-09 · 6 min read

Overview

Peec AI is an AI search analytics platform built for professional marketing teams and SEO agencies who need to know how a brand shows up inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. If you're a DTC founder or growth marketer asking "are we visible when people ask AI assistants about our category," Peec AI is a legitimate, professional-grade answer to the measurement side of that question - but it is a monitoring tool, not a fix. It tells you where you stand across engines; it does not change your product pages or content to improve what those engines cite.

Short verdict: Peec AI earns its $95/mo Starter price for teams running multiple client or brand projects who want daily tracking across the three biggest AI engines with unlimited seats - genuinely useful for agencies and multi-brand operators. Solo DTC operators or small teams watching a single brand will often get more value per dollar starting with Otterly AI's $29/mo tier, and larger organizations needing deeper governance and conversation analytics should look at Profound or AthenaHQ. If what you actually want is to fix your AI visibility rather than just measure it, that's a separate job - one that pure analytics platforms, Peec AI included, don't cover.

Best For

Pick Peec AI if you're an in-house SEO team or agency juggling multiple brand or client projects and want one dashboard with daily, multi-engine visibility data and unlimited seats - the per-project structure and lack of per-seat fees make it scale sensibly for that workflow.

Pick Otterly AI if you're a bootstrapped DTC brand or small marketing team that just wants to know where you stand in ChatGPT and AI Overviews without paying agency-tier prices; $29/mo is the cheapest credible way into this category.

Pick Profound if you're an enterprise or larger mid-market team that needs conversation-level analytics, SOC2/SSO compliance, and automated workflow "Agents," and your budget can absorb enterprise-tier pricing once you outgrow the ChatGPT-only starter plan.

Pick AthenaHQ if you run a mid-market or enterprise GEO program, want AI agents layered on top of raw tracking, and are comfortable committing without a free trial - just watch credit-based overages as usage scales.

Pros And Cons

Peec AI pros:
- Tracks the three engines that matter most today - ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews - with daily refresh
- Built for multi-project use, so agencies can run many client trackers under one account
- Unlimited seats on every tier, so cost doesn't climb with team headcount
- Clear, predictable tier ladder ($95 → $245 → $495 → custom Enterprise) as needs grow

Peec AI cons:
- Only 3 engines included by default; a 4th engine is a paid add-on running $35–$165/mo depending on tier
- Pure analytics - no on-site optimization component, so you still need separate tooling or manual work to act on what it finds
- Pro and Advanced tiers ($245 and $495/mo) get expensive quickly once you need more prompts, projects, or engines
- No sub-$95 entry tier, so it's not the cheapest way to start monitoring AI visibility if budget is the primary constraint

Recommendation

For an SEO agency or in-house team managing several brands that wants best-in-class multi-engine AI search tracking without per-seat fees, Peec AI's $95/mo Starter plan is a defensible pick - it covers the three engines that matter most and scales cleanly to $245 and $495/mo as projects and prompt volume grow. It's not the cheapest option (Otterly AI starts at $29/mo) and it's not the most enterprise-ready (Profound and AthenaHQ offer deeper governance and agent tooling for larger orgs), but for the specific job of daily, multi-project AI visibility monitoring, it sits in a sensible middle tier.

The one thing no amount of Peec AI reporting will do is fix your AI visibility for you - it's a dashboard, not a remediation tool. Solo DTC brands on a tight budget should start with Otterly AI and upgrade only if prompt volume demands it. Brands that want visibility tracking paired with actual on-site AI-search optimization - not just a report telling you what to fix - are better served by a platform like Buffy, which handles both halves of the problem instead of leaving you to hire someone to act on Peec AI's numbers.

Faq

How much does Peec AI cost?

Peec AI has four tiers: Starter at $95/mo, Pro at $245/mo, Advanced at $495/mo, and a custom-priced Enterprise plan. All tiers include unlimited seats. Tracking a 4th AI engine beyond the three included by default costs an extra $35–$165/mo depending on your tier.

Is Peec AI worth it for a small DTC brand?

It depends on what you need. If you're a solo brand or small team just trying to see how you show up in ChatGPT or AI Overviews, Peec AI's $95/mo entry price is higher than necessary - Otterly AI's $29/mo Lite plan covers the same core need more cheaply. Peec AI earns its price when you're managing multiple projects or brands and want daily, multi-engine tracking with no per-seat cost, which is a more agency- or multi-brand-shaped problem than a single-brand one.

What AI engines does Peec AI track?

Peec AI's core plans track ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. A fourth engine can be added as a paid add-on ($35–$165/mo by tier) if you need broader coverage.

What's a good cheaper alternative to Peec AI?

Otterly AI is the most direct budget alternative, starting at $29/mo and covering ChatGPT, AI Overviews, Perplexity, and Copilot tracking with unlimited team members. The trade-off is a lower prompt allowance at the entry tier, so heavy trackers may still end up paying more once they add capacity.

How does Peec AI compare to Profound or AthenaHQ for a larger team?

Profound (from $99/mo, annual-billed) and AthenaHQ (from $295/mo) both target enterprise and larger mid-market teams more directly than Peec AI does. Profound adds conversation-level analytics, automated Agents, and SOC2/SSO compliance; AthenaHQ layers AI agents onto its GEO tracking but skips a free trial. Peec AI's strength by comparison is its multi-project structure and unlimited seats at a lower starting price, making it the better fit for agencies rather than single large enterprises.
