Boost AI Search & Filter vs Algolia: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Boost AI Search & Filter and Algolia head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 6 min read

Overview

Boost AI Search & Filter and Algolia are both AI-powered search and discovery tools, but they serve different teams. Boost is a Shopify-native app: install it, and semantic search, filters, and merchandising are live within days, with pricing tied to your store's GMV. Algolia is a developer-first search API used across web, mobile, and commerce, offering more flexibility and control at the cost of engineering time and usage-based billing that scales with traffic.

For most Shopify merchants weighing Boost AI Search & Filter vs Algolia for Shopify search in 2026, the short answer is this: pick Boost if you want AI search running fast without a dev team, and pick Algolia if you're running a custom or multi-platform storefront and have engineering resources to implement it properly. Professional teams evaluating both, including growing DTC brands in markets like India, should model total cost as GMV grows (Boost) against total cost as query volume grows (Algolia) before committing to either.

Best For

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if you're a Shopify merchant who wants AI-powered search, filtering, and merchandising live in days without hiring engineers.

Pick Algolia if you have an in-house dev team and run a custom, headless, or multi-platform storefront where you need full control over the search stack.

Pick Algolia if personalization and custom ranking logic matter more to you than out-of-the-box Shopify merchandising tools.

Pick Boost if your store's GMV is under roughly $400K/mo, since that's where its tiered pricing stays most cost-effective before overage fees start compounding.

Pros And Cons

Boost AI Search & Filter
Pros: Purpose-built for Shopify, so setup and AI semantic search go live quickly without custom development. Entry pricing at $29/mo is accessible for small stores, and the tiered structure ($159/mo under $50K GMV, $599/mo under $400K GMV, $1499/mo under $2.5M GMV) is easy to plan against expected revenue. Filters and merchandising are bundled in, not a separate purchase.
Cons: Pricing is tied to GMV, not to actual search usage, so costs rise automatically as your store grows. Overage fees ($2 per extra $1,000 GMV) can add up near tier boundaries, and monthly-only billing means there's no annual discount to lock in a lower rate.

Algolia
Pros: Developer-grade API that works across web, mobile, and commerce, not just Shopify, so it fits custom or headless storefronts. AI ranking (on the Grow Plus tier) and personalization offer more control over relevance than a typical plug-and-play app, and a free tier lets teams prototype before committing.
Cons: Requires real engineering time to implement, tune, and maintain; it is not a plug-and-play Shopify app. Usage-based pricing scales with traffic, which makes costs harder to forecast than Boost's GMV tiers, and there's no native Shopify merchandising layer out of the box (verify current pricing and integration options).

Recommendation

For a standard Shopify store, Boost AI Search & Filter is the more practical pick: it's built for Shopify specifically, gets AI search and merchandising live fast, and its lower tiers ($29 to $599/mo) are reasonable for stores under $400K GMV. Once you're scaling past that, run the GMV-overage math before committing, since $2 per extra $1,000 GMV adds up. Algolia earns its place when you have engineering resources and need search infrastructure that spans a custom storefront, multiple platforms, or heavy personalization logic beyond what a Shopify app offers; just budget engineering time alongside its usage-based fees. Neither product addresses how your catalog gets discovered by AI assistants and search engines off-site. DTC brands that want both a strong on-site AI search experience and off-site AI-visibility (showing up in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other AI-driven answers) are the ones who should look at something like Buffy alongside their search stack, not instead of it.

Faq

How much does Boost AI Search & Filter cost compared to Algolia?

Boost AI Search & Filter has published GMV-based tiers: $29/mo entry, $159/mo for stores under $50K GMV, $599/mo under $400K GMV, and $1499/mo under $2.5M GMV, plus $2 overage per extra $1,000 GMV, billed monthly only. Algolia doesn't publish flat prices; it uses custom, usage-based pricing tied to traffic and query volume, with a free tier to start (verify current pricing directly with Algolia).

Is Boost AI Search & Filter worth it for a small Shopify store?

Generally yes, if you want AI-powered search and filtering live quickly without engineering work. At $29 to $159/mo for smaller stores, it's a reasonable cost for the merchandising and semantic search features included. The thing to watch is what happens to your bill as GMV climbs into the higher tiers, so check your growth trajectory against the pricing table before committing long-term.

Can Algolia be used with a Shopify store?

Yes, Algolia can be integrated with Shopify, but it takes more custom development than installing a native app like Boost. It's typically chosen by merchants with in-house engineering resources, especially those running headless or custom storefronts where they need more control than a standard Shopify search app provides.

Which is better for AI-powered product filtering, Boost or Algolia?

Boost is purpose-built for Shopify filtering and merchandising, so the filtering UI and logic come ready to use. Algolia is more powerful and flexible underneath, but you (or your dev team) have to build the filtering interface and logic yourselves using its API. If you want filtering out of the box, Boost is the faster path; if you need custom filtering logic, Algolia gives you more room to build it.

Do Boost AI Search & Filter or Algolia help my products show up in AI search tools like ChatGPT or Google AI Overviews?

No, both are on-site search tools focused on matching a shopper's query to products within your own catalog. Neither is designed to influence how your products are discovered or cited by AI assistants and AI-driven search results outside your store. If off-site AI visibility matters to your growth plan, that's a separate layer worth evaluating alongside whichever on-site search tool you choose.
