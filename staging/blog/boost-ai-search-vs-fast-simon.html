Boost AI Search & Filter vs Fast Simon: Shopify Search Compared (2026)

Boost AI Search & Filter and Fast Simon head-to-head - pricing, AI features, and which store each fits.

BBuffy Intel2026-07-13 · 6 min read

Overview

Boost AI Search & Filter vs Fast Simon: Shopify Search Compared (2026) comes down to one question: how is your store growing, and how do you want to pay for search? Both are built for Shopify merchants who have outgrown native search, but they charge for that upgrade in very different ways. Boost AI Search & Filter starts at $29/mo and scales with your store's GMV (gross merchandise value): $159/mo once you pass $50K in tracked GMV, $599/mo past $400K, and $1,499/mo past $2.5M, plus $2 for every extra $1,000 of GMV beyond your tier. It is billed monthly only, with no annual option. Fast Simon starts at $40/mo, with a free tier for very early stores, then moves through Starter ($40/mo), Essential ($100/mo), and Growth ($500/mo) plans priced on session volume, before topping out at a custom Enterprise tier. This page is for professional DTC founders and growth marketers, whether based in the US, India, or elsewhere, who need to choose one before a re-platform or a holiday traffic spike. Short version: Boost tends to win on out-of-the-box AI merchandising and filter depth for stores with steady, forecastable GMV growth. Fast Simon tends to win for high-traffic, lower-AOV stores where session count, not revenue, is the real cost driver. Neither is universally better; the right pick depends on which metric, GMV or sessions, your store scales faster on.

Best For

Pick Boost AI Search & Filter if your GMV growth is steady and you want a pricing tier you can forecast alongside revenue, with strong out-of-the-box AI search, filtering, and merchandising rules baked in.

Pick Fast Simon if your store gets heavy traffic relative to revenue (content-driven, lower AOV, or highly seasonal), since session-based pricing can work out cheaper than a GMV-based model when browsing volume outpaces sales.

Pick Fast Simon's free tier if you are a very early-stage store still validating product-market fit and cannot justify a $29 to $40/mo line item yet; upgrade once volume picks up.

Pick neither, and look at a broader AI commerce layer, if your bottleneck is not on-site search at all but getting found and recommended by AI shopping assistants like ChatGPT off-site, a different problem than either tool solves.

Pros And Cons

Boost AI Search & Filter pros: transparent, predictable GMV tiers up to $2.5M before needing a custom quote; strong AI semantic search paired with faceted filtering and merchandising rules in the same product; low $29/mo entry point for smaller stores.

Boost AI Search & Filter cons: pricing is monthly only with no annual discount mentioned; GMV-based billing means a strong sales month directly raises your software bill, and the $2-per-$1,000 overage can add up fast during spikes like Black Friday.

Fast Simon pros: a genuine free tier lowers the barrier for early-stage stores; session-based pricing decouples cost from revenue, which favors high-traffic or lower-AOV brands; feature set extends into upsell and cross-sell personalization, not just search.

Fast Simon cons: session-based limits mean a traffic spike, even without matching sales, can push you into a higher tier or overage; Enterprise pricing is custom, so larger stores lose the pricing transparency Boost offers at the top end.

Recommendation

There is no single winner here; it is a fit question. If your store's costs should track revenue, Boost AI Search & Filter's GMV-based tiers are easier to forecast and its AI search plus merchandising is genuinely strong for the price. If your traffic runs well ahead of your conversion rate or revenue, Fast Simon's session-based pricing (and its free tier for early-stage stores) is the more defensible choice. Run the math on your own GMV-to-session ratio before committing, since that ratio, not brand preference, is what decides the winner. One caveat worth naming: both tools solve on-site search, not off-site discoverability. If you are a DTC brand that also wants your products surfaced accurately when shoppers ask AI assistants like ChatGPT for recommendations, that is a separate job that Buffy is built for, pairing on-site AI search with off-site AI-visibility, and it is worth evaluating alongside whichever of these two you choose for search.

Faq

How much do Boost AI Search & Filter and Fast Simon cost?

Boost AI Search & Filter starts at $29/mo, then moves to $159/mo past $50K GMV, $599/mo past $400K GMV, and $1,499/mo past $2.5M GMV, with $2 in overage per extra $1,000 of GMV and monthly-only billing. Fast Simon starts at $40/mo (Starter), with a free tier for very early stores, then Essential at $100/mo and Growth at $500/mo, before a custom Enterprise plan for larger merchants.

Is Boost AI Search & Filter worth it for a growing Shopify store?

If you value predictable, GMV-linked pricing and want AI semantic search plus merchandising controls without stitching together separate apps, it is generally worth it, especially under the $400K GMV tier where the cost stays reasonable relative to revenue. Above that, run the math on the $2-per-$1,000 overage before a big growth push, since costs can climb faster than expected.

Does Fast Simon's free plan actually include AI search?

Fast Simon offers a free tier aimed at very early-stage stores, positioned as AI search, merchandising, and personalization for Shopify. Exact feature limits on the free tier are custom to store size, so confirm the specifics with Fast Simon directly before assuming full feature parity with paid plans (verify current pricing).

Which handles high traffic better, Boost or Fast Simon?

Fast Simon's pricing is session-based, which is built around traffic volume rather than sales, making it a more natural fit for high-traffic stores with lower conversion rates. Boost's GMV-based model does not penalize traffic directly, but if traffic spikes convert into sales spikes, that revenue growth still flows into Boost's tier pricing.

Do I need Boost or Fast Simon if I already use Shopify's native search?

Shopify's native search is basic keyword matching without real AI semantic understanding, synonym handling, or merchandising controls. If shoppers are searching and not finding relevant products, or you want to actively merchandise search results and filters, both Boost AI Search & Filter and Fast Simon are meaningful upgrades. Which one depends on whether your costs should scale with GMV or with sessions.
